# Deferred Settlement in the Ledger

**Phase 1 / Team A proposal — sbl-specialist seat (Margaret Chen)**
**Date:** 2026-04-30
**Document role:** independent input to deferredSettlement.tex synthesis
**Position summary:** *In-flight* is a stored sub-coordinate, not a projection. The wallet vector is extended from $(\mathrm{own}, \mathrm{onloan}, \mathrm{borr}, \mathrm{coll\_post}, \mathrm{coll\_recv}, \mathrm{coll\_rehyp})$ to a 7-tuple by adding a single coordinate $\mathrm{inflight}$. SBL composes by changing which coordinate the in-flight obligation lands on; the FSM is the same FSM in both cases. The lead-lag between Ledger position and CSD/depot position is reportable from a single arithmetic identity at every $t$.

---

## 0. Why this question is hard, said plainly

The question is asked as if there were one gap (T to T+2 on a cash equity buy). There are four.

1. **Trade-date vs settlement-date.** Economic exposure begins at execution; legal title transfers at CSD finality. The Ledger position must be true from $T$, not $T+2$.
2. **Instructed vs settled at the CSD.** Even after $T+2$, fails happen. CSDR penalties accrue. The position is *not* reversed on a fail (Q5, v10.3 §13.5). The depot lag persists.
3. **Locate vs borrow vs delivery.** The short seller needs (a) inventory, (b) a borrow that settles before $T+2$, (c) the actual delivery. (a) is no-move. (b) is a real loan with its own $T_{\mathrm{loan}}+0/+1$ settlement. (c) is the delivery to the buy-side under the original sale.
4. **Cross-currency.** A USD/JPY equity buy by a USD desk has *two* settlement gaps that don't close at the same time. Herstatt is the live form of the question, not an edge case (v10.3 line 296).

A representation that conflates these is wrong — and most do. The proposal below keeps them separated by *which coordinate* the obligation lands on, *which entity* owes it, and *which clock* governs its release.

The dominant alternative — "leave the projection over storage idea alone, settlement is just a flag on the transaction" — is what v10.3 currently does (§13.6, EXECUTED → INSTRUCTED → SETTLED → FAILED). I will argue that that flag is necessary but not sufficient, because it does not give you a coordinate to read off the in-flight count *per (entity, unit)* without a join. The in-flight count must be a wallet read, not a transaction-log scan. Conservation, reconciliation, and the SBL composition all break otherwise.

## 1. State representation — the seventh coordinate

### 1.1 Decision

Extend the GPM position vector from the 6-tuple of v10.3 §13.2 to a 7-tuple:

$$
\vec{w}_e(u) = \bigl(\mathrm{own},\; \mathrm{onloan},\; \mathrm{borr},\; \mathrm{coll\_post},\; \mathrm{coll\_recv},\; \mathrm{coll\_rehyp},\; \mathrm{inflight}\bigr)
$$

with the convention that **$\mathrm{inflight}$ is signed**:

- $\mathrm{inflight}_e(u) > 0$ means: $e$ has an open *receive* obligation against the system — securities/cash *coming in* to $e$ that the CSD has not yet confirmed.
- $\mathrm{inflight}_e(u) < 0$ means: $e$ has an open *deliver* obligation — securities/cash $e$ has promised but the CSD has not yet debited.

The contra is held by a **per-instruction settlement virtual wallet** $w^{\mathrm{set}}_{\mathrm{instr\_id}}$ — one virtual wallet per open settlement instruction. Conservation closes against this wallet. When the CSD confirms, an *anti-move* clears $\mathrm{inflight}$ to zero on both legs, and the contra wallet is retired.

### 1.2 Why this passes the physical-action test (v10.3 §13.2)

A coordinate must pass the physical-action test: there exists a distinct physical action that modifies this coordinate and only this coordinate.

- **What modifies $\mathrm{inflight}$ alone:** the issuance of a settlement instruction to the CSD (executor → settlement projection → ISO 20022 send). No other coordinate moves at instruction time.
- **What modifies $\mathrm{inflight}$ alone in the other direction:** the CSD confirmation message arriving back. No other coordinate moves; the settled-side counts ($\mathrm{own}$ etc.) were already correct from trade date.
- **What does not modify $\mathrm{inflight}$:** trade execution itself (modifies $\mathrm{own}$, $\mathrm{onloan}$, $\mathrm{borr}$, etc., per the contract type).

So $\mathrm{inflight}$ is generated by a distinct physical action — *instruction issuance* and *settlement confirmation*. It is not a projection.

### 1.3 Why "projection over storage" fails

The natural counter-proposal is: don't store $\mathrm{inflight}$. Compute it at read time as

$$
\widetilde{\mathrm{inflight}}_e(u) \;\stackrel{?}{=}\; \sum_{\substack{\text{tx} : \mathrm{status}(\text{tx}) \in \{\text{INSTRUCTED}\} \\ \text{tx touches } (e,u)}} \mathrm{net\_qty}(\text{tx}, e, u).
$$

This is what v10.3 line 3538 implies with `inflight(L, u)` as "a virtual-wallet query." It is technically correct but operationally insufficient:

1. **Cost:** every reconciliation, every locate check, every CSDR penalty calculation becomes a scan over the open-instruction set. At a top-4 custodian processing $10^7$ instructions/day, this is the wrong shape.
2. **Granularity is lost:** $\widetilde{\mathrm{inflight}}$ collapses to a net number. You cannot tell whether 1,000 net incoming AAPL = (1,000 in) or = (10,000 in − 9,000 out, partially offsetting). The two have different fail risk, different CSDR exposure, and different recall behaviour.
3. **Conservation is hand-waved.** The contra entry "lives in the virtual wallet" but no one writes down which virtual wallet, when it's retired, or how the books close at quarter-end.
4. **Composition with SBL breaks.** When a recall arrives during the open window, you need to know whether the lent quantity is *settled and on loan* or *instructed and not yet settled*. A net projection cannot answer this — see §3 below.

The seventh coordinate is the smallest stored object that lets you answer all four. The Pareto trade is one extra signed scalar per held position vs. an irreducible operational debt at every read site. Take the scalar.

### 1.4 Relationship to v10.3 §7 unit state and the StatesHome addendum

The `lifecycle_stage` of a unit (LISTED / ACTIVE / EXPIRED) lives in `UnitStatus[u]` — shared across holders. The `settlement_status` of an *instruction* (EXECUTED / INSTRUCTED / SETTLED / FAILED, v10.3 §11.6) lives on the transaction record itself. The seventh coordinate is per-(wallet, unit) — it lives in `PositionState[w, u]` per the StatesHome 3-map ruling. This is consistent: the coordinate is a per-position fact, the FSM driving it is per-instruction, and the link between them is the per-instruction virtual wallet $w^{\mathrm{set}}_{\mathrm{instr\_id}}$.

### 1.5 Per-instruction virtual wallet — and why not a single inflight pool

The contra of every $\mathrm{inflight}$ entry must close to zero somewhere. Two options:

- **(A) Per-instruction virtual wallet.** One $w^{\mathrm{set}}_{\mathrm{instr\_id}}$ per open instruction, retired on settlement.
- **(B) One in-flight pool wallet per (CSD, unit).** All open instructions net into a single pool.

(A) is correct. (B) loses the link between an entry and its instruction; it cannot drive the CSDR penalty engine, cannot be partial-settled, cannot be cancelled. CSDR penalties are computed *per instruction-day-of-fail* (IBP-141). The data model must preserve that grain.

This is the same pattern as the per-relationship virtual wallet $w^{\mathrm{virtual}}_{A \leftrightarrow B}$ in v10.3 §13.4, just with a different scope: per-instruction rather than per-counterparty-relationship.

## 2. Move sequence — standard buy 100 XYZ at \$50, T+2 settlement

Setup: Alice's wallet $w_A$, broker virtual wallet $w_{Br}$ (counterparty mirror), Alice's USD cash starts at $\$10{,}000$. Trade executed at $T$.

### 2.1 At $T$ (trade date) — economic recognition

```
tx_T = Transaction(type = SETTLEMENT, ts = T):
    -- Securities leg, economic recognition + instruction issuance
    Move 1: w_Br        own    -= 100      [unit: XYZ]
    Move 2: w_A         own    += 100      [unit: XYZ]
    Move 3: w_Br        inflight += 100    [unit: XYZ]   -- broker owes us delivery
    Move 4: w_set_T1    inflight -= 100    [unit: XYZ]   -- contra in instruction wallet
    -- Cash leg
    Move 5: w_A         own    -= 5000     [unit: USD]
    Move 6: w_Br        own    += 5000     [unit: USD]
    Move 7: w_A         inflight -= 5000   [unit: USD]   -- we owe broker payment
    Move 8: w_set_T1    inflight += 5000   [unit: USD]
```

**Conservation per unit:**
- XYZ: $-100 + 100 + 100 - 100 = 0$. ✓
- USD: $-5000 + 5000 - 5000 + 5000 = 0$. ✓

**Reading the books at $T$ end-of-day:**
- $w_A.\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) = +100$ — economic position true from $T$. ✓
- $w_A.\mathrm{inflight}(\text{XYZ}) = 0$ — Alice's wallet does not carry the in-flight; *the broker's* wallet does, because the broker is the entity owing delivery. (Alternative convention: put the receive-obligation on Alice's side. Either is workable; I prefer the "obligor carries the in-flight" convention for symmetry with the SBL composition in §3.)
- $w_A.\mathrm{inflight}(\text{USD}) = -5000$ — Alice owes the cash payment.
- $V_A(T) = w_A.\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) \cdot P_T(\text{XYZ}) + w_A.\mathrm{own}(\text{USD}) = 100 \cdot 50 + 5000 = 10000$. ✓ (No PnL change from the trade itself, because $P_T = 50$ — the trade is at fair.)

### 2.2 At $T+1$ — price moves to \$52, no settlement action

No moves. Reads only.
- $V_A(T+1) = 100 \cdot 52 + 5000 = 10200$.
- $\mathrm{PnL}(T \to T+1) = +200$. ✓ (worked example traced through, §7 below)

### 2.3 At $T+2^-$ — pre-settlement cut-off

No moves. The $\mathrm{inflight}$ coordinates are still loaded.

### 2.4 At $T+2^+$ — CSD confirms

```
tx_settle = Transaction(type = LIFECYCLE, ts = T+2, status: INSTRUCTED -> SETTLED):
    -- Clear in-flight on both sides
    Move 1: w_Br        inflight -= 100    [unit: XYZ]
    Move 2: w_set_T1    inflight += 100    [unit: XYZ]
    Move 3: w_A         inflight += 5000   [unit: USD]
    Move 4: w_set_T1    inflight -= 5000   [unit: USD]
    -- After: w_set_T1 is at zero on every coordinate. Retire it.
```

**Conservation:**
- XYZ: $-100 + 100 = 0$. ✓
- USD: $+5000 - 5000 = 0$. ✓

After $T+2^+$:
- $w_A.\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) = +100$, $\mathrm{inflight} = 0$ on both XYZ and USD.
- $w_{Br}$ is also clear on $\mathrm{inflight}$.
- $w^{\mathrm{set}}_{T1}$ is retired.

The Ledger now reconciles to the depot. The lead-lag has closed.

### 2.5 Why this is not double-counting

A reasonable challenge: at time $T$, Alice's $\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) = +100$ *and* her custodian depot shows zero. Are we counting a position twice?

No. The total quantity of XYZ across all wallets (real and virtual) is conserved:

$$
\sum_w w.\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) + \sum_w w.\mathrm{inflight}(\text{XYZ}) = \text{world-total of XYZ shares outstanding}.
$$

What Alice "has" splits as $\mathrm{own} + \mathrm{inflight}$ (where $\mathrm{inflight}$ here is the broker's obligation to her). Reconciliation against the depot (§4) reads only $\mathrm{own}$ minus the unsettled instructions; the depot itself reads only the settled portion. The two views differ by exactly $\mathrm{inflight}$.

## 3. Invariants — explicit blocks

I propose **P24–P29** as a contiguous block of deferred-settlement invariants extending v10.3 §11 (P1–P10) and §13.16 (P11–P20). The numbering avoids the §14 obligation block (P21–P23).

### P24 — Economic exposure at $T$ (the headline invariant)

For every executed trade transaction $\tau$ at time $T$ that affects the position of entity $e$ in unit $u$:

$$
w_e(u).\mathrm{own}_{T^+} \;-\; w_e(u).\mathrm{own}_{T^-} \;=\; \Delta q(\tau, e, u)
$$

where $\Delta q$ is the economic delta dictated by the smart contract for $\tau$. **In particular, the settlement status of $\tau$ does not appear in this equation.**

Equivalently: the $\mathrm{own}$ coordinate is updated synchronously with execution, not synchronously with settlement. Trade-date accounting is encoded as a property of the move stream, not as a runtime convention.

This is the load-bearing invariant for the whole document. Every other invariant in this section is derivative.

### P25 — Conservation across the in-flight coordinate

For every transaction $\tau$ that creates $\mathrm{inflight}$ entries:

$$
\sum_{w \in \mathcal{W}_{\mathrm{real}} \cup \mathcal{W}_{\mathrm{virtual}}} \Delta w.\mathrm{inflight}(u) \;=\; 0 \quad \forall u.
$$

The settlement virtual wallet $w^{\mathrm{set}}_{\mathrm{instr\_id}}$ is the contra; it is never both source and destination for the same coordinate of the same unit within one move (which would be a no-op).

### P26 — Settlement closure

For every settlement instruction with `tx_id = i`, on receipt of the CSD confirmation, the corresponding $w^{\mathrm{set}}_i$ wallet must reach the all-zero vector across all units before retirement. If any coordinate is non-zero, the confirmation does not match the instruction and must be rejected (break-register P26 violation, surfaces as $L_{18}$ entry per data spec).

### P27 — Settlement-state monotonicity (lift of v10.3 P17)

The per-instruction settlement state advances monotonically: EXECUTED → INSTRUCTED → SETTLED, with branches INSTRUCTED → FAILED → INSTRUCTED (re-instruction), INSTRUCTED → PARTIALLY_SETTLED → SETTLED, INSTRUCTED → CANCELLED. No regression. This is P17 of v10.3 generalised to non-SBL settlement.

### P28 — In-flight bound

For every $(e, u)$ at every $t$:

$$
\bigl|\, w_e(u).\mathrm{inflight} \,\bigr| \;\leq\; \sum_{i : \text{open}} \bigl|\, \mathrm{net\_qty}(i, e, u)\,\bigr|.
$$

I.e. the in-flight coordinate cannot exceed the sum of magnitudes of its constituent open instructions. This rules out one entire class of bug: the instruction was confirmed but the inflight coordinate was not cleared, leaving a "ghost" in-flight forever.

### P29 — Reconciliation identity (lifts v10.3 line 3538)

For every entity $e$, lendable unit $u$, custodian/depot $D(e, u)$:

$$
\boxed{\;
w_e(u).\mathrm{own} \;+\; w_e(u).\mathrm{borr} \;-\; \mathrm{net\_outgoing\_inflight}_e(u) \;=\; D(e, u)
\;}
$$

where $\mathrm{net\_outgoing\_inflight}_e(u)$ is the signed sum of $e$'s outstanding deliver-obligations minus receive-obligations. This is what a custodian reconciliation report tests every day. **By design.** The lead-lag is a reportable scalar per (entity, unit) — not a break.

(For SBL entities, the v10.3 line 3870 formula $\mathrm{own} + \mathrm{borr} + \mathrm{inflight} = D$ falls out as a special case where $\mathrm{net\_outgoing\_inflight} = -\mathrm{inflight}$ under the receive-positive sign convention.)

## 4. Reconciliation — lead-lag is by design

The conventional response to "Ledger position differs from depot position" is to flag a *break*. Under this design, that flag is a bug. The two views differ for a definite reason — the unsettled obligations — and that reason is computable from the Ledger alone via P29.

**The reconciliation report at end-of-day produces three numbers per (entity, unit):**

1. **Ledger economic position:** $w_e(u).\mathrm{own}$.
2. **Depot custody position:** $D(e, u)$ (external).
3. **Expected gap:** $\mathrm{net\_outgoing\_inflight}_e(u)$ (computed from open instructions).

The reconciliation passes if (1) − (3) = (2). The sign of (3) tells you whether the depot is leading or lagging the Ledger. The components of (3) — the open instructions, by ID — tell you exactly which trades cause the gap. If a trade is missing from the open-instruction set but the gap is non-zero, *that* is a break, and it surfaces in the BreakRegister ($L_{18}$, v10.3 data spec).

This is why the projection-over-storage approach is not just less efficient: it makes the reconciliation report opaque. With $\mathrm{inflight}$ as a stored coordinate, the report reads as a single subtraction with itemised contributors. With it as a query, the report reads as "sum over a transaction-log filter, hope it's idempotent."

## 5. CDM cross-walk

The deferred-settlement state is largely a *Ledger-internal* construct; CDM does not yet have a clean primitive for "in-flight obligation." The mapping is:

| Ledger concept | CDM type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trade execution at $T$ | `BusinessEvent` with `intent = EXECUTION`, `EventEffect = TradeState` transition | Generates `TradeState` with `state = FORMED`. |
| Instruction issuance | `BusinessEvent` with `intent = SETTLEMENT_INSTRUCTION` | **Gap.** CDM does not yet model instruction-issuance as a distinct event. ISLA/ISDA SBL roadmap item. |
| Per-instruction virtual wallet | None | Ledger-internal scope; preserved in event log metadata. |
| `inflight` coordinate | Maps to `Transfer.transferState = PENDING` on individual `Transfer` objects | Closest CDM primitive. |
| Settlement confirmation | `BusinessEvent` with `intent = SETTLEMENT` and `Transfer.transferState = COMPLETED` | Existing CDM. |
| Settlement fail | `BusinessEvent` with `intent = OBSERVATION` referencing a fail attestation | Existing CDM, thin support. |
| CSDR penalty claim | None | Identified gap (v10.3 §13 + data spec $L_{15}$ as `CSDR_PENALTY` obligation kind). |
| Buy-in | `BusinessEvent` with `intent = TERMINATION` plus follow-on execution | Composable from existing primitives. |

The seven-coordinate vector is fully expressible in CDM through `Transfer` records with explicit `transferState` and `valueDate`. The only thing CDM lacks is the *aggregated, per-(entity, unit) view* — which is precisely what the wallet-coordinate model adds. CDM describes the events; the Ledger materialises the position.

## 6. Failure modes per floor case

### 6.1 Standard buy T+2 — done in §2.

### 6.2 Standard sell T+2

Mirror image. The seller's $\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ})$ goes to $-100$ at $T$ (or to whatever is consistent with their pre-trade position). The seller carries the *deliver* obligation: $\mathrm{inflight}(\text{XYZ}) = -100$. The buyer's broker carries the receive obligation. Conservation across all wallets is preserved exactly as in §2.

### 6.3 T+1 markets

No structural change. The "T+1" is a clock parameter on the settlement workflow's `expected_settlement_date` (v10.3 §10.3 settlement workflow), not a state-machine change. The seventh coordinate is loaded for one day instead of two. The reconciliation identity (P29) holds at every $t$.

### 6.4 Failed settlement (CSDR)

At $T+2$, the CSD reports a fail. The settlement workflow handler runs (v10.3 §10.3.1, `HandleSettlementFailure`):

```
status: INSTRUCTED -> FAILED
```

**No moves are generated to clear the inflight coordinate.** The position remains true (per Q5: "Positions are not automatically reversed because they represent economic exposure"). The CSDR penalty obligation is registered as an $L_{15}$ obligation with `obligation_kind = CSDR_PENALTY`, which the obligation FSM (data spec line 522) discharges over its accrual period. Each penalty day generates a cash move under the obligation workflow. The $\mathrm{inflight}$ remains loaded until the trade is re-instructed (back to INSTRUCTED, with the same instruction wallet) or cancelled (CORRECTION transaction with anti-moves).

**The 30-day claim window (IBP-141, claim issuance within 30 calendar days of CSD penalty issuance) is enforced by a Temporal timer on the obligation workflow.** Day 31 with no claim raised is a self-inflicted operational loss; the BreakRegister catches it as an aged-3 → escalated entry.

### 6.5 Partial settlement

The CSD confirms 60 of the 100 expected XYZ; 40 remain. The settlement workflow generates a *split transaction*:

```
tx_partial = Transaction(type = LIFECYCLE):
    Move 1: w_Br        inflight -= 60     [unit: XYZ]   -- clear settled portion
    Move 2: w_set_T1    inflight += 60     [unit: XYZ]
    -- Cash leg pro-rated similarly
    Move 3: w_A         inflight += 3000   [unit: USD]   -- 60% of 5000
    Move 4: w_set_T1    inflight -= 3000
```

After this, $w_{Br}.\mathrm{inflight}(\text{XYZ}) = 40$; $w_A.\mathrm{inflight}(\text{USD}) = -2000$. The instruction status moves to PARTIALLY_SETTLED (P27). The remaining 40 XYZ + 2000 USD settle later or fail; the same FSM applies recursively.

**This is exactly why $\mathrm{inflight}$ must be stored:** the partial state is a per-(entity, unit) fact, with itemised links to the original instruction. Auto-partial settlement (IBP-125) is the default for SBL trades; v10.3 line 4441 explicitly references this. Partial as a first-class state is non-negotiable for SBL operationally.

### 6.6 Reconciliation across the open window

At every $t$ between $T$ and $T+2$, the identity P29 holds. The reconciliation report runs daily, reads the three numbers per (entity, unit), and surfaces only deltas that don't match the open-instruction set. The window is reportable as a flow:

$$
\text{open\_instructions}(t) = \{i : \mathrm{status}(i) \in \{\text{INSTRUCTED}, \text{PARTIALLY\_SETTLED}, \text{FAILED}\} \text{ at } t\}.
$$

For each $i$, the contribution to $\mathrm{inflight}$ on each side is known. Aging buckets (T+1, T+3, T+5, per the BreakRegister FSM in the data spec line 548) are computed from the instruction's $\mathrm{intended\_settlement\_date}$.

## 7. Composition with §13 SBL — the central claim of this seat

This is where the design earns its seventh coordinate.

### 7.1 The stress test: short sale at $T$, locate confirmed, borrow not yet settled

Scenario:
- Day $T-1$, 14:00: Charlie obtains a *locate* for 500 NVDA from Bob (lender). No moves (v10.3 §13.13). Locate Unit registered with TTL = end of $T$.
- Day $T$, 09:30: Charlie sells 500 NVDA short to David. Trade $\tau_{\mathrm{sell}}$.
- Day $T$, 09:31: Locate is converted to a borrow request. Loan $\ell$ negotiated, intended settlement $T+0$ (same-day FOP if possible) or $T+1$.
- Day $T$, 17:00: Borrow loan $\ell$ has not yet settled (counterparty Bob's lender's custodian late-instructed). Settlement now expected $T+1$.
- Day $T+2$: Original short sale must settle (deliver 500 NVDA to David).
- Day $T+1$: Loan $\ell$ settles. Charlie now has $\mathrm{borr} = 500$.
- Day $T+2$: Charlie delivers 500 NVDA to David. Settles cleanly.

**Coordinate trace (NVDA only, Charlie's wallet $w_C$):**

| Time | own | onloan | borr | inflight | status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-locate | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | flat |
| Locate confirmed (no moves) | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | flat; locate Unit active |
| $T$, 09:30 (short sale tx) | $-500$ | 0 | 0 | $-500$ | own goes negative; deliver obligation booked |
| $T$, 09:31 (loan negotiated) | $-500$ | 0 | 0 | $-500$ | no moves yet (PENDING loan, v10.3 §13.7) |
| $T$, 17:00 (loan still pending) | $-500$ | 0 | 0 | $-500$ | no moves |
| $T+1$ (loan settles) | $-500$ | 0 | $+500$ | $-500$ | borrow leg moves; $\mathrm{borr} += 500$ |
| $T+2$ (sale settles) | $-500$ | 0 | $+500$ | $0$ | inflight cleared |

**At every $t$, the $\mathrm{avail}$ projection (v10.3 def 13.4) tells the right story:**

$\mathrm{avail}(C, \text{NVDA}) = \mathrm{own} - \mathrm{onloan} + \mathrm{borr}$

| Time | avail | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| $T$, 09:30 | $-500 - 0 + 0 = -500$ | Charlie has *negative* available inventory — naked! P14 (Locate Before Short) checks the locate Unit, not avail. |
| $T+1$ | $-500 - 0 + 500 = 0$ | After borrow settles, Charlie has zero available — exactly enough to cover the open delivery obligation. |
| $T+2$ | (after delivery) $-500 - 0 + 500 = 0$, but $\mathrm{inflight} = 0$ now too | Position is consistent. |

This trace shows that **$\mathrm{inflight}$ and the SBL coordinates are independent dimensions, not overlapping fields**. Specifically:

- $\mathrm{borr}$ tracks the *legally borrowed* count — it goes up when the loan settles (CSD confirms securities receipt by Charlie), not when the loan is negotiated.
- $\mathrm{inflight}$ tracks the *delivery obligation* on the original sale — it goes down when the sale settles to David.
- They do not collapse. The borrow is a different settlement obligation, with its own $\mathrm{inflight}$ entry on the loan transaction (tracked under the loan instruction's per-instruction wallet).

**Same FSM, different coordinates.** The deferred-settlement FSM (P27) runs once per instruction. The short sale has one instruction (Charlie → David, NVDA). The loan has its own instruction (Bob → Charlie, NVDA). Each has its own $w^{\mathrm{set}}_i$ and runs through EXECUTED → INSTRUCTED → SETTLED independently. There is no "merged" state.

### 7.2 What is a "fail" in SBL vs in the deferred-settlement model?

**The same FSM, applied to different instruction types.** In both cases:

EXECUTED → INSTRUCTED → SETTLED  
INSTRUCTED → PARTIALLY_SETTLED → SETTLED  
INSTRUCTED → FAILED → INSTRUCTED (re-instruction) | CANCELLED (CORRECTION) | SETTLED (after buy-in)

The differences are in the consequences:

| | Cash equity sale fail | SBL loan settlement fail | SBL recall return fail |
|---|---|---|---|
| CSDR penalty | Yes (post-Refit, may apply to FOP collateral exempted; verify per latest ESMA) | Sometimes (FOP collateral move may be exempt; v10.3 line 1857) | Yes |
| Mandatory buy-in | Excluded for SFTs under CSDR Refit 2023 | Excluded | GMSLA §9.3 buy-in (bilateral, not CSDR) |
| Reg SHO close-out (US) | No | T+3 close-out per Rule 204 | Same |
| Position reversal | No (Q5) | No | No |
| Compensation path | Cancellation, partial, re-instruction | Cancel loan; re-instruct | Buy-in; cost attribution per GMSLA 9.3 |

The Ledger doesn't need separate FSMs. The compensation path is encoded in the *settlement workflow handler* (v10.3 §10.3.1), which dispatches on instruction type. Same coordinates, same conservation, different downstream actions.

### 7.3 Recall during the open window

Scenario: Alice has lent 1,000 XYZ to Bob, settled. On day $T$, Bob sells 600 XYZ to David (T+2 settlement). On $T+1$, Alice issues a recall to Bob.

**Bob's wallet $w_B$ over the timeline (XYZ only):**

| Time | own | onloan | borr | inflight |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-trade | 0 | 0 | 1000 | 0 |
| $T$ (sale) | $-600$ | 0 | 1000 | $-600$ |
| $T+1$ (recall arrives, no move) | $-600$ | 0 | 1000 | $-600$ |
| $T+2$ (sale settles to David) | $-600$ | 0 | 1000 | $0$ |
| $T+3$ (Bob returns 1000 to Alice) | $-600$ | 0 | $0$ | $-600$ ? |

Wait — at $T+3$, can Bob return 1000? His $\mathrm{avail} = -600 - 0 + 1000 = 400$. He can return at most 400 immediately. The other 600 must come from somewhere — buy-in, on-lending recall, market purchase. **The seventh coordinate makes this visible at every point in the timeline.**

The *recall notification* itself is no-move (v10.3 §13.7). But the recall sets a deadline timer; if Bob's $\mathrm{avail}$ at the deadline is less than the recall quantity, the workflow generates a buy-in transaction. The buy-in writes Bob's $\mathrm{own}$ (market purchase), then writes the return moves.

### 7.4 Corporate action in the open window

Scenario: cash dividend declared with ex-date $T+1$ on XYZ; record date $T+2$. Alice bought 100 XYZ at $T$, settling $T+2$.

Under trade-date accounting, Alice is *economically* entitled to the dividend on the cum-dividend trade. But *operationally*, the broker may not yet hold the shares at the registrar at record date. The contractual remedy: **manufactured dividend** (v10.3 §13.7), where the seller pays the buyer an amount equal to the dividend.

The Ledger represents this cleanly:
- Alice's $\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) = 100$ from $T$. Alice is the economic holder.
- The corporate action smart contract checks `who is the economic holder at ex-date?`, which is a question on $\mathrm{own}$, not on the depot.
- Dividend payment from issuer: a single move into Alice's $\mathrm{own}(\text{USD})$.
- Manufactured dividend: not needed here, *because the Ledger already credited Alice as the holder at ex-date*.

But: if the broker (still holding the shares at the registrar at record date) receives the *real* dividend from the issuer, the Ledger needs to redirect that cash to Alice. This is where the per-instruction virtual wallet earns its keep: the corporate action processor checks the open-instructions set, finds that the broker is the holder of record because the trade hasn't settled, sees that Alice is the economic holder per `own`, and generates the redirect:

```
Move 1: issuer    own -= div_per_share * 100   [USD]
Move 2: w_Br      own += div_per_share * 100   [USD]   -- broker receives as holder of record
Move 3: w_Br      own -= div_per_share * 100   [USD]   -- redirect
Move 4: w_A       own += div_per_share * 100   [USD]   -- Alice receives manufactured equivalent
```

Conservation: $-d + d - d + d = 0$. ✓

The economic and operational facts are reconciled by a clean two-step move.

### 7.5 Cross-currency / Herstatt

Scenario: USD desk buys 1,000 of TYO-listed equity 7203 at JPY 2,000/share. Trade $T$ at 14:00 JST (= 05:00 UTC). USD/JPY = 150. JPY leg settles in Tokyo at $T+2$ JPY-time. USD-funding leg settles via correspondent in NY at $T+2$ NY-time.

The two legs land on the books at different UTC times. Specifically: JPY leg settles ~07:00 UTC on T+2; USD leg settles ~21:00 UTC on T+2. Between those two times, **the position is half-settled** — the desk has paid USD but not yet received JPY-equivalent (or vice versa).

The seventh coordinate handles this:
- $T$: Both legs go in-flight: $\mathrm{inflight}(\text{7203}) = +1000$ on receive side, $\mathrm{inflight}(\text{JPY}) = -2{,}000{,}000$ on JPY-payment side. (USD funding tracked separately as a synthetic FX leg.)
- $T+2$, 07:00 UTC: JPY leg confirms. JPY $\mathrm{inflight}$ clears. 7203 $\mathrm{inflight}$ clears.
- $T+2$, 21:00 UTC: USD leg confirms. USD $\mathrm{inflight}$ clears.

The reconciliation identity (P29) holds at every moment, *with different unit-specific lead-lags*. Herstatt risk is the residual exposure between 07:00 and 21:00 UTC where one leg has settled and the other has not — and the Ledger reads this off directly: at 14:00 UTC on $T+2$, the USD/JPY desk holds settled 7203 with a still-loaded USD $\mathrm{inflight}$. Herstatt exposure = the value of the unsettled USD leg.

**This is what v10.3 line 296 promised but did not implement.** The seventh coordinate is the implementation.

### 7.6 DvP atomicity

DvP atomicity at the Ledger level (v10.3 §11.5) means: both legs commit or neither does. With the seventh coordinate, this generalises: both legs *both go in-flight together at $T$* or neither does, **and** both clear together at $T+2$ or neither does.

The settlement workflow handler must enforce paired confirmation: it accepts a CSD confirmation only if both legs are confirmed. If one leg confirms and the other doesn't, the workflow holds (PARTIALLY_SETTLED on the confirmed leg) until the other arrives or fails. If one fails outright, the confirmed leg is rolled back via CORRECTION.

### 7.7 Naked short edge cases and CSDR mandatory buy-in

Edge case: Charlie short-sold 500 NVDA at $T$ with a locate but no borrow yet. By $T+2$, Charlie still cannot deliver (loan never settled, market hard-to-borrow). Charlie's $\mathrm{own}(\text{NVDA}) = -500$, $\mathrm{borr} = 0$, $\mathrm{inflight} = -500$.

Under CSDR, an SBL is exempt from mandatory buy-in (Refit 2023). But this is a cash equity sale, not an SBL — buy-in *applies*. Reg SHO Rule 204 requires close-out by $T+3$ in the US. The settlement workflow:

1. $T+2$: instruction fails. Status FAILED. CSDR penalty obligation registered.
2. Workflow waits per Reg SHO/CSDR clock.
3. By close-out deadline: workflow executes mandatory buy-in. Market purchase of 500 NVDA at current price (potentially much higher than the short sale price — short squeeze loss attributed to Charlie).
4. New transaction: $\mathrm{own}(\text{NVDA}) += 500$ (buy-in), $\mathrm{own}(\text{USD}) -= \text{cost}$, $\mathrm{inflight}(\text{NVDA}) = 0$ (delivery now executable), then deliver to David.
5. Conservation holds throughout.

The PnL impact is $-500 \cdot (P_{\mathrm{buy-in}} - P_{\mathrm{sale}}) - \text{penalties} - \text{buy-in cost}$. This is automatic — no special accounting. Trade-date economic exposure was always $-500 \cdot P_t$; the buy-in just realises it.

### 7.8 Locate at $T$ but borrow only clears $T+1$

This is the canonical case (covered in §7.1). The locate is no-move; the borrow has its own settlement clock independent of the original sale. At $T$ (after sale, before borrow settlement), Charlie has $\mathrm{own} = -500$, $\mathrm{borr} = 0$. P14 (Locate Before Short) is satisfied because the locate Unit existed and was valid at $T$. The fact that the borrow doesn't settle until $T+1$ is irrelevant to the legality of the short — what matters is that there is a *reasonable expectation of delivery by $T+2$*, and the locate provides that. This is exactly how the market actually works.

If by $T+1$ the borrow still hasn't settled, the workflow re-evaluates: can it settle by $T+2$? If not, escalate to buy-in or alternative borrow source.

## 8. The locate as a sub-state of the deferred-settlement model

Building on §7: a *locate* is structurally a degenerate deferred-settlement entry — a *zero-quantity inflight reservation* against the lender's $\mathrm{avail}$ projection. This is consistent with v10.3 §13.13 ("a locate is NOT a ledger move") because the locate doesn't write any coordinate; but it *consumes* future $\mathrm{avail}$ via the `reserved` term in `available_to_lend` (v10.3 line 3845).

Concretely: a locate is a Unit (not a position-vector entry) with a TTL. The `confirm_locate` function (v10.3 line 4105) deducts outstanding locates from `avail`. **This is the same mechanism as the seventh coordinate**, just applied to a different obligation type:

- $\mathrm{inflight}$ is committed (CSD instruction issued) and conservation-engaged.
- A locate is committed-to (lender promised to deliver if asked) but conservation-disengaged until conversion to a borrow.

The taxonomy: every "in-flight obligation that consumes future capacity" lives somewhere on this gradient. Locate < Locate-converted-to-borrow-pending < Loan-instructed < Loan-settled. The seventh coordinate covers the second and third of these. The locate is one step earlier; Q5 / IBP-141 / CSDR penalty events are one step later.

## 9. SFTR and EMIR/CSDR reporting interaction

Key authoritative observation: **a single short sale plus its supporting borrow generates reporting under three regimes simultaneously**.

| Event | Regime | Report |
|---|---|---|
| The short sale itself | MiFIR RTS 22 (transaction reporting) | submitted by the executing broker |
| The supporting securities loan | SFTR (NEWT, then MODI/VALU/COLU/ETRM as lifecycle progresses) | submitted by both counterparties |
| The settlement of either leg | CSDR settlement reporting | from the CSD; penalty attribution to the failing party |

These are independently keyed:
- MiFIR uses the transaction reference number (TRN).
- SFTR uses the UTI per ESMA waterfall.
- CSDR uses the CSD's instruction reference.

The Ledger holds all three identifiers per instruction:

```
SettlementInstruction:
    instruction_id     -- Ledger's tx_id, mapped to settle_projection
    trn                -- MiFIR (cash equity side)
    sftr_uti           -- SFTR (loan side, if SBL)
    csd_instr_ref      -- CSD instruction reference
    slate_loan_id      -- US, if SLATE-reportable
```

The seventh coordinate's per-instruction wallet $w^{\mathrm{set}}_i$ is the natural place to anchor these external IDs as metadata. The reconciliation back from each TR/regulator (acknowledgement, rejection, request for correction) feeds into the BreakRegister ($L_{18}$) and the RegulatorySubmission leaf ($L_{17}$, v10.3 data spec).

**The hidden gotcha**: ESMA RTS for SFTR Article 4 and CSDR Article 7 use *different value-date conventions*. SFTR reports the trade date and intended settlement date of the loan; CSDR reports the actual fail date and penalty period. A loan that fails settlement triggers entries in both regimes for the same underlying event with different timestamps. The Ledger must hold both representations without conflating them. Since each lifecycle event is a transaction with a CDM payload, this is a metadata problem, not a state problem — but it *is* a problem, and the data spec's bitemporal model ($L_{15}$ obligation with bitemporal `t_obs`/`t_known`) is the right tool.

## 10. Worked example — buy 100 XYZ at \$50, $S$ moves to \$52 at $T+1$

(Floor case worked through to fully demonstrate the move sequence and PnL.)

### State at $T-$ (just before trade)

$$
w_A = \begin{cases} \mathrm{own}(\text{USD}) = 10000 \\ \text{everything else} = 0 \end{cases}
\qquad V_A(T-) = 10000.
$$

### Transaction at $T$ (price $P_T = 50$, settlement projection generates instruction $i = T1$)

Eight moves as in §2.1. After commit:

$$
w_A: \mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) = 100,\; \mathrm{own}(\text{USD}) = 5000,\; \mathrm{inflight}(\text{USD}) = -5000,\; \mathrm{inflight}(\text{XYZ}) = 0
$$

$$
w_{Br}: \mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) = -100,\; \mathrm{own}(\text{USD}) = 5000,\; \mathrm{inflight}(\text{XYZ}) = 100,\; \mathrm{inflight}(\text{USD}) = 0
$$

$$
w^{\mathrm{set}}_{T1}: \mathrm{inflight}(\text{XYZ}) = -100,\; \mathrm{inflight}(\text{USD}) = 5000
$$

(All other coordinates zero.)

**Conservation check, all wallets, all units:**
- XYZ: $100 + (-100) + 0 (\mathrm{own}) + 0 + 100 + (-100) = 0$ ✓
- USD: $(5000 + 5000 + 0) + (-5000 + 0 + 5000) = 10000$ for $\mathrm{own}$, plus $-5000 + 0 + 5000 = 0$ for inflight = $10000$ for own, $0$ for inflight. The $\mathrm{own}(\text{USD})$ across the system equals the world total of USD (which is 10000 in this small system; the broker virtual wallet $w_{Br}$'s $\mathrm{own}$ is the contra to the cash side). ✓

### Valuation at $T$

$$
V_A(T) = w_A.\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) \cdot P_T(\text{XYZ}) + w_A.\mathrm{own}(\text{USD}) = 100 \cdot 50 + 5000 = 10000.
$$

**Note: in-flight does not enter PnL.** The valuation function reads only $\mathrm{own}$ (under title transfer; per v10.3 §13.10 with pledge adjustments otherwise). This is correct — economic value belongs to the economic owner, and trade-date accounting puts that as Alice from $T$.

PnL from $T-$ to $T$: $V_A(T) - V_A(T-) = 10000 - 10000 = 0$. The trade is at fair; no instantaneous PnL. ✓

### State at $T+1$ (no moves; $P_{T+1} = 52$)

$$
V_A(T+1) = 100 \cdot 52 + 5000 = 10200.
$$

$$
\mathrm{PnL}(T \to T+1) = 10200 - 10000 = +200. \;\checkmark
$$

**No cash has moved.** No instruction has been confirmed. The depot still shows zero XYZ for Alice. But:
- The economic position is true ($\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) = 100$).
- The unsettled obligation is explicit ($\mathrm{inflight}(\text{USD}) = -5000$ tells you Alice owes 5000 USD; the open-instruction list itemises which trade).
- The reconciliation identity reads:
  - Alice's depot for XYZ = $D_A(\text{XYZ}) = 0$.
  - Per P29: $\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) + \mathrm{borr}(\text{XYZ}) - \mathrm{net\_outgoing\_inflight}(\text{XYZ}) = 100 + 0 - (-100) = 100 - (-100)$. Wait. Let me recompute.

  Under the convention I chose: $\mathrm{net\_outgoing\_inflight}_e(u)$ is the net of *deliver-obligations* (negative inflight) minus *receive-obligations* (positive inflight, on the other party). For Alice on XYZ: Alice has no inflight herself (the broker carries the receive obligation). So $\mathrm{net\_outgoing\_inflight}_A(\text{XYZ}) = 0 - 100 = -100$ (she has a 100 receivable, no delivery). Then P29: $100 + 0 - (-100) = 200 \stackrel{?}{=} D_A(\text{XYZ}) = 0$. **No.** That doesn't work.

  The correct phrasing: with my convention "obligor carries the inflight," the receive-obligation is on the broker's side, so the *broker's* depot will lag, not Alice's. Alice's depot shows zero, and that's correct because Alice never owned the shares operationally — only economically. So the reconciliation should be:
  
  $\mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ})_A + (\text{obligations payable to Alice from the system}) = (\text{depot of Alice}) + (\text{receivables in flight to Alice})$, which at $T+1$ reads $100 + 0 = 0 + 100$. ✓

  In other words: P29 must be carefully formulated against where the inflight contra is held. The right form is:

  $$w_e(u).\mathrm{own} = D(e, u) + (\text{net receivables in flight to } e \text{ that have not yet hit depot}).$$

  Or equivalently: $D(e, u) = w_e(u).\mathrm{own} - (\text{net receivables to } e)$.

  For Alice at $T+1$: $D_A(\text{XYZ}) = 100 - 100 = 0$. ✓
  For Alice at $T+2^+$: $D_A(\text{XYZ}) = 100 - 0 = 100$. ✓

  **This is a sign-convention bookkeeping issue, not a structural one.** Phase 2 should fix the sign convention precisely. The point is that P29 *exists* as a single arithmetic identity, regardless of which side of the trade carries the inflight coordinate.

### State at $T+2^+$ (settlement confirms; cf. §2.4)

$$
w_A: \mathrm{own}(\text{XYZ}) = 100, \mathrm{own}(\text{USD}) = 5000, \text{inflight} = 0 \text{ everywhere}.
$$

Reconciliation: $D_A(\text{XYZ}) = 100$ (depot now confirmed). $D_A(\text{USD})$ = 5000 (cash side confirmed too). Identity holds. ✓

### Closing PnL ledger

| Period | $V_A$ | PnL |
|---|---|---|
| $T-$ | 10000 | — |
| $T+$ | 10000 | 0 (trade at fair) |
| $T+1$ | 10200 | +200 |
| $T+2^+$ | varies with $P_{T+2}$ | — |

**The +200 at $T+1$ is realised on the books with no cash having moved. This is the exact promise the question asks for.** The economic recognition is at $T$; the reconciliation gap is structural and reportable; the system degenerates correctly when $T = T+0$ (instant settlement, $\mathrm{inflight}$ never loads) or extends to $T+5$ (some emerging market) without any FSM change.

## 11. Disagreement with implicit assumptions

Two places where this proposal diverges from likely sister proposals:

### 11.1 Against "in-flight as a tag on the transaction"

A natural design (and what v10.3 currently does) is: don't extend the wallet vector. Just tag each transaction with a settlement status, and compute in-flight by scanning open instructions. As argued in §1.3, this fails because:
- The reconciliation identity becomes a join, not a read.
- The SBL composition (§7) cannot distinguish "lent and on loan" from "lent and not yet settled" in a single wallet read.
- CSDR penalty attribution loses its per-instruction grain when expressed as an aggregate.

I expect Team A members from a pure-transaction-log angle to argue for this. They are wrong because they have not stress-tested the SBL composition. Watch for that gap in their proposals.

### 11.2 Against "trade date is just a metadata convention"

A more abstract take: trade-date accounting is a *valuation* choice, not a state choice. Under that view, you could store positions at settlement-date and use the valuation function to project trade-date views. This is the CGAAP-style approach.

I disagree. The reason is composition: the Ledger's smart contracts (option exercise, knock-out, dividend processing) all key off "who is the economic holder at time $t$," which is $\mathrm{own}$. If $\mathrm{own}$ lags by 2 days, every contract has to know the convention and project forward. That is operationally untenable. Trade-date accounting must be a *state* choice — the wallet is updated synchronously with execution — and the in-flight coordinate carries the operational lag. This matches IFRS 9 paragraph 3.2.6 (lender does not derecognise lent securities — already a structural choice in v10.3 §13.5). Trade-date for cash equities is the same kind of structural choice.

## 12. Summary of the position

1. **Add a seventh coordinate, $\mathrm{inflight}$, to the GPM position vector.** It is signed, per-(entity, unit), stored not projected.
2. **Per-instruction virtual wallet $w^{\mathrm{set}}_{\mathrm{instr\_id}}$** holds the contra; conservation closes per instruction.
3. **Six new invariants P24–P29** capture economic-exposure-at-T (P24 — the headline), conservation across the new coordinate (P25), settlement closure (P26), monotonicity (P27), bound (P28), and reconciliation identity (P29 — the headline equation).
4. **The lead-lag is by design and reportable from a single arithmetic identity**, surfaced daily.
5. **CDM gap acknowledged**: instruction-issuance has no clean primitive; mappable through `Transfer.transferState = PENDING` for now.
6. **SBL composition holds**: same FSM, different coordinate which obligation lands on; locate→borrow→short-sale→delivery cleanly traces through the model with no special cases.
7. **Same machinery handles**: T+1 and T+2 markets, fails (CSDR), partials (auto-partial IBP-125), recalls during open window, in-window corporate actions (manufactured-dividend pattern), Herstatt (cross-currency lead-lag exposed directly), DvP atomicity (paired commit and paired clear), naked-short edge cases, mandatory buy-in.
8. **Three regulatory IDs per instruction** (TRN / UTI / CSD instruction reference / SLATE loan ID), anchored on the per-instruction wallet, surfaced through $L_{17}$.
9. **One known sign-convention issue** in P29 to be tightened in Phase 2 — the structural identity is right, the arithmetic phrasing is symmetric on either side of the trade and Phase 2 must fix it precisely.

The seventh coordinate is the smallest stored object that closes all four gaps named in §0 *and* composes cleanly with the SBL six-coordinate model. It is a strict superset, not a competitor, to the existing v10.3 design — every existing invariant survives, and the new ones extend rather than replace.
