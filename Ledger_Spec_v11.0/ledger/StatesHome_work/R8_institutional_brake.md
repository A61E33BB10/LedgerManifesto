# R8 — Institutional Brake: Final Adversarial Gate

*Viktor Haldorsson, Senior Advisor – Institutional Continuity & Risk Assessment. Sole function: identify reasons not to proceed.*

---

A proposal materially indistinguishable from this one has crossed my desk perhaps a dozen times in 27 years: a carefully formalised re-keying of the state schema, accompanied by a numbered list of invariants, a Pareto table, and an assurance that this time the abstraction is the minimum basis of the problem. In each prior case the outcome was functionally identical to the starting state, preceded by eighteen months of migration overhead, two production incidents traceable to incomplete retrofit, one regulator letter requesting clarification on reporting surface, and eventual quiet reversion to the pragmatic shape that had preceded the reform. The accumulated organisational scar tissue is unambiguous. I catalogue below the fatal flaws that the R1–R7 chain has not, in my considered view, adequately discharged.

## Fatal flaws

**F1 — Migration scope is understated by an order of magnitude.** The proposal replaces v10.3 line 1034 and splits `unit_state` — stated as two edits. In every comparable retrofit I have observed, the true surface is every downstream reader of `unit_state`, every reconciliation job keyed on the old shape, every audit extract, every reporting adapter, every test fixture, every generator, and every Rosetta alignment document. v10.3 is ~1,800 lines of formal specification with cross-references; the StatesHome_work chain itself acknowledges collapse of `WalletState` into `PositionState`, which means every W-keyed reference in the existing corpus must be re-examined, not merely rewritten. Parallel-running two schemas during cutover will exceed the proposed timeline and budget by the customary 300% that these programmes always overrun by, and rollback mid-migration is not the two-way door the authors imply (see F8).

**F2 — Ownership of the fungibility predicate is unassigned and ungovernable.** C8 mandates that each `ProductType` declares `is_fungibility_preserving`. The proposal does not specify who owns it per product, what the approval chain is, what happens when Legal disagrees with Product on whether a coupon step-up is Preserving, or how the predicate is versioned when the predicate itself must change. This is the single most consequential decision in the entire schema — "Preserving" means existing `PositionState[w,u]` rows survive untouched across an economic change — and R6_formalis §5 dispatches it with three examples and the word "decidable." In prior comparable programmes, predicates of this class either calcified into unmaintained boilerplate or became the site of quarterly escalations between front-office and middle-office. No RACI, no fallback, no tie-break authority. This is a governance void dressed as a type signature.

**F3 — Performance risk at real scale is unanalysed.** `PositionState` keyed by `(WalletId, UnitId)` with a monotone (never-GC) carrier: at 10M wallets × 1M units the worst-case key space is 10^13. Even at sparsity, every wallet-onboarded unit ever held leaves a permanent tombstone. The R7 chain scores "reconciliation = 9" without a single measurement of Option-accessor cost, cache behaviour on the (w,u) hash, or storage growth over a ten-year retention horizon. Monotonicity as a correctness property is attractive; monotonicity as an operational commitment is an unbounded liability. C1 has been reasoned about; it has not been benchmarked.

**F4 — Twelve conditions is a multi-quarter test programme, not a release.** C1–C12 each require property generators, shrinkers, metamorphic properties, mutation coverage, and — per R7_correctness §6 — differential cashflow tests across every fungibility-preserving amendment on every holder. R7_testability confidently scores TEST=9; R7_correctness lists five "blockers before merge" that are themselves multi-sprint items (handler/product-type predicate at the type level, closed cross-product generator, Claessen-Hughes shrinker, differential amendment test, metamorphic conservation). Every comparable formalisation I have seen delivered 40–60% of the promised property coverage in the first release, with the remainder perpetually deferred. The proposal conflates the proof obligations that appear in R6_formalis with the engineering work required to discharge them in CI.

**F5 — Mandate-as-unit creates a regulatory reporting surface that has not been mapped.** If `u_MA` is a registered unit with `ProductTerms[u_MA]` and conservation `Σ_w w(u_MA)=0`, then by the ledger's own definitions a mandate issuance is a trade-shaped event. SFTR, EMIR, MiFIR reporting logic downstream of the ledger keys off unit registrations and position changes. The proposal nowhere addresses whether mandate issuances and amendments enter the regulatory reporting surface, whether they require LEI pair reporting, UTI allocation, or CCP identification, or whether the manager-as-issuer pattern is compatible with existing counterparty taxonomies. R6_formalis §3 resolves the type-theoretic objection; it does not resolve the reporting obligation. This is precisely the second- and third-order regulatory obligation that has not been mapped.

**F6 — CDM alignment claim is load-bearing and unverified.** CDM carries `TradeState` per-`Trade`; the proposal asserts `PositionState[w,u]` is the equivalent. `Trade` and `(w,u)` are not isomorphic: a single trade can generate multiple `(w,u)` updates (novation, give-up, allocation) and a single `(w,u)` row aggregates many trades. The Rosetta NS1–7 alignment referenced in the persistent project memory has not been re-validated against the 3-map schema. Shipping on an unverified Rosetta mapping is the kind of latent defect the external party exits long before it surfaces.

**F7 — Onboarding cost is real.** Three abstract maps, Option+Monotone orthogonality, overlay-keyed mandate state, fungibility-predicate dispatch, and twelve numbered conditions is not a two-hour learning curve regardless of what R7_simplicity scores it. New engineers will build intuition against `PositionState` and violate C4 capability-scoped reads in month two. The proposal has no training artefact, no decision tree, no runbook.

**F8 — Forward migration is not reversible.** Once `WalletState` is collapsed into `PositionState[w, u_MA]`, the information that a given fact was "wallet-scoped" versus "mandate-scoped" is lost; the overlay-key `u_MA` becomes the sole locus. Rolling back to v10.3's two-level model requires reconstructing which overlay rows correspond to which former `WalletState` fields — a reconstruction that depends on the same handler discipline the rollback is presumably rolling back because it failed. This is a one-way door presented as symmetric.

## Philosophical termination

The R1–R7 chain is an impressive specimen of convergent formal reasoning, and I do not dispute its internal coherence. What it lacks — what these exercises invariably lack — is an accounting of the gap between a schema that is Pareto-optimal on paper and a production ledger that has absorbed a decade of exception handlers, reconciliation carve-outs, and regulator-driven patches. Every transformation rediscovers the value of the status quo eventually; the question is only whether the rediscovery occurs before or after the incident. Boldness is a luxury mature institutions cannot afford, and a 3-map schema with 12 conditions and a product-declared predicate is, whatever else it may be, bold.

## Final position

**Fatal flaws identified: 8 (F1–F8).**

**Gate: FAIL.** On the basis of institutional history and the observable realities of production, this proposal should not proceed in its current form. Continuity has preserved this ledger far longer than re-keying ever could.

— Viktor Haldorsson, R8, sealed.
