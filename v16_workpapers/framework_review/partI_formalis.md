## CRITICISMS (today)
C1 [HIGH — gravest]. Two documents answer the same Constitution-categorical
   question ("may a ledger-COMPUTABLE number be recorded?") in opposite ways.
   VM parks it honestly (PARK-1 vs C-4.11 "computed when needed and never stored").
   MD-16 records the gate decision's FUNCTIONALS and PERCENTILES — each a
   deterministic map from state $m^\ast$ to a number, i.e. a projection by C-4.11's
   own definition — and declares "no collision with C-4.11 ... captures an
   event-outcome, not a projection." Recording the pass/fail BIT is provenance and
   fine; recording the computed numeric fields IS materialising a projection, the
   very act PARK-1 parks. The "event-outcome not a projection" classification is
   asserted, not proved, and reads as the narrow-then-relabel move CLAUDE.md §1
   most forbids. If the owner keeps C-4.11 literal, MD-16's recorded fields become
   non-conforming — an undisclosed cross-document blast radius the parking index
   hides. FIX: ground MD-16's recording on MD-11/C-14.15 (a simulated path must
   capture its outputs as re-entered observations — this needs NO C-4.11 amendment),
   OR fold the numeric fields under PARK-1. Drop the "not a projection" step.
C2 [HIGH]. MD-16 Gate 1 discharges VM PE-1(A5) INCORRECTLY. Gate 1 guarantees
   membership of ONE endpoint state $m^\ast\in\Theta_{AF}$ at a pinned cut. A5 needs
   $\Sigma(\cdot;S)\in\mathcal A$ for $S$ in a NEIGHBOURHOOD of the valuation spot,
   and its "holds automatically" route needs INTERIORITY plus continuous $\mathcal D$.
   Three gaps: (i) endpoint vs neighbourhood — PE-2/PE-3/PE-5 need $\partial_S\Sigma$,
   $\partial_{SS}\Sigma$, properties of a curve, not a point; (ii) membership vs
   interiority — a boundary $m^\ast$ passes Gate 1 yet breaks openness; (iii) the
   base/ORIGIN surface is admissible only by MD-9 DETECTION, and $m^\ast$ is the
   DESTINATION, so the neighbourhood A5 needs (around the origin) is gated at neither
   end. "What the child assumes, this parent guarantees" overstates. Mitigant: VM is
   internally honest — PE-3/PE-5 treat $\sprod^2$ poles and sign-flips as recorded
   diagnostics, so nothing breaks silently; but the DISCHARGE CLAIM is false as
   written. FIX per A3.
C3 [MEDIUM-HIGH]. MD-4's title "Every observation bears three times" contradicts
   C-2.7 (v1.41): an event the Monitor EMITS rather than observes bears NO monitor
   time. A re-entered observation (VM-1: every model-priced leg) is emitted through
   the door, not observed at the boundary — so it bears no monitor time. MD-4's
   universal fails for exactly the observations VM is built on. FIX: MD-4 -> "bears
   up to three times; an emitted/re-entered observation bears no monitor time."
C4 [MEDIUM]. Object-identity drift. MD-16 asserts "VM-5 $\equiv\mathcal D$"; VM
   PE-1(A5) says $\mathcal D$ is "the surface-dynamics CONTENT OF the re-marking
   convention VM-5." Containment != identity: VM-5 spans all channels (spot / vol /
   correlation held-invariant); $\mathcal D$ is the surface-move component only.
   MD-16's equivalence silently drops the correlation channel. FIX: MD-16 ->
   "$\mathcal D$ is the surface-move component of VM-5."
C5 [MEDIUM]. VM-11 promises "a valuation at every step"; MD-16 says a stressed
   state that fails a gate "does not exist." VM-11 inherits "gap-free through the
   horizon" from MD-4/MD-11, but MD-4's gap-free is READ-BACK (served / identity
   history); STRESSED histories are gate-conditional and can be UNDECIDABLE
