# v1.41 Register Review — the time envelope (C-2.7, C-12.4/12.5/12.6)

REVIEW ONLY. Feeds a separate drafting step. Scope: the time envelope alone. No material
change: every sketch below preserves truth conditions. The drafting writes final text; the
shapes here are shapes, not text.

## 1. The register — devices the drafting must reuse

The manifesto is the owner's voice, not spec voice. It runs in **long confident declarative
periods**: the result leads, the reason follows inside the same sentence, joined by em-dashes
and semicolons rather than broken into displayed steps. There are **no bullet lists in
normative flow** (the itemized `watch/contract/apply` blocks are picture-chapter walkthroughs,
not commitments).

Concrete devices, all of which the drafting reuses so the finesse is inaudible:

- **Definitions woven inline, never displayed.** A term is set in **bold**, glossed by an
  em-dash or relative clause, and used in silence after. The three times are named this way
  inside running prose (177-183), not in a table.
- **The example carries the weight.** The covered call (196-211) is a full Mon/Tue/Wed/Thu
  narrative that *is* the argument — "Fold the assignment at its door time and the book stays
  wrong. Fold it at its execution time and the tail refolds" — not an illustration bolted to a
  prose claim. It is preserved intact and reorganized around, never compressed.
- **Concrete anchors ground every abstraction.** "the time that would be enforced in court";
  "buy shares then meet the dividend's record date and you are entitled; meet the record date
  then buy and you are not." Courtroom and desk, not formalism.
- **Aphorism by antithesis, rule of three.** "Views recompute automatically; money never
  does." "a duplicate to be absorbed, never a neighbour to be ordered." "Verification lives
  with the events; authority lives with the transactions." Semicolon triads carry parallel
  clauses (864-867). Occasional wit ("We live in a simulation…").
- **Present-tense, declarative.** "meaning lives in execution order"; "The monitor's clock
  orders nothing". The system *is* thus.

The finesse is legitimate only where it keeps these devices. It never converts a woven
definition into a list, never trades an anchor for a paraphrase, never flattens an aphorism.

## 2. Where the prose makes the envelope harder than it need be

**D1 — 177-183, the three-times naming sentence: asymmetric overload.**
"An event bears three times: the **execution time** … --- the time that would be enforced in
court, asserted by its source, contestable only in the world and corrected only by a later
event, never edited at the door; the **monitor time** …; and the **door time** …."
The triad scaffold ("three times: A; B; and C") is load-bearing — it says these are three
coordinates of one event and must stay one sentence. The defect is *asymmetry*: member A
carries five appositive qualifications while B and C carry a bare locating clause each, so the
reader meets the naming skeleton and the whole contest/correction/edit theory of execution
time in one breath. Not "three separate sentences" (that loses the triad); rather, let the
naming triad name all three cleanly with their short locating clauses, and let execution
time's qualifications land in the *next* sentence, where the paragraph already turns to
execution order. Sketch: `An event bears three times: the execution time at which it happened
in the world; the monitor time at which the Event Monitor observed it at the boundary; and the
door time at which the single writer admitted it through the one door.` — then a following
period opens on execution time's authority (see D2).

**D2 — 178-180, execution-time qualifications: mismatched joinery.**
Four qualifications ride one intonation with inconsistent connectives — three comma-appositives
plus an "and"-bundled pair ("contestable only in the world **and** corrected only by a later
event"). The reader cannot tell coordinate from subordinate. The content is a clean quartet:
court-authoritative / source-asserted / world-mutable / door-immutable. Relocated to its own
sentence (per D1), render as one em-dash-gated parallel series, uniform commas, "and" removed
so the four beat evenly. Sketch: `Execution time is the time that would be enforced in court
--- asserted by its source, contestable only in the world, corrected only by a later event,
never edited at the door.` Every qualification preserved; the signature phrase kept verbatim
(§3).

**D3 — 183-186, "meaning lives in execution order": principle yoked to mechanism.**
"…meaning lives in execution order, so the fold's order is execution order --- a late arrival
whose execution time precedes events already folded takes its place among them, and everything
after it is refolded." Two distinct facts share one dash-joined breath: the *principle*
(execution order is the fold order) and the *mechanism* (late arrival inserts, tail refolds);
and the mechanism clause holds a nine-word relative subject before its verb "takes." End the
principle as its own aphoristic period, then state the mechanism as its own sentence with the
verb brought forward. Sketch: `…so the fold's order is execution order. A late arrival takes
its place among the events already folded whenever its execution time precedes theirs, and
everything after it is refolded.` Both facts kept; "meaning lives in execution order" left
standing as the signature (§3).

**D4 — 187-190, the total-order sentence: the "resting only on" tail restates two of three
keys and drops the third.**
"…decided by execution time, then door time, then the event's hash --- deterministic, total,
and computable by any party from the record alone, resting only on the time the world would
enforce and the time the door assigns." The order is decided by *three* keys; the tail says it
rests "only on" *two* (execution and door), silently omitting the hash. A precise reader cannot
tell whether this is precision (the hash is record-internal, not an external input, so it is
not something the order "rests on") or a slip. Shape sketch: separate the property claim
(deterministic, total, party-computable from the record alone) from the key characterization,
so the "only on … and …" no longer appears to contradict the three-key rule.
**FLAG (content, not prose):** whether the hash is excluded from what the order "rests on" is a
substantive claim — do not resolve it in wording. Return to the responsible agent to confirm
the intended reading before the drafting commits; a "clarification" here could silently narrow
or widen the claim. No sketch asserts the resolution.

**D5 — 864-870, C-12.6 explain-item sentence: nested lists in one breath.**
"…is published as a named explain item --- in the profit-and-loss explain, a line attributed to
the reordering, to the event that caused it, and to the segment of its lateness: the world's,
from execution to monitor, or ours, from monitor to the door." A triad ("to X, to Y, and to
Z") is held open while a colon-introduced disjunctive apposition ("the world's … or ours …")
is parsed — two list structures nested. Let the main clause and its triad close, then give the
lateness split its own short sentence, in the split's existing canonical wording. Sketch:
`…a line attributed to the reordering, to the event that caused it, and to the segment of its
lateness --- the world's, from execution to monitor, or ours, from monitor to the door.`
(promoting the colon to a dash and letting the split stand as a closing apposition, not a
nested one). **Note, no action:** the same world/ours split is stated at 191-192 and again
here; this is deliberate — C-2.7 and C-12.6 are self-standing clauses. Do not dedup across
clauses; the split's wording is canonical and identical in both, which is correct.

The covered call (196-211) carries no prose defect. It is the model of the register — parallel
imperatives, semicolon triad close — and is preserved intact.

## 3. Load-bearing signatures — reorganize around, never replace

The finesse moves these; it never rewords them. Each is a fixed anchor, aphorism, or normative
key.

- **"the time that would be enforced in court"** (178) — the authority-anchor for execution
  time. Verbatim.
- The four execution-time qualifications, individually: **"asserted by its source"**,
  **"contestable only in the world"**, **"corrected only by a later event"**, **"never edited
  at the door"** (179-180). D2 regularizes only the *joinery*; each phrase survives whole.
- **"meaning lives in execution order"** (184) — fixes why the fold obeys execution time.
- **"The monitor's clock orders nothing: it is provenance"** (190-191) — fixes monitor time's
  status.
- **"an event's lateness splits into the world's delay, execution to monitor, and ours,
  monitor to door"** (191-192) — the canonical lateness-split wording, reused verbatim in
  C-12.6.
- **"a duplicate to be absorbed, never a neighbour to be ordered"** (193-194) — the identity /
  dedup aphorism.
- **"execution time, then door time, then the event's hash"** (187) — the normative ordering
  key. Not reworded; D4 touches only the tail that follows it.
- **"the time the world would enforce and the time the door assigns"** (189-190) — the paired
  key characterizations.
- **"repaired forward, never edited"** (849-850) — C-12.4's governing verb-pair.
- **"Views recompute automatically; money never does"** (855, 871) — the correction/refold
  antithesis, in both C-12.4 and C-12.6.
- **"the rollback of a bad delivery, not a rewrite of history"** (861) — the fork aphorism,
  C-12.5.
- **"a named explain item"** (209, 867) — the fixed term for the published difference.
- The covered-call narrative entire (196-211) — the canonical example; kept intact.
