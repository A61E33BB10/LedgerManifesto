## 4. Part B promises nothing but the mandate (coordinator area 3) — **holds, with one wording exception.**
I hunted every sentence for a prohibited recommendation/solution/remedy. The document is scrupulous, and it *actively guards* against the two natural misreads: PE-3(i) forbids reading σ_prod as a pricing vol ("not the diffusion coefficient of any SDE… σ_prod ≠ σ_mod"), and PE-1(A5) keeps 𝒟 "a mathematical object only… sticky-strike, sticky-moneyness, and sticky-delta are instances," never a recommended dynamic. The one imperative in Part B ("the certificate's carry line must declare which volatility reconciles it") is a *Part A* mandate (VM-4) being pointed to, not a Part B prescription. **The carry-line DO is clear and concrete:** on the carry line, record which volatility the theta-gamma balance was reconciled against.
- **m1 (MINOR — the one advice-creep):** VM-6 now says a carry line "prices that gamma at **the wrong volatility** — the model's, rather than the product instantaneous volatility." Calling the model vol "wrong" is normative and sits in tension with VM-4's even-handed "declare which volatility reconciles it: the model's, *or* the product's," and with the whole out-of-scope stance on model choice (MD-9, MD-15, C-Scope.11). If both are legitimate declared choices, the model vol is not "wrong" — it is a choice whose residual you must surface. A counterparty in a dispute could quote your own manifesto calling your mark's vol "wrong." Soften to "a volatility other than σ_prod." This is the only place Part-B-adjacent text reads like advice.

## 5. The two volatility discussions (coordinator area 4) — **the MATERIAL finding.**

### M1 (MATERIAL) — the "convention" (Part A) and the "rule 𝒟" (Part B) are never reconciled.
- **Part A** has a *convention* family: VM-3 chains are "per re-marking convention," each localising "to its own recorded convention coordinate (VM-5)"; VM-5's convention is "which quantity the desk holds invariant" for attribution, producing the **shadow greek** "routed through the held-invariant factor."
- **Part B** has the *surface-move map 𝒟* — "a declared spot-dynamic," sticky-strike vs sticky-delta — which determines the hybrid greeks and hence σ_prod ("one product under two rules… has different σ_prod," PE-3(ii)); and PE-5's multi-name channel is the **shadow gamma**.

These are the *same underlying sticky-X choice* seen from two sides — 𝒟 gives you the hybrid greeks; the VM-5 convention splits the P&L using (in effect) those same greeks — yet the document never says so. A risk manager is left unable to answer the one question that determines what they must declare: **is 𝒟 the same declared term as the VM-5 attribution convention, or a second, independently-selectable coordinate?** (You *could* hedge sticky-strike but attribute sticky-delta — if that is legal, they are two coordinates; if not, one.) The "shadow greek / shadow gamma" collision makes the conflation near-certain: a reader will assume they are the same object, or wonder why they carry almost the same name.
- This matters for reproducibility, the document's own flagship: VM-2 says "the same coordinates reproduce the valuation," VM-5 makes the convention a coordinate, and σ_prod is "specific to the rule 𝒟." If 𝒟 is a coordinate not already in VM-2's list, the coordinate list is incomplete; if it *is* the VM-5 convention, the document has two names for one component (a one-name-discipline tension for FORMALIS/CONCORDIA — I flag, I don't adjudicate).
- **Fix (one to two sentences):** state the relationship where 𝒟 is introduced (PE-1 A5) and where the convention is (VM-5) — either "𝒟 *is* the surface-dynamics content of the VM-5 convention, recorded once and read in two roles (attribution split in Part A, hybrid greeks in Part B)," or "𝒟 and the attribution convention are two distinct declared coordinates." And reconcile "shadow gamma" (PE-5) with "shadow greek" (VM-5) — is the former an instance of the latter?

## 6. Fresh jargon audit (coordinator area 5) — minor, inherent to real math.
- **m6 (MINOR):** **Feynman–Kac** is the central term and is glossed adequately for the risk manager ("the model's own pricing PDE… its greeks combine to zero") but arrives as a bare proper noun for the non-quant — one plain handle at first use ("the model's own pricing equation, the consistency its price and greeks satisfy by construction") would carry them. **Gâteaux derivative** (PE-2) is deep, but it is confined to equation machinery the non-quant skips, with "surface-response terms" as the prose handle — acceptable. **Admissible** is glossed to "arbitrage-free" (good). **Diffusion coefficient** is glossed ("the volatility function of the model"). **"Instantaneous"** in the coined name invites confusion with the SDE instantaneous vol σ_mod, which PE-3(i) then explicitly denies — the name and its own disclaimer are in mild tension; inherited from the corpus, so note rather than rename.

## 7. Other minor findings

- **m2 (MINOR — carry-line field):** VM-4's "declare which volatility… the model's, or the product's" reads as a *binary*, but PE-4 names three candidates (model σ_mod, raw implied σ, product σ_prod). The declared field should name *whichever* vol was used, not a model/product boolean.
- **m3 (MINOR — the mandate meets the pathology):** PE-6 mandates σ_prod "computed and stored… for every product and every valuation," but PE-3/PE-5 show σ_prod is undefined at poles (Γ_hyb→0) and imaginary where N/M<0 — *for vanillas*. PE-6 says the "poles and sign changes… are thereby auditable," but does not say *what to record when σ_prod does not exist*. State it: the pathology (pole / no real value) is itself the recorded diagnostic — otherwise an implementer does not know what to write to the field.
- **m4 (MINOR — why the mandate helps):** the single mandate's *value* is not spelled out. What does a σ_prod pole or blow-up *tell the desk*? That the carry line's theta-gamma reconciliation is degenerate there — the carry-line analog of VM-4's O(1) residual at a digital. One clause connecting "σ_prod pathology" to "carry-attribution fragility" turns PE-6 from an instruction into a control the practitioner understands.
- **m5 (MINOR — the missing number, high-value):** Part A shines because it puts a number through everything; Part B never puts a single number through σ_prod. Its headline claim — "even a vanilla has σ_prod ≠ σ" — is abstract where it could be visceral. Reuse Part A's base call (σ=30%, Γ=0.0265, vega ~0.20/pt): under a stated skew (say −1 vol point per +1% spot), exhibit Γ_adj, then σ_prod = 30%·√(Γ/Γ_adj) = *[some 27-ish%]* — "the breakeven vol is 27%, not the 30% you quoted." One number bridges the two parts and makes the whole point of Part B land for the practitioner.
