# DIRAC — Round 9 — States.tex

**Lens:** Is the three-home structure inevitable — no unexplained special case?

**Verdict: OBVIOUS**

## What makes it inevitable

The derivation is a genuine 2×2, and both axes are *exhaustive*, which is what
inevitability demands (not merely a tidy enumeration):

- **Key axis** — unit alone vs (holder, unit). Wallet-alone is excluded (every
  economic fact about a wallet is a fact about its relationship to some unit;
  KYC/permissions are identity, not economic state). Multi-unit is reified to a
  unit. The reification is flagged in-line as *assumed, not established here*,
  with the managed-account mandate (−1/+1) discharging it for one relationship.
- **Discipline axis** — keep the prior vs discard it. A true binary (keep ∨
  discard; deletion barred by immutability), not an arbitrary list.

Three cells filled, one empty. Three homes, two maps (terms and status share the
unit key and ride as a pair). All of this falls out of the two axes.

## The one asymmetry, and why it is not a special case

The empty cell — (holder, unit) × definition — is the single asymmetry my lens
must test. I checked whether it falls out of the *same* principle that fills the
other three, or requires an extra axiom.

It falls out of the one boundary test: no (holder, unit) fact is read
past-dated, because every such fact is the ledger's own (held quantity folded
from the move stream, high-water mark written by the valuation event), and an
external per-(holder, unit) figure is a reconciliation input, not a definition
the ledger adopts. The seal argument (a definition there would be a second writer
for a held quantity `applyMove` already owns) is *overdetermination* — explicitly
marked secondary ("could not be admitted in any case"). One principle fills all
four cells. "Three homes are forced, not counted" holds.

## Load-bearing example that closes the gap

Benchmark identity vs benchmark level (same index provider authors both) proves
the *test*, not the author, places a fact. It also carries the otherwise-dense
point that placement is by **consumption at the boundary**, not recoverability —
both terms and status are replay-recoverable, yet a past value consumed in live
operation (a past-dated entitlement driven by terms in force on a date) must
exist as data, while a past value wanted only for audit is left to replay.

## Fresh-engineer check

Each occupied cell has one concrete, sufficient reason: buyer +1000 / seller
−1000 (Position needs the holder); one settlement price copied per holder would
drift = reconciliation break by construction (Status drops the holder);
append-only version list and overwrite-in-place cell cannot inhabit one value
(Terms distinct from Status). A reader who has never seen the problem reaches
"three is forced."

  inevitability.
- The two-key exhaustiveness rests on the multi-instrument reification, which the

The structure reads as inevitable. The empty cell is the one asymmetry, and it
is placed by the same single test that fills the other three. OBVIOUS.
