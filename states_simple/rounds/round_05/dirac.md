# DIRAC — Round 5 — States.tex

## Verdict: NOT-YET

The structure is close to inevitable. Three of the four cells fall out cleanly,
each shape forced by its discipline (NonEmpty list ← append-keep-prior;
sum type ← overwrite-keep-current; record ← conserved + riders), and the
"three homes, two maps" collapse is honest: distinction-by-key makes maps,
distinction-by-discipline makes fields of one value. The managed-account
dissolution — the tempting wallet-keyed counterexample resolving into a
(client, mandate-unit) position — is the right Dirac move and I admire it.

But the whole claim of *three* rests on the **fourth cell being empty**, and the
emptiness is asserted by survey, not derived by structure. That is the residue.

## The residue

The document fixes its placement principle in §The Answer:

> "what places a fact is how its correction is recorded, not who authored the
> number — the same index provider's benchmark identity is terms, its benchmark
> level status."

This rules authorship *out* as a discriminator. Good for terms-vs-status.

Then §Why Three (P4) argues the empty cell precisely *by* authorship/origin:

> "Every position is the ledger's own record of its move events; any external
> per-(holder, unit) figure ... is a boundary reconciliation input, not a
> definition the ledger stores and versions. ... Nothing makes a position a
> version-retained definition, so the cell stays empty."

So the empty cell is empty because a survey of (holder, unit) facts turns up only
running observations, plus a scoping decision not to store external per-pair
statements. That is *contingent* emptiness dressed as *structural* emptiness. A
fresh reader cannot, from the stated principle, rule out a future per-(holder,
unit) versioned definition — the "2×2, three occupied" reads as current-state,
not as inevitable. And the careful reader feels the two passages in tension: P4
decides the cell by who authors / where the fact originates, the very ground
§The Answer forbade.

The unification that would close it is *present in pieces but never stated*: the
ledger **versions what it receives at its boundary** (unit definitions — terms,
ISIN, reference data — corrected and audited against an external authority) and
**derives what it owns** (relationships — every position folded from its own move
stream). The definition discipline can therefore inhabit only the key on which
the ledger is a *recorder of external authority* — the unit key. On the
(holder, unit) key, where the ledger *is* the single-writer authority, there is no
received definition to version; all per-pair facts are owned, derived, running
observations. Empty by construction, not by survey.

This also dissolves the apparent contradiction: authorship does not decide
*which discipline* among stored facts (so benchmark level is status, not terms),
but received-vs-owned decides *which key can host the definition discipline at
all* (so no (holder, unit) definition exists). Two different questions; the text
collides them.

## What would flip this to OBVIOUS

State the received-vs-owned principle once, where the empty cell is argued (§Why
Three P4 and/or §The Answer's fourth-cell sentence), and reconcile it explicitly
with "not who authored the number" by distinguishing "which discipline among
stored facts" from "which key may host a definition." Then the fourth cell is
empty by construction and the three-home count is inevitable rather than
surveyed.
