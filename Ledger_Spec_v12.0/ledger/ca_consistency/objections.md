### Objections vs THORP
THORP is the strongest-anchored of the four designs — every file:line citation verifies against the v12.0 bundle — and its architecture is sound at the top level: basis coordinate as a UnitStatus fold, tag as foreign key assigned at a parse boundary, enforcement once at a skolem-sealed consumption door, protected primitives untouched, adjusted data as lineage-carrying derivations. But the committee finds the proofs systematically outrun the constructors. The epoch numbering is unstable under the design's own retro-effectivity story (effective-prefix counting over a knowledge-ordered append-only list renumbers stored immutable tags); ApplyBoundary-as-append breaks the P6 idempotence the StatusWrite discipline it joins explicitly states; the groupoid/T⁻¹ time-travel theorem fails on `Pending`, unguarded `f = 0`, and terminal successions; `Pending` cannot crystallise inside an append-only list by any specified operation; derived-data transport silently requires either re-derivation (never mandated) or equivariance of the derivation under adjustment — a black-box-interior property whose use violates the model-agnostic directive and which MILEWSKI explicitly refuses; the scalar-affine `Op` cannot express the composition/succession adjustments the design claims to cover; and the Tier-2 types cannot implement two of the workflows claimed for them (quarantine's partial snapshot, §5.6's cross-basis attribution). One type-level claim (Tier-1 'no epoch at which the wrong composite typechecks') is refuted by the exported Boundary constructor. Where THORP is weak, a competitor is demonstrably stronger on the same point (MILEWSKI's absolute idempotent SetBasis and refusal of equivariance; NOETHER's unexported Adj constructor and datum-kind operator alphabet; NAZAROV's named TA-BASIS trust assumption with detection). The design is repairable — most objections have known fixes exhibited in the competing memos — but as filed it asserts several theorems its exhibited types and data do not prove.
- [MATERIAL] `ApplyBoundary BoundaryRec` appends to `usBasis :: [BoundaryRec]`, violating the StatusWrite discipline it claims to join, which is stated and relied upon as total, last-write-wins, and idempotent (P6; sec04.tex:257-260, Ledger.hs applyStatus). — Breaks a stated invariant: an append is not idempotent, so a duplicated or replayed ApplyBoundary double-increments the epoch and corrupts replay determinism (P8); MILEWSKI's absolute-valued `SetBasis (CAEpoch n) [...]` demonstrates the P6-compliant alternative, so THORP is strictly dominated on this point.
- [MATERIAL] The EpochN tag is an unstable foreign key: epoch is defined as the length of the *effective* prefix of an append-only (knowledge-ordered) basis history, so a retro-effective boundary insertion (explicitly in scope, §1 and §7, finding 8) renumbers all later epochs and silently re-points every stored `toBasis` and `usLastSettle` tag on immutable log entries. — Breaks referential integrity and determinism across restatement: the design versions the basis history 'like ProductTerms' but the stored tags carry no basis-history version coordinate, so the same immutable tag denotes different bases before and after a restatement, admitting deterministic mispricing of every observation ingested before the correction; equal-brEff ties additionally leave the non-commuting composite order under-defined.
- [MATERIAL] Section 6's time-travel claim rests on 'T⁻¹, which exists because operators are invertible', but the design's own constructors refute total invertibility: `Pending SourceRef` has no action in either direction, `f ≠ 0` on `Affine Rational Cash` is a comment rather than an enforced guard, and the merger-for-cash succession edge (§8) is terminal with no inverse. — A claim asserted, not proved (Correctness principle): the groupoid theorem fails on the exhibited data type, so backward transport for historical valuation is partial exactly where the design says it is total; NAZAROV explicitly models the non-invertible terminal case, showing the gap is real and known.
- [MATERIAL] The `Pending` crystallisation path is unimplementable as stated: when the spin-off parameter is published, the design provides no operation that resolves `Pending` inside an append-only `[BoundaryRec]` without either mutating a BoundaryRec (forbidden by its own append-only invariant) or appending a resolution record that corrupts 'epoch = length of effective prefix'. — Unimplementable / breaks the stated append-only invariant: the two available moves each violate a stated property, and no third is specified — the spin-off case the memo calls 'the normal case, not the edge' (§7) has no well-defined state transition.
- [MATERIAL] Transport of derived (joint-basis) data is undefined without an unstated assumption: `withSnapshot` claims to transport every consumed datum to the target basis while the framework 'never inspects the derivation', but transporting a derived datum across a boundary on one of its inputs requires either re-derivation from rebased inputs (never mandated anywhere in the memo) or the assumption that adjustment commutes with the derivation. — Violates the model-agnostic directive: equivariance of a derivation under adjustment is a property of the black box's interior, precisely the fact MILEWSKI names and refuses ('rebase ∘ derive = derive ∘ rebase ... is a modelling fact'); THORP silently depends on it for the index/divisor case (§2, §5 failure mode c) or else leaves the transport undefined — either way the constructor cannot be implemented as specified.
- [MATERIAL] The operator alphabet is scalar-affine only (`Op = Affine Rational Cash | Pending`), yet the design claims coverage of index composition maintenance, basket redefinition (sec07.tex:132-135), and succession — adjustments that are substitutions and recompositions on non-scalar datum kinds, not affine maps on scalars. — Unimplementable at the stated types: there is no Kind-indexed action (contrast NOETHER's `Recompose σ`/datum-kind table and MILEWSKI's `Kind a`/`KComposition`), so the claimed closure of findings 5 and 6 and the appF Observable-row rewrite cannot be expressed by the exhibited `Op`; the coverage claim is asserted, not constructible.
- [MATERIAL] The attribution workflow (§5 item 6) requires transporting the opening (quantity, price) pair into the closing snapshot's basis, but the Tier-2 skolem seal makes `Snapshot b0` and `Snapshot b1` never unify and no typed cross-snapshot transport door is provided — as typed, only the bare Cash subtraction `pnl` is expressible and the claimed basis-invariant decomposition is unwritable. — Unimplementable as stated: the very mechanism that seals against mixing also forbids the legitimate transported comparison; adding a naive escape hatch would break the seal, and the memo specifies neither the door nor its restriction, so the §5.6 numbers cannot be produced by the §4 API.
- [MATERIAL] The quarantine regime ('book values on time minus a named exclusion list') is unrepresentable in the constructor's type `Either StaleReport (Snapshot b)` with total `PriceV b = UnitId -> Price' b`: either the whole snapshot fails (which is the Block regime, not Quarantine) or excluded units still receive a `Price' b`, resurrecting the priced-in-wrong-basis hole the design exists to close. — Type system fails to prevent the invalid state (Level 1/Level 4 mismatch): the stated types cannot implement the stated default workflow; NOETHER's `Either BasisError Cash` per consumption and MILEWSKI's `Valuation = Priced | Unpriced | PricedStale` sum show the representable alternatives, so the totality inherited from sec05's PriceVec is here a liability the memo does not resolve.
- [MATERIAL] Tier-1's claim that the mis-ordered composite 'is not an arrow of the groupoid — there is no epoch at which it typechecks' is false: `Boundary (e :: N)` has an exported record constructor with a free type index, so `Boundary { bF = 2, bC = 0 } :: Boundary e` types the split first and the wrong composite compiles. — A correctness claim asserted, not proved: the guarantee holds only under a canonical-authorship restriction (unexported constructor, ledger-fold-only authoring) that NOETHER ('the constructor is UNEXPORTED and the sole author is the ledger's fold') and MILEWSKI ('constructor NOT exported') both state and THORP omits — the exhibited counterexample defeats the stated type-level theorem.
- [MATERIAL] The per-(unit, source) epoch offset that assigns every observation's tag at ingest is a load-bearing external trust assumption with no name, no owner, and no detection control: a mis-declared offset yields mis-tagged data that the transport machinery then certifies as coherent, and the only stated residual control (daily reconciliation of vendor factors vs applied BoundaryRecs, §5 item 7) checks boundaries, not per-source tags. — Admits a mispricing channel the framework then launders as type-correct; strictly dominated by NAZAROV, which names TA-BASIS with owner, violation consequence, and W3 partition-quarantine as its detection signal — THORP has the same exposure and none of the controls.
- [STYLISTIC] `ingest :: ... -> Maybe TaggedObs` discards the failure reason, against the typed-failure discipline the memo applies everywhere else (`Either StaleReport`, `StaleReport` contents enumerated in §7). — Inconsistent error discipline, easily repaired to an `Either IngestError TaggedObs` without structural change; no invariant is broken because `Maybe` is total, but the design's own standard (nothing silent, failures enumerated) is not met at its own parse boundary.
- [STYLISTIC] The 'flagged-stale carry' regime introduces a logged election event with no named canonical writer: a design whose entire method is closed writer tables (C11, the ApplyBoundary rows it does add) leaves this event class without its row. — Documentation/closure gap rather than a broken invariant: the event is well-conceived (auditable, typed), but omitting its writer row in a memo that adds C11 rows for every other new event is an incompleteness in the migration list (§9), fixable by one line.
- [STYLISTIC] `withSnapshot`'s continuation scopes the `Left StaleReport` case under the skolem quantifier `forall b` even though `StaleReport` carries no b-indexed data, obscuring where the seal actually binds. — Clarity issue only: `(forall b. Snapshot b -> r) -> Either StaleReport r` (or equivalent) expresses the same guarantee with the quantifier where the proof lives; the published signature works but misstates the scope of the sealing argument in a document that trades on making such scopes exact.

### Objections vs NAZAROV
NAZAROV's synthesis is architecturally sound in its skeleton — the coordinate choice (a ledger-authored, monotone, per-unit denomination epoch in UnitStatus, advanced atomically with the CA's moves through the existing single door) is correctly argued against timestamps and calendars; the protected primitives are untouched; adjusted data are strictly derivations with recorded lineage; and the model-agnostic directive is held throughout (operators are declared data applied by one generic evaluator, illustrations properly bounded). But the enforcement layer under-delivers its claims on four fronts the committee counts as material: the composition law folds operators in booking order rather than effective order, so out-of-order CA notifications produce a permanently wrong, immutably logged composite by the memo's own non-commutativity example; nothing welds the declared operator to the moves booked in the same transaction, so Property 5 remains unproved and a mismatched declaration commits silently (where THORP proves the invariance identity and MILEWSKI mandates a neutrality oracle); the typed seam is unit-blind and its Adjust values forgeable, so the headline invariant 'mixed-basis pricing is a type error' is unsound across units and D9's fail-closed rule is asserted rather than constructed; and the cross-unit joint basis needed for the index/divisor case the memo claims to close has no carrier in the single-Nat index, with the affine scalar alphabet unable to express the succession and recomposition morphisms of its own CDM table. Add the internal contradiction in W2's use of 'coherent'. The design is strictly dominated by THORP on joint bases, per-source lag, and the value-invariance theorem, and by NOETHER/MILEWSKI on sole-authorship of adjustment arrows; its distinctive contributions — the re-stamp event class for late notifications, the W3 epoch-partitioned quorum, and the W4 attestation class for CA declarations — are genuine and worth carrying forward into whichever skeleton survives.
- [MATERIAL] The composition law (D10) folds adjustment operators in log (booking) order while D1 defines ε by committed count, so two corporate actions notified out of effective order compose in the wrong order permanently, and the design explicitly forbids representing the correct composite ('no other order is representable'). — Admits a mispricing by the memo's own non-commutativity discriminator (49 vs 50): the §6 re-stamp mechanism corrects observation coordinates but never reorders the operator fold, and the immutable log freezes the wrong composite; NOETHER and THORP both index epochs by effective order with knowledge time on the bitemporal axis, so the target is strictly dominated here.
- [MATERIAL] Nothing welds the declared operator parameters in CaDecl to the moves booked in the same atomic τ_CA: a transaction declaring AMul(1/2) on price-like data while its txMoves triple every holding (or AAdd(−2) while moving €3/share of cash) typechecks, commits, and silently breaks value continuity. — Admits a mispricing through a fully legal path and leaves Property 5 (Lifecycle Value Invariance) unproved: the §appB oracle list (basis coherence, composition order, fail-closed, re-stamp round-trip) contains no value-invariance or neutrality oracle, whereas THORP proves (q·f)·((p−c)/f) = qp − qc against the booked cash leg and MILEWSKI mandates a per-CA-class neutrality oracle — the target is strictly dominated on the one theorem the pairing was supposed to buy back.
- [MATERIAL] The typed seam is unit-blind: Quote, Price, QtyAt, Adjust, and sameEpoch are indexed by epoch alone with no UnitId, so pricing unit B's position with unit A's quote at a numerically equal epoch typechecks, `consume` accepts it, and a ledger-authored Adjust derived for unit A applies to unit B's data. — Breaks the very invariant the design claims to enforce ('mixed-basis pricing is a type error') — epochs are per-unit counters, so equality of bare Nat indices across units is meaningless; the mispricing is representable both at compile time and through the runtime witness check, while THORP and MILEWSKI carry the unit in the tag and reject WrongUnit explicitly; the per-unit-keyed PriceVec refinement mitigates one call path but the normative seam functions (markValue, adjust, consume) remain unsound.
- [MATERIAL] The Adjust GADT constructors (AMul, AAdd, AId, AThen) are presented as freely available, so any consumer can fabricate `adjust (AMul r) q` with an arbitrary rational and cross an epoch boundary with no committed declaration, defeating D9's fail-closed rule ('no Adjust value, no crossing'). — The stated invariant 'adjustments exist only where a declaration exists' is asserted but not delivered by the mechanism: unlike NOETHER (constructor unexported, sole author the ledger's fold over logged CA events, 'an Adj m n IS a proof') and MILEWSKI (rebase as the only producer inside a sealed scope), nothing in §5 restricts authorship of Adjust values, so an undeclared or wrongly parameterised crossing is representable — a silent-fallback-shaped hole the design's own convictions forbid.
- [MATERIAL] D7's 'epoch vector of its inputs' for derived data has no carrier anywhere in the formalism: the single-Nat phantom index cannot express a joint basis over several units, so the cross-unit index/divisor case (reproduction (c)) that §4 and the §9 CDM table claim to handle is untypeable, and D7 immediately collapses the vector to 'all inputs share ε', which is meaningless across units with independent per-unit counters. — Unimplementable as specified for an in-scope failure mode the memo claims to close: a divisor derived from constituents at epochs 3, 7, and 2 can never satisfy a single shared index, so either the design silently ignores constituent bases (mispricing) or blocks all basket valuation (unshippable); THORP's joint basis `Map UnitId EpochN` with pointwise join solves exactly this and strictly dominates.
- [MATERIAL] The operator alphabet (AId, AMul, AAdd — exact-rational affine maps on scalar quotes) cannot express constructs the memo itself relies on: the merger 'conversion morphism' to a successor unit starting at ε = 0 (crosses units and resets the index — no constructor has that type), the composite (composition, weights, divisor) operator of the index row, and quantity-side transport (adjust is typed only on Quote, yet §8's attribution re-expresses QtyAt holdings across the boundary via 'the declared dual' with no typed function to do it). — The mechanism as typed cannot implement its own §9 CDM mapping or its §8 attribution walkthrough — the claimed closures of FM-6 (datum succession) and reproduction (c) rest on morphisms that have no representation in the design's stated types, violating the project's correctness principle that properties hold by construction rather than assertion.
- [MATERIAL] W2's failure semantics ('value with the last coherent basis assignment as a whole') is undefined under the design's own definition of coherence — §5 defines a coherent vector as one whose epoch assignment agrees with ε_t for every unit in scope, and a stale assignment by that definition is precisely not coherent — while the whole-vector reading would regress fresh, unaffected units to a stale basis. — Unimplementable as stated: the reporting fallback that decides which number every valuation consumer sees during a CA window rests on a term the memo defines once, in a way that contradicts its use; the design needs (and does not supply) a second, internal-consistency notion of coherence distinct from agreement-with-current-ε, and the ambiguity spans the exact window where mis-based valuation occurs.
- [STYLISTIC] The 'does not compile' rhetoric of §8 and §11 overstates the system-level guarantee: since every stored datum and position flows through the existential erasure (SomeQuote/SomePos), the operative protection in the deployed system is the runtime sameEpoch witness check returning Left BasisMismatch, and the compile-time exclusion holds only at didactic authorship sites. — The memo concedes this itself ('the runtime complement of the compile-time law') yet headlines 'a program that does not compile' three times, including in the verification protocol; the claim should be stated as compile-time-at-authorship, runtime-at-the-seam — the same honest framing C11 uses — rather than as a blanket unrepresentability result; MILEWSKI's rejection of DataKinds epochs for exactly this reason ('static knowledge the system cannot have') is the more precise statement of the same architecture.
- [STYLISTIC] A single scalar epoch per unit that advances on every basis-changing CA for all datum classes forces every declaration to enumerate an explicit identity action on an open-ended datum-class registry (D9), and re-tags every position and every datum class on, e.g., a divisor-only or dividend-only event where most classes are untouched. — A minimalism and operability friction rather than an invariant break: fail-closed-with-mandatory-AId is a defensible discipline, but it couples the epoch of every datum class to every CA and makes later addition of a new datum class retroactively block crossings over old epochs; a per-class or joint-basis coordinate (as in THORP) carries the same guarantee with less churn.
- [STYLISTIC] D6 'repairs' the sec04 2×2 by declaring the spec's own worked example (sec04.tex:48–52: 'a benchmark level the ledger records and overwrites is ledger-authored') a misclassification and reversing it by re-reading the criterion, without the derivation-then-proof discipline the project's correctness principle demands for revising settled text. — The three-home model is explicitly open to revision, so the move is in scope, but the memo presents a substantive reclassification of observed facts into the Terms-column discipline as 'corrected, not extended' by fiat; the change may well be right, yet it is asserted rather than argued, which is a clarity/method defect, not a correctness one.

### Objections vs NOETHER
NOETHER's diagnosis and placement are sound and match the convergent consensus of all three competitors: the basis is a fold over logged CA events, hence a UnitStatus field behind the existing single door; data carry a compact reference (the epoch), not a copy; enforcement is a typed seam at the quantity-price pairing; adjustment is lazy over an immutable raw store; effective order dissolves the composition-order question. Nothing protected is touched and the migration surface is genuinely minimal. But the execution has soundness holes at nearly every point where the competitors did the harder work: the epoch write is a non-idempotent increment in a write set whose stated law is last-write-wins idempotency (P6) — a broken invariant the memo claims not to weaken; the phantom Nat index carries no unit coordinate, so mixed-unit pairings at coincidentally equal epochs typecheck and the central 'compile error' guarantee is unsound precisely in the multi-unit (index/divisor) case; succession (Substitute to a successor at epoch 0) and the inverse adjustments §5 promises for time travel are both unrepresentable in the Adj GADT that defines legality; adjust is claimed total and cache-sound on Integer prices with no rounding rule, falsifying both totality and the path-independence-of-derivation claim; the ingestion stamp defaults to the ledger's prevailing epoch, mis-basing the lagging-source case (appD's mQuoteEx) the design claims to subsume; and late/retro-effective CAs renumber the epoch ordinals with no re-stamp mechanism for already-stamped data, breaking the stability of the design's own foreign key. Each hole is closed by at least one competitor (MILEWSKI's absolute SetBasis and invertible groupoid with runtime-honest skolems; THORP's unit-scoped skolem snapshot, per-(unit,source) tag, and exact invertible transport; NAZAROV's witness equality, fail-closed declarations, and re-stamp events), so on the seam mechanics NOETHER is strictly dominated; its distinctive contributions — the conservation-pairing framing and the P10 restatement with proof unchanged — survive, but the memo as written asserts stronger guarantees than its own artifacts can carry.
- [MATERIAL] The new constructor `AdvanceEpoch CAOp` is an increment, not an absolute write, so it violates the stated law of the closed StatusWrite set — 'each write is last-write-wins and idempotent (P6)' (sec04.tex:235, 251–260) — which the design claims to extend without weakening. — Breaks a stated invariant: every existing StatusWrite is absolute-valued and re-application is the identity; re-applying AdvanceEpoch under retry/replay-with-redelivery advances the epoch twice, silently mis-basing every subsequent adjustment path. The migration table (§8) asserts 'Invariants: none weakened,' which is false for P6's write discipline. Competitor MILEWSKI's `SetBasis (CAEpoch n)` is absolute and idempotent and strictly dominates here; NOETHER adds the non-idempotent form without remark or proof.
- [MATERIAL] The phantom epoch index carries no unit coordinate: `Price (e :: Nat)` and `Bal (e :: Nat)` unify across distinct units, so `markValue (balance of unit A at epoch 5) (price of unit B at epoch 5)` typechecks — the claimed guarantee 'mixed-basis pairing is a compile error' is unsound exactly in the multi-unit case (index divisor, constituents) the Recompose operator is introduced for. — Counterexample to the design's central invariant: epochs of different units are unrelated counters, and coincidental numeral equality is common (every unit starts at 0). Derived multi-unit data ('g(d_1@n,…,d_k@n) type-checks only when all inputs share one index', §3) is therefore vacuous or wrong: constituent A at epoch 3 and constituent B at epoch 3 share an index but not a basis. THORP's skolem-scoped Snapshot and joint basis `Map UnitId EpochN`, and NAZAROV's per-unit witness equality, both close this; NOETHER's single-Nat index does not.
- [MATERIAL] The succession semantics of `Substitute u' r` — data at (u_old, final epoch) map to (u_new, 0) — is unrepresentable in the enforcement GADT, whose only non-identity constructor is `AStep :: Adj m n -> CAOp -> Adj m (n+1)`: the epoch reset to 0 and the change of unit have no carrier in `Adj`. — Unimplementable as specified: `AStep a (Substitute u' r) :: Adj m (n+1)` types the result as epoch n+1 of the same index family, while §1 declares the successor 'starts at epoch 0' of a different unit. The C8 data-plane claim ('the identifier-succession axis and the basis axis are one mechanism') rests on an arrow the type system cannot express; since Adj is also the sole author of legal adjustments, succession-crossing data have no legal path at all.
- [MATERIAL] Time-travel rule 2 (§5) permits a datum stamped ahead of the replayed epoch to be 'inverse-adjusted... where the operator is declared invertible,' but `Adj m n` admits only ascending paths (AId, AStep) and `adjust :: Adj m n -> Price m -> Price n` only forward application; no inverse arrow, no invertibility-declaration mechanism, and no backward evaluator exist anywhere in the design. — A stated legal operation is untypable in the artifact that defines legality: the prose and the enforcement layer diverge, so either time travel with later-stamped data is impossible (contradicting §5) or it happens outside the typed seam (destroying the guarantee). MILEWSKI's groupoid with `Iso`/`invIso` and THORP's invertible transport `T_{n→m} = T_{m→n}^{-1}` supply exactly the missing structure; NOETHER claims the capability without its carrier.
- [MATERIAL] `adjust` is declared 'total; pure; deterministic' on `Price e = Price Integer` while `Scale k m` acts as ×m/k: halving an odd integer minor-unit price is not integer-valued, no rounding rule is stated, and the exactness argument (§2) covers only quantity residues (explicit cash-in-lieu moves), never the price side. — Admits a mispricing and falsifies two claims at once: totality of `adjust` into Integer fails on odd inputs under Scale 2 1, and the caching soundness claim 'A_{m→n} = A_{k→n} ∘ A_{m→k}, so caching at any intermediate epoch is sound' fails under per-step rounding (rounding at epoch k then adjusting differs from adjusting once) — the derivation is no longer path-independent even given the total order. All three competitors carry exact rationals with a single, named rounding site (THORP §2, NAZAROV D8/D11, MILEWSKI §4.1/§10); NOETHER omits the arithmetic discipline its own conservation theorem requires.
- [MATERIAL] The ingestion stamp 'epoch-at-observation' mis-bases lagging sources: a feed still disseminating in the old basis after the ledger's transition — the next-open lag the spec itself documents (appD.tex:46–48, `mQuoteEx`) — is stamped with the new epoch unless it self-declares, so its cum value is consumed unadjusted at the new epoch, reproducing exactly failure mode (b) inside the typed seam. — Admits a mispricing through the front door: §2's vendor-provenance paragraph handles only feeds that declare their epoch (pre-adjusted at source); the silent-lag case, which appD exists to handle and which the design claims to subsume ('Reference/Ledger.hs:713–720 likewise subsumed', §8), has no mechanism — the stamp defaults to the ledger's prevailing epoch, which is a timestamp-conflation at the boundary of the very kind §1 rejects. THORP's per-(unit, source) tag with a per-source epoch offset closes this case explicitly.
- [MATERIAL] Epoch ordinals are unstable under late or retro-effective corporate actions: §5 indexes epochs 'by effective order,' so a late CA effective in the past renumbers every subsequent epoch, and raw observations immutably stamped under the as-known numbering are silently reinterpreted against a different basis in the corrected sequence — the design specifies compensating transactions for committed moves but no re-stamp mechanism for stored data. — Breaks referential integrity of the design's own foreign key: a stamp `(u, t_obs, m)` written before the restatement names operator chain a_{m+1}..a_n of the as-known sequence, but the corrected replay resolves the same stamp against the restated chain, applying wrong operators deterministically — a mispricing that replays reproducibly. NAZAROV's re-stamp derivation events (right values, corrected coordinates, appended with lineage) are the missing operation; NOETHER's 'basis versions' sentence names the problem without supplying the data-side repair.
- [MATERIAL] The semantics of a datum kind for which a CA event declares no action is unstated, and the framework text hard-codes a blanket classification ('price-like contragrediently... per-share references as price-like, share-count data covariantly') that functions as a default action — absence of declaration yields a default transformation or silent pass-through, never a refusal. — Admits a mispricing via fail-open semantics: §2 simultaneously says the action is 'declared... on each datum kind' and legislates the classification in normative framework prose, so a datum kind the event's declaration does not cover (a dimensionless ratio, a count-like reference) crosses the boundary under the built-in rule rather than blocking. NAZAROV D9 states the correct discipline — 'Absence NEVER means identity; identity MUST be declared' — and NOETHER, whose §6 failure table is otherwise careful, omits this row entirely; the closed four-letter alphabet (Scale/Translate/Substitute/Recompose) likewise has no stated behavior for a CA it cannot express.
- [MATERIAL] The repeated claim that the wrong number 'does not compile' ('€200,000 does not exist in the program', §4) overstates a static guarantee that cannot hold: epochs are runtime replay outputs (the design's own `Obs = forall m. Obs (SNat m) (Price m)` is existential), and the memo never presents the runtime-to-type bridge — no singleton equality witness at consumption, no skolem scope, and no stated authorship discipline for `Bal e` (who assigns the index to a balance read is never said). — Unimplementable as stated: with existentially stamped observations and fold-derived epochs, the honest guarantee is a runtime witness check that refuses (as `value`'s `Either BasisError` concedes), not compile-time exclusion; and absent a single read-side door for `Bal e`, nothing shown prevents indexing a balance at an arbitrary epoch, which voids the seam entirely. MILEWSKI states and solves exactly this ('a DataKinds epoch index would claim static knowledge the system cannot have'; rank-2 skolem, sameness not value); NAZAROV supplies the `sameEpoch`/`Refl` machinery; NOETHER asserts the stronger property without the mechanism that could deliver even the weaker one.
- [STYLISTIC] The §4 remark 'moneyness preserved, no sign flip' asserts a property of what the downstream pricing function will see and conclude from adjusted inputs, stepping past the directive's boundary that correctness is about which data are legal to feed, never about what the function does with them. — A one-sentence drift toward instrument-behavior commentary; the adjustment of the strike datum itself is a permitted illustration, but the moneyness claim is a statement about the black box's inputs' joint meaning under pricing — it should be struck or rephrased as 'both references land in one basis.'
- [STYLISTIC] The conservation statement (§2) is not Bourbaki-grade: 'the pairing ⟨q,p⟩ is invariant under the joint action' is false as stated for Translate, and the correction ('minus explicit-cash translations') is relegated to a parenthesis instead of appearing in the statement of the theorem, whose hypotheses (exact arithmetic, cf. the material rounding objection) are also unstated. — Violates the house style the memo claims ('Bourbaki-grade statements'): the exception clause belongs in the proposition — invariance holds up to the explicitly booked cash legs of the same transaction, under exact arithmetic — and THORP states precisely this identity, (q·f)·((p−c)/f) = q·p − q·c, with the cash leg named on the right-hand side.
- [STYLISTIC] The operator alphabet is keyed on CA event class ('Each CA event class declares an element of an operator alphabet') rather than on the declared terms of the notice, leaving the taxonomy load-bearing where the parameters should be. — Clarity/robustness of exposition rather than a broken invariant, since the operator does ride the event as payload: but THORP's formulation — 'the declared adjustment terms of the notice, never the event class; a 5% stock dividend declares f = 21/20 and is therefore a split regardless of its name' — is the correct register, and NOETHER's class-first phrasing invites exactly the taxonomy disputes it need not have.

### Objections vs MILEWSKI
MILEWSKI's memo is the most category-theoretically articulate of the four and is fully compliant with the model-agnostic directive (the thin-groupoid exposition is bounded, each abstraction's purchase is named, illustrations stay illustrative, and the refusal to assume derivation equivariance is exactly right); the log-order resolution of operator non-commutativity, the rejection of DataKinds epochs in favor of skolem sameness, and the lazy-at-consumption answer to open question 2 are all sound design judgments. But at FORMALIS standards the enforcement layer does not prove what it claims: the rank-2 seam is unsound because `positionAt` takes an arbitrary Ledger so the skolem never seals the ledger whose epoch the scope read (a mixed-basis valuation compiles — the headline unrepresentability claim fails); `Stamped` has no single-door constructor so stamps are forgeable, falsifying §9.5; `arrow` is admittedly non-total on adversarial epochs and returns a silently wrong Right on negative inputs; and the chain-contiguity invariant `arrow` depends on is nowhere stated. The algebra itself is simultaneously too strong and too narrow for the domain: universal exact invertibility plus complete-spec-at-commit excludes pending-parameter spin-offs and terminal cash mergers; identity-by-default on unnamed datum kinds is a silent-fallback mispricing path whose fail-closed alternative the `forall a. Kind a -> Iso a` encoding cannot even express; the single-unit basis cannot type multi-unit derived data, leaving failure mode (c) without a carrier; and succession is untypeable because `rebase`'s WrongUnit guard blocks the exact crossing `AdjSubst` exists for. On the seam-sealing, joint basis, pending operators, per-source lag, and the `usLastSettle` repair, the design is strictly dominated by THORP; on fail-closed declaration and non-invertible morphisms, by NAZAROV. The author's Phase-3 candidate (timestamp + calendar check) is correctly refuted by MILEWSKI's own argument against pure (i), which survives this review intact; the epoch-in-UnitStatus, stamp-on-datum, type-at-seam architecture is the right shape, but this instantiation of it needs the ledger sealed into the scope, a single-door ingest, fail-closed kind actions, a joint basis, and pending/terminal operators before any of its §9 unrepresentability claims are theorems.
- [MATERIAL] The rank-2 seam is unsound: `positionAt :: BasisCtx b -> Ledger -> WalletId -> Maybe (At b Qty)` accepts an arbitrary Ledger, so the skolem b does not seal the ledger whose epoch `withBasis` read, and a mixed-basis valuation compiles. — Counterexample: open a scope on `clone_at t` (epoch n), call `positionAt ctx currentLedger w` where the current ledger has committed the CA (balances at epoch n+1), rebase the historic datum to epoch n via `rebase`, and `markValueAt` typechecks — the phantom-PnL figure the design declares unrepresentable (§9.1, §9.3) is a well-typed program. The design's own §6 time-travel workflow puts two ledgers in flight simultaneously, making this path realistic. THORP closes exactly this hole by constructing `snapBal` inside `withSnapshot`; MILEWSKI leaves the ledger a free parameter and is strictly dominated here. This breaks the design's headline invariant: mixed-basis pricing is claimed to be a compile-time error, and it is not.
- [MATERIAL] `CAAdj = CAAdj (forall a. Kind a -> Iso a)` with the rule 'each CA names what it does per kind, identity elsewhere' installs identity as a silent default, so a datum kind the registrar forgot to name passes through an epoch boundary unadjusted with no error. — Admits a mispricing: a split registered on KSpot but not on KDivSeries leaves per-share dividend forecasts off by the split factor, and nothing fails — no Left, no quarantine, no oracle fires until a valuation is already wrong. Worse, the total-function encoding makes fail-closed inexpressible: `forall a. Kind a -> Iso a` must return something for every kind, structurally forcing a default. NAZAROV's D9 states the correct discipline (absence NEVER means identity; identity must be declared `AId`, undeclared kinds cannot cross); MILEWSKI's type cannot even represent that rule. This is the 'silent fallback' defect class the whole design exists to remove, reintroduced at the operator table.
- [MATERIAL] Ingestion stamping is specified contradictorily — §4.3 says observations are stamped 'from the source's declared convention (§7)' while §5 step 1 says 'stamped from UnitStatus at snap' — and under the UnitStatus reading the late-CA window (case β) produces a silent mispricing the memo asserts cannot occur. — In case β the CA is effective in the world but unlogged: the exchange disseminates post-split ~50 while the ledger's epoch is still n, so the gateway stamps 50 at epoch n and the book values 1,000 × 50 = half its true value. The §7 claim 'positions and data agree on the stale basis, valuation is internally consistent' is therefore false in exactly the window it describes — consistency of stamps does not make the stamped coordinate true. The design provides no detection trigger for this window (NAZAROV's W3 epoch-partitioned quarantine with innovation detection, and THORP's per-(unit,source) epoch offset generalizing mQuoteEx, both do); the residual is misfiled under 'mis-declared vendor convention' when it is a structural window in which a correct declaration is impossible. A specification contradiction at the single boundary that determines the truth of every stamp is a specification-completeness failure (FORMALIS Test 1) that admits a mispricing.
- [MATERIAL] The §9.5 unrepresentability claim — 'a bare unbased scalar has no path to the seam' — is false as specified: `Stamped` is plain data with no unexported constructor or single-door smart constructor stated, so any scalar can be laundered by fabricating `Stamped u (CAEpoch k) x` and feeding it to `rebase`. — The §3 proof is 'by induction over the exported API' and is discharged only for `At` (constructor explicitly not exported); no analogous export discipline is stated for `Stamped`, which derives (Eq, Show) and is presented as ordinary data at rest. `rebase` trusts the stamp unconditionally, so a forged stamp at the scope's epoch yields a well-typed `At b` value from a number that never passed the ingestion boundary. Compare THORP's `ingest`, the one total ingest function mirroring `move`'s smart-constructor discipline (Ledger.hs:204–210). The stated invariant is broken by the code as given; the fix is easy, but the claim as filed is not a theorem.
- [MATERIAL] `arrow` is not total over its declared domain: `CAEpoch` wraps `Int`, negatives are representable, the guard `max n m > length gs` does not exclude them, and `take (m - n) (drop n gs)` with negative n (drop of a negative is the identity) composes the wrong generator segment and returns `Right` with a wrong Iso instead of a typed error. — A wrong arrow returned as Right is a mispricing, not a failure — worse than partiality. The type also violates 'types encode properties': the epoch should be a natural number and monotonicity/contiguity should be unforgeable, yet `CAEpoch (-3)` and `CAEpoch 999` are freely constructible and `Stamped` carries them. The memo itself lists 'totality review of arrow on adversarial epoch inputs' as an open item, i.e., the author concedes the obligation is undischarged; at FORMALIS standards an admitted totality gap on the core composition function is a HIGH-severity finding, and here the failure mode is a silently wrong Right.
- [MATERIAL] The correctness of `arrow` rests on an unstated, unenforced invariant — `length (caChain l u) == usBasis` with generator i mapping epoch i to i+1, contiguously — which the idempotent last-write-wins `SetBasis` and the list-typed `Chain` projection do not guarantee. — Invariants must be stated (FORMALIS Level 2). `SetBasis` is absolute-valued for P6 idempotence on the *counter*, but the chain is 'a PROJECTION of the log' of type `[CAAdj]`: replaying a duplicate `SetBasis (CAEpoch 3)` must not double-append its generator, and a `SetBasis (CAEpoch 5)` arriving over a chain of length 3 creates a gap — neither case is addressed, and `arrow`'s guard and `drop n / take (m-n)` indexing silently presuppose exact contiguity. If chain and counter desynchronize, `arrow` composes the wrong generators and returns Right: a mispricing path guarded by an invariant that appears nowhere in the memo, is not typed, and has no oracle in §10.
- [MATERIAL] Identifier succession is untypeable in the code given: `rebase` rejects `u /= u'` with `WrongUnit`, so a datum stamped on the predecessor unit can never enter a scope opened on the successor, yet `AdjSubst u' r` and the §8 merger row claim exactly that crossing. — After a merger the positions live on u' (C8 paired-issuance moves) and the scope must open on u'; every historical datum is stamped `Stamped u_old e v`, and the first guard of `rebase` returns `Left (WrongUnit u' u_old)` before any arrow is consulted. The per-unit groupoid (epochs of one unit) has no cross-unit arrows, so `AdjSubst`'s 'value part' is a map that no reachable code path can ever apply across the seam it exists for; the promissory note 'the identity part in §8' is never redeemed — §8 restates the claim without a mechanism. NOETHER and NAZAROV both handle this (successor starts at epoch 0 with a declared conversion morphism keyed to usSupersededBy); MILEWSKI's §8 claim 'one fact, two projections' contradicts his own §4.3 code.
- [MATERIAL] The basis coordinate is a single (UnitId, CAEpoch) pair, so derived data whose inputs span multiple units — an index level from constituent spots, a divisor referencing constituent epochs — have no representable basis and no typeable derivation path: `zipAt` requires one skolem b, and b is tied to one unit's `BasisCtx`. — The §3 compositionality claim ('a derived datum's basis is the one basis shared by all its inputs') is only defined within a single unit's epoch lattice; inputs on different units have no shared b by construction, so legitimate multi-unit derivations are unwritable inside the seam and must be computed outside it, untagged — under-enforcement disguised as over-restriction. The design's answer to failure mode (c), 'a CA on the index unit itself,' has no propagation rule: nothing obliges a companion CA on the index unit to be registered atomically with a constituent's CA, and if it is omitted the identity-by-default objection means nothing fails. THORP (joint basis `Map UnitId EpochN`, derivation graph as the propagation edge set) and NAZAROV (epoch vector, D7) both carry the cross-unit case; MILEWSKI is strictly dominated on the exact failure mode the exercise names.
- [MATERIAL] The algebra's axiom that every generator is exactly invertible, combined with C3 atomicity requiring a complete AdjSpec at commit, makes two real CA classes unrepresentable: operators whose parameter is unknown at effectiveness (spin-off before the allocation ratio publishes) and one-way events (merger for cash, non-bijective composition redefinitions). — For the spin-off, the design forces a trilemma: delay the whole CA transaction (positions wrong after effectiveness), commit a guessed AdjSpec (a mispricing committed to the immutable log), or advance the epoch without the generator (no representation exists — `SetBasis` carries the specs). THORP's `Pending SourceRef` shows the case is real and cleanly representable; MILEWSKI cannot express it. For terminal events, the framework 'requires only exact invertibility' — an axiom, not a theorem about the domain — and NAZAROV's D10 demonstrates the domain contains non-invertible morphisms; MILEWSKI offers no terminal or pending escape, so such events either misregister as invertible or fall outside the algebra entirely, and the §6 backward-arrow time-travel guarantee quietly assumes inverses that do not exist for them.
- [MATERIAL] The revised `UnitStatus` retains `usLastSettle :: Maybe Qty` (Ledger.hs:292) unchanged — a basis-relative, price-like cached datum left dimensionally wrong and epoch-unstamped inside the very record the revision touches to add `usBasis`. — A last-settle value cached before a boundary and read after it is consumed one epoch stale with no stamp, no arrow, and no type error — the design's defining defect, surviving inside its own repair, in a field the author edited around. There is no path from `usLastSettle` through `rebase` because it is not `Stamped`; it reaches consumers bare. THORP identifies and fixes exactly this (`Maybe (Price, EpochN)`); MILEWSKI is strictly dominated by a repair that costs one field change he was already making.
- [STYLISTIC] §10's claim that 'path-independence holds again because each endpoint's V is forced single-basis by type' is asserted without the cross-boundary lemma: single-basis endpoints do not by themselves restore the telescope across a CA whose transaction books cash. — The telescoping proof needs value continuity through each boundary up to the explicit booked legs — THORP proves it as the identity (q·f)·((p−c)/f) = q·p − q·c paired with C3 — whereas MILEWSKI offers only a per-CA-class neutrality oracle (§5.5) restricted to value-neutral events; a dividend is not value-neutral and the interior V_s difference across its boundary is left unproved. The conclusion is very likely recoverable, so this is an under-proof (a proof obligation delegated to a test), not an admitted mispricing: stylistic, but at FORMALIS standards a theorem stated as 'regained' must carry its boundary lemma.
- [STYLISTIC] Excluding `AdjScale 0` by a runtime check at CA registration while the stored type `AdjSpec` freely represents it is the same remembered-gate pattern §4.5 rejects for basis checks, applied inconsistently to the operator plane. — Types should prevent invalid states: `interp (AdjScale r) = Iso (*r) (/r)` is total only under an invariant (r ≠ 0) the type does not carry, and the totality claim in §10 ('interp is total because non-invertible specs are excluded at registration') is exactly the shape of argument — correctness by a check that must be remembered at one site — that §4.5 uses to reject the plain runtime epoch check. A single-door check is defensible (it is the spec's `move` idiom), so this is an inconsistency of stated principle rather than a broken invariant: stylistic.
