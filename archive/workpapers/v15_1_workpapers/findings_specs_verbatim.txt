## 0. Standing constraints

Violation of any is a **blocking defect**, not a style note. Each has a named certifier (§1, Bench C) who can veto the freeze.

**C1 — Direction of authority is one-way.** The specification is rated against the Constitution. Where they conflict, the specification is *never* drafted around it and the Constitution is *never* silently edited. The conflict goes to `parking_index.md` with the exact proposed amendment text and the exact clause it replaces. `constitution_v1_2_proposed.tex` is a **proposal**, not an amendment; nothing in v15.1 may cite it except the parking index.

**C2 — Category theory is subordinate.** Every categorical passage is a *second telling*, in a marked box, that removes residual ambiguity for a reader who wants it. **Deleting every box must leave the specification complete and every proof intact.** No concept may be introduced categorically first. No proof may depend on a box. The four existing boxes (§2.8, §3.8, §8.4, §14.1) are the **ceiling: the net count in v15.1 must not exceed four.** Two repairs below (F1's term-adjustment operator, F2's two-axis fold) have clean categorical statements and will tempt you. **Resist.** This is not a category theory article; the boxes exist for clarity and nothing else.

**C3 — Surgical addition.** No reformatting. No scope creep. No backward references to superseded versions. Change what the findings require and nothing else. A diff touching a chapter no finding names is rejected on sight.

**C4 — De-pedantry.** Precision kept, ornament cut. Concrete before abstract. No abstraction unless used twice or unless it makes a statement checkable. Every claim stated so a reader with no prior exposure can check it.

**C5 — Page cap 100, and the cap is not the binding constraint.** There is room. **Never cut a claim, a number, or a design point to save a page.** The repairs will *add* roughly 15pp; the redundancy cuts (F14, F15) recover roughly 11pp. Expect to land in the high seventies. The cuts happen because repetition violates **C4**, not because of budget — this is the one place v15.0 fails its own de-pedantry standard, and it fails it badly.

**C6 — Every guarantee is an executable check.** A repair that adds a property to the invariant catalogue (Ch. 14) **must** add the corresponding property to the test regime (Ch. 15) in the form a generator and a shrinker consume, **and that property must demonstrably fire.** A property defended only in prose is not a repair.

### F1 — BLOCKING — The corporate-action watch is missing from every derivative's product graph
*Chapters: §3.8, §5, §8.6, Ch. 14, Ch. 15. Constitutional impact: none — this **restores** a Constitution §7 claim the spec dropped. Certifiers: JACOBI (G6), ASHWORTH (G8), TALEB (G4), CONCORDIA.*

**The break.** OT-1's declared watch set is exactly one edge guard: OMEGA's official close against the barrier 80.00. Its graph is three nodes, two edges (touch, expire). Now split OMEGA two-for-one. **No edge of OT-1's graph fires.** The §8.5 operator menu is explicitly *value-frame* — a barrier is a **declared term**, not market data, so no operator touches it. The next recorded close prints ≈ 47.50, the guard `close ≤ 80.00` is met, the Event Monitor emits a touch, and **USD 1,000,000 pays out on a phantom knock.**

This is not a missing paragraph. Invariant 3.2(i) says the watch list *equals* the out-edge set of the current node — so with no corporate-action out-edge from `live`, the term adjustment is **unrepresentable**. The type discipline the specification is proudest of is precisely what forbids the fix.

Constitution §7 already has it right: *"when the underlying stock splits, the contract's strike and multiplier are adjusted by appending a new terms version."* **The spec lost it.**

**Required repair.**
1. **§3.8:** every unit whose terms *reference* another unit declares, on **each** lifecycle node, a corporate-action out-edge — guard: a recorded corporate-action event on a referenced underlying; action: append a new ProductTerms version applying the declared **term-adjustment operator**. It is a self-loop: the node is unchanged, the terms version is not.
2. Introduce the **term-adjustment operator** as the exact sibling of the market data operator, acting on the *declared-term* frame rather than the value frame, and subject it to §8.1's **totality**: for every (declared-term kind, event kind) pair an operator is declared — identity where genuinely unaffected, but **declared, not assumed**. An undeclared pairing refuses at the door. Reuse the `Dim` machinery: a barrier and a strike are `Price`-dimensioned and halve; a participation rate is `Proportional` and stands; a multiplier is `Quantity`-dimensioned and **doubles**. Note the direction — and note that this is the **one** place a `Quantity`-dimensioned thing *is* carried by an operator, unlike the balance plane where only a move may change quantity. **Say why the wall differs: a multiplier is a term, not mass.** Do not let this pass without the sentence; an unexplained exception is what rots a design.
3. **§8.6:** extend the invariance witness to derivatives. For a *holding*, the witness is Q × P invariant across the event, bound by move-and-operator. For a *derivative* there is no move, so the witness is over **declared terms**: the payoff on the post-event frame equals the payoff on the pre-event frame. Work it on OT-1 under the OMEGA split — barrier 80.00 → 40.00; payout USD 1,000,000 **unchanged** (a fixed cash payout is neither `Price` nor `Proportional` but a **declared absolute** — register it as such, and say so); post-split close 47.50 does **not** breach 40.00. **Show the phantom the witness refuses: 47.50 against the stale 80.00.**
4. **Ch. 14:** catalogue the witness in both readings. **Ch. 15:** `prop_termAdjustmentInvariance` — generate a corporate action on the underlying of a generated derivative; assert payoff invariance, and assert **no spurious guard fires**.

**CARTAN — run the same attack on the other threads.** VS-1's 252 fixings are against IDX closes; an index constituent split moves the *divisor*, not the level — so is VS-1 genuinely unaffected? If so the operator is the **identity**, and §8.1 requires that it be **declared**. FUT-IDX's multiplier 10 — same question. Both must appear as declared identity pairings or as real adjustments. **Silence is the defect.**

### F2 — BLOCKING — Bitemporality collides with the single fold
*Chapters: Ch. 2, §5.5, Ch. 6, §8.8 (W2), Ch. 14, §15.4. Constitutional impact: **an amendment to Constitution §12. Park it.** Certifiers: CONCORDIA (mandatory), TALEB (G4).*

**The break.** The spec tells two incompatible stories about what a home is.

- §6.1, Principle 6.4, Theorem 14.5: each home is a fold of **the log**, in **log order**; re-folding a prefix reproduces it **bit for bit**.
- §5.5, §6.5, §15.4: the total order for a unit's fixings is the **observation index k**, not log order; a late print is inserted at position k and the tail A_k, A_{k+1}, … is **recomputed from k forward**.

Different sequences, different folds. They disagree the instant a print is late. Three consequences:

**(a) Theorem 14.4 is ambiguous.** "The ledger as of t is the fold of the prefix up to t" — as of t, does A show the pre-insertion or the post-insertion value? §8.8's W2 says **both** must be queryable ("as known at t" and "with corrections through t′"). **W2 is right.** Chapter 14 is written as though there were one axis.

**(b) It is the very thing §2.8 condemns.** "Reinterpreting history through new code is a feature" of event sourcing, and the spec's boast is that it does no such thing. Recomputing a **committed** accrual tail because new information landed **is** reinterpretation of committed history — by data rather than by code, which is better, but not innocent, and "the fold is over the ordered sequence" does not make the problem disappear.

**(c) The recomputation is never proved deterministic.** Theorem 14.5 proves bit-for-bit replay of a **log-order** fold — which is not the fold that runs.

**Required repair (KLEPPMANN leads; FORMALIS arbitrates soundness; CONCORDIA certifies).**
1. Make both axes **explicit and primitive**. Every projection is parameterised by a pair: **valid time** (which observations are in force — the observation-index order) and **transaction time** (the log prefix — what was known). Write it once, in the Ch. 2 typed picture — `project :: LogPrefix -> KnowledgeHorizon -> View` — and let the whole document use it.
2. Restate **Theorem 14.4** over the pair. As-of-t / as-known-at-t is the "as it stood" view; as-of-t / as-known-at-now is the corrected view. **Both are folds; both are deterministic; neither rewrites the other.** This is what W2 has been asserting since Chapter 8 with no machinery behind it.
3. Restate **Theorem 14.5** as determinism over *both* axes, and prove the recomputation case: re-folding in observation-index order over a prefix that includes the late insertion is a function of that prefix alone.
4. **§2.8:** rewrite the "not event sourcing" paragraph to say what is *true*. Committed transactions are never reinterpreted; a **superseded** figure is corrected by a compensating transaction that names it. The accrual tail is not silently rewritten — it is recomputed **and the difference booked as compensating transactions**, exactly as §6.5 already says for the downstream CCP-cash figures. Say it once, plainly. Drop the hand-wave.
5. **§15.4:** `prop_bitemporalDeterminism` — generate a history and a late insertion; assert (i) the as-known-at view is **unchanged**, (ii) the as-of view **recomputes bit-exactly**, (iii) **every** superseded downstream figure has a compensating transaction and **none is left standing**.

**Constitutional impact.** Constitution §12 says every historical state is reconstructible "by replaying the log up to the desired **timestamp**" — one timestamp, one axis. If the repair is sound, §12 needs two. **Park the amendment with exact text.**

### F3 — BLOCKING — `SettlementState` has no home; homes-sufficiency fails
*Chapters: Ch. 6, §9.3, Ch. 11, §17.3. Constitutional impact: none — the repair is Constitution §6 applied. Certifiers: CONCORDIA, ASHWORTH (G8), MATTHIAS-β (G9).*

**The break.** Ch. 11 introduces `data SettlementState = Instructed | Settled | Failed` and calls it *"a correctness input, not a presentation dimension."* But it is a property of an **instruction** — and an instruction is neither a unit nor a (wallet, unit) pair. Several instructions on one (wallet, unit) can be open at once, so PositionState's key **cannot** hold it. **Principle 6.1 (one fact, one home) and Principle 6.3 (the homes suffice) are both violated — by the specification's own Chapter 11.**

**Required repair.** The answer is already in §5.6: *every right and obligation the legal contract creates is a unit.* **An instructed-but-unsettled trade is an obligation** — a DvP delivery-and-payment obligation.

1. **Make it a unit.** Its lifecycle node (`instructed | settled | failed`) lives in **its own UnitStatus**, which is exactly a home. Sufficiency restored; no new machinery; no fourth home.
2. **Collapse three half-specified mechanisms into one graph.** The **market claim** (§11.3), the **fails-cascade obligation** (§9.3), and the settlement obligation are nodes and edges of **one product graph** on the settlement-obligation unit. §9.3 currently mints the fails obligation *"symmetric to the market-claim unit"* without saying it **is** the same unit at a different node. Say it.
3. **Delete** `data SettlementState` as a free-standing type. Proposition 11.1's "recorded settlement state" reads the settlement-obligation unit's UnitStatus, like any other fact.
4. This **discharges an open problem** §17.3 currently names ("the fails-cascade obligation's full valuation and lifecycle"). It moves from the open-problems index into the body. **Update §17.3.**

### F6 — MAJOR — PositionState's key cannot carry the facts the episodes put in it
*Chapters: §6.1, §5.1, §5.3. Constitutional impact: none. Certifiers: CONCORDIA, ASHWORTH (G8).*

`positionState :: (Wallet, Unit) -> PosFacts` is **one slot per pair**. But:
**(a)** two dividends in flight on ACME with different payment dates need **two live entitlements**;
**(b)** a futures position with a new lot traded today needs **both** yesterday's settled level **and** the new lot's trade price.

The type sketch quietly promises something the episodes must break. Two admissible repairs — Bench A picks one and defends it:

- **(i)** `PosFacts` is explicitly an **event-keyed collection**, keyed by the cause-derived identifier of the firing that wrote each fact. Say so **in the type**, and state the retirement rule (a fact retires when its obligation discharges).
- **(ii)** For futures, store the aggregate **settled value** Σ qᵢ·levelᵢ as one integer and compute VM as `settleₜ × Q − storedSettledValue`. This is what CCPs actually do, it preserves the key, and the multi-lot case falls out.

**FORMALIS: (ii) alone does not close (a).** Some form of (i) is required regardless. **ASHWORTH: two dividends in flight is not an exotic case, it is a Tuesday.**

### F8 — MAJOR — Proposition 11.1 is not total: the fourth quadrant is missing
*Chapters: §11.3, Ch. 8, §16.3. Constitutional impact: none, but a **new named trust assumption**. Certifiers: CARTAN (G1), ASHWORTH (G8), LEX MANDATUM.*

Four quadrants of (cum / ex) × (settled / unsettled at record date). **You enumerate three.** The missing one: **ex trade, settled before the record date.** The buyer is the registered holder, the owned plane shows the buyer, so determination pays the buyer — who bought **ex** and is **not entitled**. The seller should receive it, via the mirror claim.

In practice an external convention saves you: the ex-date is set relative to the record date and the settlement cycle *precisely so that* an ex trade cannot settle in time to register. But **that convention is nowhere on the record** — and it breaks in exactly the case that matters: a large special dividend under a **due-bills** regime, where the ex-date falls **after** the payment date.

**Required repair — do both.**
- **(a)** Handle the fourth quadrant with the **mirror market-claim unit**. The machinery exists; §11.3 already says *"the construction is symmetric."*
- **(b)** Name the coherence as **TA-EXDATE** in the §16.3 trust-assumption list, alongside TA-KIND and TA-TERMS, in the same four-field shape: owner, violation, detection, residual.

(a) is cheap; (b) is honest, because a due-bills regime violates the assumption **lawfully**. What you may not do is **claim totality on three quadrants**.

### F9 — MAJOR — The price of a settled-to-market future is undefined
*Chapters: Definition 7.1, §7.3, §15.4. Constitutional impact: none. Certifiers: CONCORDIA, JACOBI (G6), ASHWORTH (G8).*

Definition 7.1 sums `owned(w,u) · P(u, σ(u))` over **valued** units. FUT-IDX **is** a valued unit; W-ALPHA holds +1, W-CCP holds −1. If `P(FUT-IDX)` is the futures level, **NAV double-counts against the VM cash already settled** — and the flagship instrument's NAV is **undefined**.

The spec states the analogous rule for OT-1 (triggered ⇒ price 0) and §15.4 even makes it a **safety invariant** (`NoDoubleCount`) — but never states it for the future.

**Required repair.** One sentence in §7.3; one line in the model:

This is §9.5's STM/CTM regime key applied to **valuation**. Note in §7.3 that the regime bit therefore carries a **second** consequence beyond the balance sheet — which sharpens, rather than duplicates, §9.5's warning about its misdeclaration. Extend `NoDoubleCount` in §15.4 to quantify over **all** extinguishing lifecycle events, not just OT-1's trigger.

### F12 — MAJOR — Deposit-neutrality is asserted unconditionally in the Constitution and quietly narrowed in the spec
*Chapters: Constitution §8, Proposition 7.3, §17.3. Constitutional impact: **a real conflict. Park it.** Certifiers: CONCORDIA (mandatory), LEX MANDATUM (G3).*

Constitution §8: *"a deposit cannot move profit and loss — not because a formula subtracts it, but because it cannot change net owned value,"* with case 2 being financing *"against an **equal** obligation."* **Unconditional.**

Proposition 7.3: it is a theorem *"**exactly when** the declared financing rate equals the fair rate,"* and otherwise *"the day-one difference is a **financing basis, not a deposit**."*

## 4. PARETO convergence, per package

1. **Bench A drafts.** Named owner writes; KARPATHY holds the pen; the diff is surgical (C3).
2. **Bench B critiques, independently.** No critic sees another's. Each critique states: the claim attacked, the **counterexample or the named missing case**, and the severity. **A critique with neither is discarded before voting.**
3. **Bench A votes** each critique **valid** or **invalid**, with a reason. Valid → repaired next round. Invalid → **answered** in `convergence_log.md`. Answered, not ignored.
4. **Stopping rule.** Minimum **3** rounds, maximum **7**. **Early stop when ≥ 75% of a round's critiques are voted invalid by ≥ 75% of Bench A.**
5. At round 7 without convergence, the residue is **parked** as a named open problem with the exact disagreement recorded. Not resolved by fiat. Not escalated.
6. **Escalation is forbidden.** Every question that would go to the owner goes to `parking_index.md` instead, with exact proposed amendment text. **The run does not stop. The run does not ask.**

**Anti-vacuity (TALEB certifies).** A property whose precondition is never generated **passes without witnessing anything**. Every property added under C6 must be shown to **fire**: report, per property, how many generated histories reach its precondition. **Zero is a blocking defect, not a green test.** The specification says exactly this about its own generators (§15.2) — hold the repairs to it.

## 5. Certification, and the only way this run ends

**PARETO convergence is necessary and not sufficient.** A converged package goes to **Bench C**.

1. Each certifier examines **only their objective**, against the **whole document** — not the diff.
2. A certifier returns **CERTIFIED** or **VETOED**. A veto **must** state the objective missed and the evidence. **A veto without evidence is void.**
3. **A veto reopens the package** and **resets its PARETO round count**. Bench A repairs; Bench B re-critiques; the package returns to certification.
4. **The run freezes when, and only when, every certifier has returned CERTIFIED — and CONCORDIA has returned CERTIFIED on the whole document, at the end, after every other signature is in.** Hers is the last one taken and the only one that cannot be worked around.
5. **Deadlock, and how it terminates without the owner.** If a package is vetoed on **three** full certification cycles, the residue may be **parked** — but parking is admissible **only if the vetoing certifier and CONCORDIA both sign the parking entry.** If they do, the veto is discharged and the entry stands as a named open problem with the dissent recorded **verbatim**. If they do not, the cycle continues. **There is no third exit, and there is no exit through the owner.**
6. `certification_record.md` logs every signature, every veto, every veto's evidence, and every discharged veto with its parking entry. It is the run's audit trail, and it is held to the standard of the ledger it describes: **nothing is silently dropped.**
