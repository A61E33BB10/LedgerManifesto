# Phase 1 Bench Consultation — SBL Specialist (for MINSKY)

## 1. Answer to the question asked

**Headline.** The coordinate vector never has to carry a distinction it cannot — *provided each legal regime is mapped to its natural coordinates rather than forced through a shared one*. The failure mode in v13.1/v14 is not the vector; it is treating "cash collateral" as one thing and reading §8's `coll_recv` clause as universal. The load-bearing axis in securities financing is **title transfer vs. security interest (pledge)**, not cash vs. securities. Within a regime, cash and non-cash behave identically; across regimes they diverge. Fix the axis and the rest follows.

**(i) Cash VM on `own` (v13.1/v14) vs. §8's `coll_recv` — reconcilable, but only regime-conditionally.** §8's parenthetical ("cash received as collateral, recorded on the collateral-received coordinate") is *correct for a pledge and wrong for title transfer*. Under a **pledge** (2018 GMSLA Security Interest over Collateral; NY-law pledge CSA) the giver retains ownership; the taker holds a lien and value it does not own — §8 category 3, custody, `coll_recv`. Under **title transfer** (standard GMSLA 2000/2010; GMRA; English-law VM CSA — the *dominant* regime in SBL, repo, and variation margin) the taker takes full legal and beneficial ownership of the cash and owes an equivalent-return debt — §8 category 2, *financing received against an equal obligation created in the same transaction*. Title-transfer cash is genuinely owned; it must sit on `own`, with the return leg as its own obligation unit.

So neither prior spec is simply wrong: v13.1/v14 modelled the title-transfer case, the constitution stated the pledge case. **v15 must refine §8's clause to be regime-conditional**, not overturn either. If instead v15 forces *all* cash collateral onto `coll_recv` (literal §8), three things break under title transfer: (a) reinvestment return on the cash is real *owned* P&L, but §4 says `coll_recv` drives no PnL — you bolt on an exception; (b) `coll_recv` implies custody/segregation, misstating the balance sheet (title-transfer collateral is on-balance-sheet for the taker) and the credit risk (the giver is an *unsecured creditor*, not a beneficiary of segregated property); (c) rehypothecation machinery would be invoked to let a party spend its own cash. Conversely, forcing pledge collateral onto `own` books ownership of assets the taker does not own, inflates its NAV, and destroys the giver's *proprietary* (in rem) recovery on insolvency.

**(ii) Yes — title transfer forces ownership re-booking, and the return claim stays faithful because it is a unit (§6).** Poster: `own(asset) −Q`; gains a **claim-for-return-of-equivalent** unit. Taker: `own(asset) +Q`; gains the mirror **return-obligation** unit (one bilateral unit, two sides). Conservation holds at inception: the poster swaps owned asset for an equal-valued claim unit → net owned unchanged → no PnL (§8 cat. 2). For non-cash, the claim is *for equivalent securities* and is priced through the ordinary pricing layer, so the poster stays economically long the asset via the claim while the taker owns the specific asset — the in rem/in personam distinction is carried by *which* object holds the mass (own-position vs. claim-unit), not by a flag. Title-transfer collateral therefore needs **no collateral coordinate at all**; `coll_post`/`coll_recv` are for the pledge case, where the physical-action test (§4) is satisfied because posting changes possession *without* changing ownership. This also dissolves the brief's valuation tension: under a pledge `V = Σ(own+coll_post)·P` is right because **`coll_post ⊂ owned`** (encumbered-owned — the poster still owns); `coll_recv ⊄ owned` (custody). "Only owned drives PnL" holds exactly.

**(iii) Micro-case (b): bond posted as repo collateral pays a coupon.** Repo is title transfer (GMRA), so I work that as primary and give the pledge variant.

*Title transfer (GMRA — the real repo):* At inception A delivers bond B (Q units) to Cpty for cash; A holds a claim-for-equivalent-B (stays long B) + a cash-repurchase obligation; Cpty holds `own(B) +Q` + a delivery obligation. Coupon C·Q is paid by the issuer to the **current legal owner = Cpty**: `own(cash, Cpty) += C·Q`. GMRA para 5 (Income) then fires the **manufactured-payment** unit: move `own(cash) Cpty −C·Q → A own(cash) +C·Q`. Net: A retains the coupon though it does not own the bond. Here the manufactured leg is *economically load-bearing* — it is the only thing routing the coupon back to A.

*Pledge (security-interest variant):* B never leaves A's ownership — `own(B, A)=Q`, `coll_post=Q`; Cpty holds `coll_recv +Q`, unowned. The coupon follows the *owned* coordinate, which never left A: `own(cash, A) += C·Q` directly, **no manufactured leg** where collateral sits in A's name/segregated. A manufactured leg appears only if Cpty is record-holder or has rehypothecated — then it is plumbing (routing record-holder cash back to the owner), not economic re-homing.

**One rule, no special case:** the coupon *always* hits the owner; the manufactured-payment obligation is *always* its own unit fired by the income event; it is merely null when owner and economic beneficiary coincide (pledge, coupon to A). That is exactly the trap-case (c) discipline — entitlements follow `owned`, regardless of possession.

## 2. Domain constraint the ruling must respect

- **GMSLA (2000/2010) and GMRA are title-transfer** master agreements: absolute transfer of title to both the lent/purchased securities *and* the collateral, with an obligation to redeliver *equivalent* assets. The **2018 GMSLA Security Interest over Collateral** is the pledge variant. Regime is a **declared term of the collateral-agreement unit**, not a per-position flag — consistent with Q2's "economics is declared data." Coordinate placement is then *derived* from the agreement, and `legal_regime` becomes a projection (readable from position structure: `coll_recv` ⇒ pledge; `own`+return-unit ⇒ title transfer), not stored redundantly.
- **Manufactured payments** (GMSLA para 6 / GMRA para 5) and all return/repurchase obligations must be §6 units. The coordinate vector alone is necessary but *insufficient*: title transfer is unrepresentable without the obligation/claim-unit layer. That is by design.
- **Rehypothecation-permitted pledge** (SFTR Art. 15 re-use) is the genuine middle case the five-coordinate v14 model cannot carry: the taker does not own but may re-use. It needs the **`coll_rehyp`** coordinate (`coll_recv ↓, coll_rehyp ↑`) to distinguish "hold the specific asset" from "re-used it, owe equivalent." This is the one collateral coordinate beyond post/recv that must exist.

## 3. Risk in answering Q1/Q2 "yes"

- **Q1 risk:** "Cash follows the generalised coordinates exactly as securities" is *false as stated* and, if adopted literally, re-introduces the §8 conflict. It is true only *within a regime*. State the split as **title-transfer vs. pledge**, under each of which cash and non-cash are identical. The cash/securities framing is a red herring that will mis-cue implementers into a universal `coll_recv`.
- **Q2 risk:** Architecturally sound and my domain adds no obstacle — options/floored portfolios as collateral are eligibility+haircut = declared data. Two watch-items: (a) title-transfer collateral must *not* be modelled as mass on a collateral coordinate, or the "degenerate scalar = all mass on owned" story silently breaks (a title-transfer poster's asset genuinely leaves `own`); (b) **portfolio-floor valuation** (put overlay lowering the haircut) requires valuing the *package*, which means the collateral-agreement unit must reference a *set* of units with a joint payoff — ensure the pricing layer can price the pledged package, not only its lines, or the floor is lost.

## 4. Recommendation

Adopt the six-coordinate vector `(own, onloan, borr, coll_post, coll_recv, coll_rehyp)`, but rule that **coordinate placement is regime-driven and regime is declared once on the collateral-agreement unit, never stored on the position**: *title transfer* → ownership re-booking (`own` moves at both parties) plus a §6 return/repurchase obligation unit and, for income, a manufactured-payment unit; *pledge* → ownership retained by the poster with `coll_post ⊂ owned` at the giver and unowned `coll_recv` at the taker; *rehypothecated pledge* → `coll_rehyp`. Refine §8's parenthetical to read that cash collateral sits on `coll_recv` **under a security interest** and on `own` against a return obligation **under title transfer** — this keeps §4 ("only owned drives PnL"), §6 (every right is a unit), and §8 (the three inflow categories, cat. 2 vs. cat. 3) all simultaneously true, with no special case and no stored `legal_regime` modifier. Treat Q2 as yes; the only domain caveat is that the collateral coordinates carry owned mass under a pledge but never under title transfer, and the pricing layer must value pledged *packages*.
