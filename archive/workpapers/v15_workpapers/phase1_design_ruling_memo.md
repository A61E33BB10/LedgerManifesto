# Phase 1 Design Ruling — The Collateral Generalisation (Q1/Q2)

**To:** R. Delloye, project owner — for ratification
**From:** MINSKY, roster author for collateral, margin, and securities lending
**Date:** 2026-07-11 (rev. 2, incorporating FORMALIS arbitration R1–R8 and the TALEB attack)
**Authority:** Rated one-way against the Ledger Framework Constitution v1.0. Three constitutional sentences require amendment; the exact diffs are section 6. The bench consultations, the FORMALIS arbitration, and the TALEB attack are logged in the annex.

## 1. The ruling

v15 adopts one universal position representation. Every (wallet, unit) balance is a coordinate vector; the outright scalar is the degenerate case with all mass on owned; any unit — cash, bond, structured note, listed option — may sit on the collateral coordinates; eligibility and haircuts are declared terms of the collateral-agreement unit, checked at the single door, never typed. That answers Q2 yes. Q1 is answered yes in the only form that is true: cash shares the coordinate vector exactly as securities do, and for both, the coordinate an inflow writes is decided by the legal regime declared once on the governing agreement unit — never by asset class. Title transfer writes owned plus an obligation unit and touches no collateral coordinate; security interest writes the collateral coordinates as marker mass and never touches owned; settled-to-market margin writes owned with no obligation at all. Entitlement **determination** always reads the owned plane; entitlement **payment** routes through a conditional obligation unit carrying the agreement's declared trapping terms — under pledge as under title transfer. Valuation of pledged collateral is line-level by default; profit and loss is Σ owned·P with no regime branch, so constitution §4's sentence "only the owned coordinate carries economic value" holds literally and becomes an executable property.

## 2. The options

Three designs are genuine; none is a strawman.

**A — the split model (v13.1 §16, v14).** Cash collateral writes owned with the return obligation held in loan-unit state; securities collateral writes the collateral coordinates; a stored `legal_regime` field switches the poster's valuation between Σ own·P and Σ (own+coll_post)·P. It shipped twice and treasury can run it. Its costs: one fact-pattern in two representations, a valuation branch that contradicts §4 as written, and a stratified generator universe in which the trap case — an option pledged as collateral — is reachable only by a third hand-written generator family.

**B — the literal-§8 universal model.** Everything received as collateral, cash included, writes coll_recv; posting decrements owned. Fully uniform and conformant to §8 as printed. It mis-books settled-to-market margin as custody, asserting a return obligation that does not legally exist; it parks cash the receiver genuinely owns on a coordinate defined to carry no value; and because cash is fungible — the nostro observes only the sum — the coll_recv(cash) balance and the bank statement diverge intraday: the exact internal reconciliation failure the constitution exists to abolish. It also mis-routes the trap case: with owned decremented, the knock event finds no owner, or pays the taker, who does not own.

**C — the regime-keyed universal model.** One representation, placement decided by the declared regime, every claim and obligation a unit, determination and payment kept distinct. This is the ruling. Its price is three amended constitutional sentences.

## 3. The design

**Coordinates.** The stored basis per (wallet, unit) is the signed vector **(owned, lent, posted)**, each non-owned component decomposed by agreement reference. The five constitutional names are ray projections of it: owned = owned; lent out = lent⁺; borrowed = lent⁻; posted as collateral = posted⁺; received as collateral = posted⁻. Rehypothecation is not a sixth coordinate. Re-use of pledge-form collateral is a posting of mass held on the received ray, and every re-pledge move carries a **source-agreement reference** beside its governing reference — the source is an observable fact of the custody instruction, so nothing fictitious is stored — which makes pledge-form re-use project as per-(unit, agreement) actuals: SFTR Article 15, the receiver's FINREP F32.02 re-used/available split, and IFRS 7 §15 all read off it. Title-transfer received securities land on owned, commingled with proprietary holdings of the same ISIN, so their re-use projects by the same declared convention as cash (D1) — the fungibility argument does not distinguish cash from fungible securities on the same coordinate, and the ruling says so rather than overclaiming. Everything computable from the coordinates — avail, encumbered, possess, re-used — is a projection, computed when needed and never stored (§4).

**Moves and the two guards.** A move writes exactly one coordinate of one unit, positive quantity, paired legs: the same coordinate debited at the source and credited at the destination. A move whose coordinate is not owned carries a mandatory reference to its collateral-agreement unit; orphan collateral mass is not refused — it is unwritable. Conservation is stated per (unit, coordinate): **for every unit u, every coordinate c, and every log position, Σ over all wallets of bal_c(w, u) = 0** — the sum running over every wallet, virtual counterparty and issuer wallets included, because the system is closed. Issuance is the first paired-leg owned move from the issuing wallet; retirement is the log position after which every wallet's vector for u is zero. The proof is induction on the log: every plane is born empty, and every balance write is a paired-leg move. The per-unit summed law is a corollary, never the primitive — it alone would license cross-coordinate rebookings that fabricate custody while conserving the sum.

Conservation alone is too weak: it admits a wallet posting collateral it neither owns nor holds. The **coverage invariant** closes this, checked at the door on every admission, per (wallet, unit):

> Σ_G posted_G(w, u) ≤ max(owned(w, u), 0) — net over agreements, the received ray entering with positive sign — and the analogue on the lent plane. (Corrected per FORMALIS certification, DEFECT 9: the bound binds only the posting direction, so a negative-owned wallet with nothing posted — the issuer wallet conservation itself creates, or a plain short — is admissible.)

Delivered collateral is thereby bounded by possession (a rehypothecation chain passes: received −100, re-posted +60 nets to coverage 40), and a security-interest marker over assets that stay in the pledgor's custody is bounded by owned; a short poster with owned = −5,000 attempting to post 5,000 is refused at the door. The two guards must never be confused: **value sufficiency** (collateral ≥ exposure) is lawfully false intraday and is therefore an *obligation* — deadline, discharge predicate, close-out as compensation; **possession coverage** is never lawfully false, because a transaction is atomic and possession is instantaneous within it — it is therefore an *invariant*. v13.1's avail-identity and locate-drawdown guards (P14, P19) return in this single predicate.

**Entitlements: determination and payment are distinct.** The entitlement *determination* always reads the owned plane — that rule survived every attack and beat both strawmen. The entitlement *payment* routes through a **conditional obligation unit** created by the determination, whose discharge predicate carries the governing agreement's declared trapping terms — the sufficiency-and-default conditions of CSA Paragraph 5(c) / 6(d)(i) and their GMRA/GMSLA analogues — under pledge as well as title transfer. Where no agreement encumbers the owner's mass, the predicate is vacuously true and the obligation discharges at once: in the ordinary state the *condition* vanishes, never the *leg*. An unconditional issuer-to-owned pass-through is forbidden: the real agreements trap distributions exactly when a delivery amount or default exists, and §6 faithfulness binds the ledger to that.

**D1 — received title-transfer cash: owned plus a return-obligation unit.** From §4's own admission test: a quantity earns a coordinate only when a distinct real-world action can change that coordinate independently of ownership — and taking cash under title transfer *is* the change of ownership, so it earns no collateral coordinate. The receiver owns the cash, reinvests it, bears the result, and owes an equivalent-return debt: §8's second case, the obligation itself a unit (§6), **valued at the inflow amount — par plus accrued at the declared rate — so that net owned value cannot move at receipt and §8's deposit-neutrality sentence holds**. GMRA, GMSLA and the English-law CSA are title-transfer instruments; IFRS 9 has the receiver recognise the cash and the liability. SFTR Table 4 reinvestment (fields 4.4–4.7) projects from the return-obligation units per agreement, the reinvestment book, and an attribution convention declared once as data. The rationale is structural, not an appeal to blessing: the Table 4 reinvestment fields are **single-sided, entity-aggregate, and excluded from trade-repository reconciliation — there is no second side to break against** — and ESMA's guidance itself permits only a best-efforts estimate for commingled cash. The per-dollar "actual" a coll_recv(cash) coordinate would report is rejected: no bank statement can confirm which dollar moved; it is a stored attribution fiction. The genuinely *reconciled* surface is elsewhere and is named in section 5: SFTR field 2.73, collateralisation of net exposure, which must be sourced from the governing agreement consistently with the counterparty.

**D2 — title-transfer securities: owned re-books to the taker, at instruction.** The poster's balance loses the asset from owned and gains, in the same transaction, a **claim-for-equivalent** unit plus the repurchase or redelivery obligation unit. Derivation: owned must be the coordinate the move machinery can act on — the taker may lawfully sell outright; keeping the asset on the poster's owned makes that sale, and the coupon routing of micro-case (b), special constructors, and makes the legally load-bearing GMRA paragraph 5 manufactured payment unrepresentable. Three rules complete the shape, each a correctness requirement.

*Pricing.* The claim-for-equivalent is priced through the ordinary pricing layer **parameterised — as §8 valuation already is — by state, here the taker's**: while the taker performs, the claim's value tracks the referenced asset (the accrued redelivery value); it bears the taker's credit; and on the taker's default it decouples from the referenced asset and becomes a recovery claim on the taker's estate. It is *not* "priced identically to the asset" — that rule would mark the poster long at par on the one day the mark exists for.

*Identity.* Claims are **per-agreement — per netting set — units.** Close-out nets per master agreement and never across masters; and since owned moves carry no agreement reference, the claim's netting-set identity cannot live on a move — it lives in the unit's own declared terms, which name both the referenced asset and the governing agreement. The unit-registry growth this implies is accepted as the price of correct close-out; the counterparty-state pricing above is then natural, because the claim *is* a per-counterparty object.

*Timing.* Owned re-books **at instruction** (trade-date booking, as v14 already did), and the settlement gap is carried explicitly: where a record date falls between instruction and settlement, the CSD pays the record holder — still the deliverer — while the ledger's owned shows the buyer, and the reconciling leg is a **market-claim obligation unit** created over the instruction-to-settlement window, per the corporate-action standards. The market claim is a first-class recorded instrument, not a break; settlement-state is thereby load-bearing for entitlement routing — a correctness requirement of this ruling, not a deferred reporting axis.

The banking-auditor's objection — IFRS 9 §3.2.6 forbids derecognition — is overruled on the coordinate and satisfied on the report: the IFRS 7 §42D presentation is a projection, declared on the agreement regime, mapping claim-for-equivalent-under-financing to the transferred asset at fair value and the repurchase obligation to its associated liability — complete within the Ledger's fair-value scope. FINREP encumbrance is recovered the same way: under pledge, encumbered = posted⁺ of owned units (F32.01); under title-transfer financing, encumbered = the claims held under financing agreements. The receiver's templates (F32.02, IFRS 7 §15) are recovered per the Coordinates paragraph: source-tagged actuals for pledge-form re-use, the declared convention for title-transfer received fungibles. No second store: every projection reads coordinates, units, and declared terms alone.

**D3 — posting under pledge: marker mass; owned is never decremented.** The correctness-architect's mechanics are adopted and v13.1's rejected. A pledge posting is one move on the posted plane — poster +Q, taker −Q (projected as received) — admissible only under the coverage invariant, and owned is untouched, which is the legal fact: the pledgor still owns. Since posting never writes owned, V = Σ owned·P is unchanged by any post-and-return round trip: §4's "only owned drives PnL" becomes a machine-checkable metamorphic property — **insert a post-and-return pair within any watch-free interval of the referenced agreement and every later valuation is identical**. The side condition is real: an insertion spanning the agreement's valuation watch lawfully changes later firings and cash; the property owns the move algebra, and the watched case is owned by the TLA+ model of section 5. The stored `legal_regime` valuation switch is deleted; regime lives in the transaction shape (D1/D2) and the agreement unit's declared terms.

**D4 — line valuation, mandated as the default.** Collateral value under an agreement = Σ per-line market value × the declared valuation percentage, per CSA Paragraph 13's actual shape; §6 binds the ledger to what the agreements declare, and none today declares a floored package. The architecture *permits* an agreement whose declared terms define package-level valuation through the ordinary pricing layer — the Q2 principle applied to valuation — and a portfolio floor, where computed, is a projection, never a stored valuation and never a haircut the agreement does not grant.

**D5 — cash margin is four cases, keyed by declared agreement terms, and the key is fallible.** Settled-to-market variation margin is **settlement** — §8 case 1, an owned move extinguishing the day's exposure, no return obligation because none exists in law. Collateralised-to-market and title-transfer CSA cash is **financing** — §8 case 2, an owned move plus a return-obligation unit accruing the declared rate. Security-interest cash **without right of use** (segregated UMR initial margin) is **custody** — §8 case 3, a posted-plane move; fungibility raises no objection because the segregated account reconciles one-for-one against the marker. Security-interest cash **with right of use** — the New York-law CSA — follows **case 2 upon exercise of the right, and for cash, receipt into the receiver's own accounts is the exercise**: commingled right-of-use cash books as financing, never as custody. The enumeration is total.

The regime is one declared bit carrying the largest balance-sheet consequence in the design, and its misdeclaration is **a class of error §13 recomputation cannot catch**: recompute reproduces the declared bit and faithfully reproduces the wrong answer. Detection is boundary reconciliation against the counterparty's or CCP's margin statement — nothing internal. The repair path is defined now: a mid-life regime change (the CCPs' 2017 CTM-to-STM flips are the precedent) is a **ProductTerms amendment** — a new terms version — **plus compensating transactions** rebooking the obligation units and accruals of the affected interval. Both, not one. Stated openly in section 5.

Two disciplines complete the design. **Lifecycle events extinguish value, never mass**: a knock, a default, an expiry changes UnitStatus and price, never a coordinate balance; a unit retires only from the zero vector, the agreement's machinery clearing the marker planes first — with the supervised write-off path of section 5 as the exit for units no voluntary machinery will bury. **Sufficiency is an obligation; coverage is an invariant** — the two-guard contrast stated above, never collapsed into one word.

## 4. The three micro-cases, worked

**(a) Daily cash VM on a running future (settled-to-market).** Long one contract, multiplier 10; settlement prints 1,000 then 990. The Event Monitor emits the settlement-print event; the future's contract fires and proposes one transaction: move owned(USD) 100 from my wallet to the CCP wallet, plus the PositionState update recording the margin applied and the settlement level. No collateral coordinate, no obligation unit — the payment settles the day's mark and the contract resets. My NAV falls exactly 100, the day's loss; on unwind nothing is returned, which is correct. Had the agreement declared CTM, the identical cash move would be paired with the return-obligation unit valued at 100 — one declared term and one unit of difference, not a different machine. No special case.

**(b) A bond posted as repo collateral pays a coupon while pledged.** 10,000 bonds, coupon 1.25 per bond = 12,500.

*Title-transfer form (GMRA — the real repo).* At inception, booked at instruction: A's owned(bond) −10,000 re-booked to B; A holds the per-agreement claim-for-equivalent +10,000 and B the repurchase obligation. On the coupon date the Monitor emits the coupon event; the bond's contract — possession-blind and regime-blind — determines the entitlement from the owned plane: B. The issuer's payment moves owned(cash) 12,500 issuer → B. The same recorded event fires the repo agreement's contract (its income watch was declared at inception): it creates the manufactured-payment obligation — B owes A 12,500 — whose discharge predicate carries the agreement's declared conditions (no event of default, no undischarged margin deficiency); in the ordinary state it discharges at once by the move owned(cash) 12,500 B → A. A's cash offsets the coupon-drop in the claim's price, so A's PnL is exactly a bondholder's — *while B performs*, which is precisely what the claim's counterparty-state pricing says. Had the record date fallen inside an instruction-to-settlement gap, the market-claim obligation of D2 carries the reconciling leg — recorded, deadline-bearing, no break.

*Pledge variant.* The bond never left A's owned: A owned +10,000, posted +10,000 under G; B posted −10,000. The coupon event fires the same bond contract, which determines the entitlement from the owned plane: A. The payment routes through the conditional distribution obligation under G — the discharge predicate is Paragraph 6(d)(i)'s: no default, and no delivery amount created or increased by the release. Collateral sufficient, no default: the predicate holds and the obligation discharges at once, owned(cash) 12,500 issuer → A. The condition vanishes in the ordinary state; the leg never does — which is exactly where micro-case (c)'s trap will hang.

One rule covers both regimes: determination reads owned; payment discharges through the agreement-conditioned obligation, which is trivially open when nothing traps it. No special case, and no unconditional pass-through.

**(c) The one-touch knocks while pledged.** A owns one one-touch O paying 1,000,000 on touch, priced 400,000; pledged security-interest to B under agreement G at a 50% valuation percentage: A posted +1, B posted −1; collateral value to B = 200,000 against exposure 150,000.

1. The low print is recorded; the Monitor emits the touch. The knock is now **on the record**; no ledger state has changed — the Monitor emits events and cannot write state.
2. O's contract fires on the event; the Transaction Executor admits its proposal: UnitStatus → triggered, the entitlement **determined** from the owned plane — A — and the conditional payment obligation created: the writer owes A 1,000,000, discharge predicate carrying G's declared trapping terms. Mass on the posted plane is unchanged. A duplicate emission finds the unit triggered and proposes nothing.
3. The pricing layer, state-parameterised, prices triggered O at 0. Value has collapsed; mass is intact — coordinates carry mass, prices carry value.
4. G's contract, at its declared valuation watch, values B's received projection: 0. Delivery amount 150,000; it fires a margin-call obligation with a deadline.
5. The payment obligation discharges under its predicate — Paragraph 6(d)(i): release only to the extent no delivery amount is created. One transaction, two moves: owned(cash) 1,000,000 writer → A, and posted(cash) 150,000 A → B under G, delivered as credit support and discharging the call; 850,000 stays free with A. Coverage holds within the transaction (A's owned cash 1,000,000 ≥ posted 150,000). Had G declared no trapping terms, the predicate is vacuous and the full payout releases; an agreement declaring proceeds attachment would have G's contract post the cash instead — declared terms decide, which is Q2 applied. The naive design — unconditional pass-through to A — would leak 1,000,000 to a possibly distressed poster that the agreement withholds; that sentence is deleted from this ruling.
6. The pledge closes: the marker return clears the posted plane of O (B/A ±1 → 0); then the extinguishment move owned(O) A → writer takes every wallet's vector for O to zero — **the ordering is forced by the coverage invariant**, which refuses extinguishing owned while posted mass remains; then O retires at the zero vector.

No special case in the move-and-coordinate algebra. The residuals are named, not hidden: the knock–revalue–call–discharge ordering is temporal and carries the TLA+ obligation of section 5; and if no substitution or return machinery ever runs — a worthless unit nobody will bury — the supervised write-off path of section 5 is the exit. Between steps 2 and 5 the book is lawfully under-collateralised: sufficiency is an obligation, and the deadline covers it.

## 5. Trade-offs and named obligations

- **Three constitutional amendments are required** (section 6); the design contradicts §8's parenthetical and §4's list sentence as printed. Ratification precedes any v15 text that cites them.
- **Cash-reinvestment attribution is a declared convention, and its totality is a binding Phase 2 obligation.** The convention must be a total function of log state: (i) the pool predicate — a declared wallet/move predicate defining "the reinvestment book"; (ii) the period or timestamp basis of the pro-rata ratio; (iii) the rounding rule, remainders booked per §4's remainder discipline. Absent any of the three, two readers compute different Table 4 figures from one log — a defect. The safety rationale is single-sidedness (the fields are entity-aggregate and unreconciled), not regulatory blessing; the genuinely reconciled surface is **SFTR field 2.73 (collateralisation of net exposure), which must be sourced from the governing agreement consistently with the counterparty** — a named obligation. Title-transfer received securities project re-use by the same convention; pledge-form re-use keeps true actuals, given the source-agreement reference on re-pledge moves.
- **A presentation projection must be built and audited.** IFRS 7 §42D / FINREP F32.01 project from claims and regimes, complete within the fair-value scope; the receiver side (F32.02, IFRS 7 §15) projects from source-tagged markers plus the convention. Phase 2 deliverable with property tests; the auditor bench signs its faithfulness before v15 freezes.
- **The regime bit is load-bearing and unverifiable from inside.** A misdeclared STM/CTM omits or fabricates the returnable-margin liability and its interest accrual, and §13 recomputation cannot catch it — boundary reconciliation against counterparty and CCP statements is the sole detector, and the defined repair is a ProductTerms amendment plus compensating rebooking of the affected interval (D5). Relatedly, under Q2 the eligibility schedule becomes load-bearing for solvency — a haircut calibrated to diffusion will not cover jump-to-zero collateral — and the ledger does not police it; the agreement's declared terms do.
- **Per-netting-set claim units grow the unit registry.** Every financing transaction mints a claim and an obligation unit. Accepted: fungible claims would break netting-set attribution at close-out, which is the one computation the units exist to get right.
- **The five constitutional names become rays of a signed basis.** Interfaces, reports, and specification prose keep the constitutional vocabulary; the basis is a specification-level representation. Documentation burden accepted.
- **Testcommittee conditions bind.** No balance write except through a single-coordinate paired-leg move, or conservation reverts from definitional to checked; the knock–revalue–call–discharge interleaving carries a TLA+ safety/liveness proof; the barrier observation must be deterministic and replayable at the data boundary; a package-aware minimiser is needed if any agreement declares package valuation. The post-and-return metamorphic property carries its watch-free side condition (D3).
- **Settlement-state is two obligations, not one.** Entitlement routing across the instruction-to-settlement gap is a **correctness requirement ruled here** (D2: re-book at instruction, market-claim obligations as first-class reconciling instruments). The finops encumbrance/intraday-liquidity join over (coordinate × settlement-state × agreement rate) remains a Phase 2 requirement.
- **Supervised write-off retirement — named Phase 2/3 obligation.** Zero-vector retirement has no exit for a defaulted issuer's marker mass: nobody voluntarily returns a worthless unit, so dead units would accumulate, polluting encumbrance projections and drowning the obligation-liveness queue. v15 must carry an issuer-default lifecycle event permitting marker-plane clearing by compensating moves against explicit loss recognition, without the voluntary agreement machinery.
- **The SBL corollary is entailed, not open.** D2's derivation — owned is what moves can act on; the borrower's purpose is to sell — applies verbatim to the GMSLA loan leg: a lent-plane marker with owned retained makes the borrower's short sale unrepresentable or coverage-violating. The SBL chapter owns the mechanics, not the conclusion.

## 6. Constitution amendments required

**Amendment 1 — §8, third-limb sentence.** Current text, verbatim:

> "Every inflow of value is one of three things: an exchange paired with an equal-valued outflow; a contribution or financing received against an equal obligation created in the same transaction; or value held in custody without being owned (including cash received as collateral, recorded on the collateral-received coordinate)."

Replacement:

> "Every inflow of value is one of three things: an exchange paired with an equal-valued outflow — settled-to-market margin is this case, a settlement extinguishing the day's exposure with no return obligation; a contribution or financing received against an equal obligation created in the same transaction — collateral received under title transfer, cash included, is this case, owned by the receiver against an equivalent-return obligation that is itself a unit; or value held in custody without being owned, recorded on the collateral-received coordinate — collateral received under a security interest without right of use, segregated cash included, is this case. Where an inflow moves under a margin or collateral agreement, which case governs it is declared once, on that agreement unit."

The following sentence of §8 ("A deposit therefore cannot move profit and loss…") stands unchanged and remains true under all cases — the v15 text will state the fact this depends on: the case-2 return-obligation unit is a valued unit priced at the inflow amount, par plus accrued at the declared rate, so net owned value cannot move at receipt.

**Amendment 2 — §4, one clarifying sentence.** After "Only the owned coordinate carries economic value and drives profit and loss.", insert:

> "The remaining coordinates carry mass, never value: they record possession and encumbrance under a named agreement, no quantity reaches them without reference to the agreement unit that governs it, and a lifecycle event extinguishes value, never mass — a unit leaves the ledger only from the zero vector."

**Amendment 3 — §4, list sentence.** Replace:

> "…it generalises to a vector of coordinates: owned, lent out, borrowed, posted as collateral, received as collateral."

with:

> "…it generalises to a signed vector of coordinates — owned, lent, posted — whose named rays are: lent out and borrowed, the two signs of lent; posted as collateral and received as collateral, the two signs of posted."

This keeps all five names as fixed constitutional vocabulary, makes "only the owned coordinate carries economic value" exact, and gives Amendment 2's "the remaining coordinates" a clean referent.

**Conformance otherwise.** No amendment beyond these three is needed. §4's physical-action test is the very premise of D1 and D3 — title transfer is a change *of* ownership and writes owned; a pledge changes possession *independently* of ownership and writes the marker plane; re-use and encumbrance are "computable from the coordinates", hence projections by §4's closing sentence; §6 is satisfied because every claim, obligation, manufactured payment, market claim, and trap condition the agreements create is a unit or a declared term of one; §8's three categories are kept and sharpened, not replaced.
