## Deliverable 3 — Recommendation on the `mentalmodel` device (for the author; NOT executed beyond sec09)

**Recommendation: do not retire the device document-wide. Retire the *long holds/breaks analogy form*, and cap the device at one short image per concept.**

What this rewrite revealed:
- The device *failed* in sec09 for a specific reason: the four analogies were not short images but 180–210-word "here is the analogy; here is what carries over; here is what breaks" essays attached to a *dense mechanism* (the basis chain, the welds). The prior blind STYLUS×5 pass found these were the section's worst-scoring paragraphs — expository scaffolding well beyond the minimal statement. Deleting them and letting the worked split/dividend/index examples carry the intuition removed the concentration of sub-floor prose.
- The device *succeeds* elsewhere (sec03/05/06/07/08/12/16/17) because those are short single images for *primitive* concepts — water sealed in pipes (conservation), a notebook in ink (the log), a cloakroom ticket (a claim). One picture, a few lines, deletion-safe. Those do not have the sec09 failure mode.

So the lesson is not "metaphor vs example" in general; it is **length and target**. A one-line image for a primitive aids intuition; a multi-paragraph holds/breaks analogy for a mechanism competes with the mechanism and loses. For material with real machinery, a worked example on the running small world teaches better and is checkable.

Concrete proposal for the author, if desired (a separate exercise): (a) keep the device but add a house rule — a `mentalmodel` is at most ~60 words, one image, no "what carries over / what breaks" structure; (b) audit the other sections' blocks against that cap (most already comply); (c) where a block has grown into a holds/breaks essay, replace it with a worked example as done here. This is a document-wide *discipline* on the device, not its retirement. **No change was made outside sec09.**

(The `mentalmodel` environment remains defined in the preamble and in active use by 8 other sections; sec09 is no longer a user, but it was not the only user, so the environment stays.)
