## 1. Pareto frontier and adopted design

The frontier contains only the D-family. B fails at Level 1 (the type of the fact — a plural, per-lender, expiring relation — does not embed in a scalar slot on shared UnitStatus; unanimous). C fails the hard constraint (books a move for something that did not legally move) and, decisively, proves the wrong theorem: it enforces a box-invariant the law falsifies, since a lender may lawfully sell located stock after confirming (FORMALIS's proof-shape argument; WILSON's added point that C's "unrepresentable" compiles down to a value-level check in this framework anyway). A is dominated by D: same ontology, minus the arithmetic bug, minus the unstated race obligation. Within D, the round-2 record eliminates every variant but one; tie-breakers were not needed — each rival D-variant carries an undischarged material objection.

**ADOPTED: D\* — "A's ontology, welded" (FORMALIS's frame, corrected by MINSKY (a)/(b)/(c), WILSON's confirm-vs-sell, FINOPS's no-stored-counter, NAZAROV's timestamp authority and boundary split, REGULATORY's kind parameter, SBL's divisibility).** Its content:

1. **Ontology (from A, unchanged):** a locate is a TTL unit in the Unit Store, no position-vector entries, per-locate identity, lifecycle, audit record; SSR/Reg SHO record-keeping native. Nothing moves; conservation is not engaged — a claim about the world.
2. **Gate placement (WILSON, ISDA, NAZAROV):** locate confirmation is a **move-less transaction admitted through `applyTx`**; the corrected capacity predicate is its admission condition, decidable from the committed log and the transaction alone, evaluated at the transaction's position in the total order — the sec09 weld idiom. Per-(lender,security) workflow owners are **rejected as insufficient** (WILSON's confirm-vs-sell: `own` is written by sales committing from outside any locate-family owner).
3. **Activity authority (NAZAROV; WILSON's round-2 withdrawal adopted):** `active(loc, t) := confirmed ∧ remaining > 0 ∧ t < expiry`, with **t = the transaction's log timestamp**. Log-decidable, replay-exact, lag-immune; the scheduler's terminal sweep is hygiene, not authority; status-based activity reads are forbidden (single-authority obligation, WILSON v. NAZAROV).
4. **P14 structural (SBL's shape, MINSKY's point, REGULATORY's D2):** the locate carries `remaining_quantity`; **short-sale admission references a `locate_id` and decrements `remaining` monotonically in the same admission** (P15-style drawdown). Conversion to borrow co-admits the §16.8 loan-initiation moves and the drawdown/terminalisation **in one atomic transaction** (FINOPS's conversion-atomicity clause). FORMALIS's "sale and conversion in the same transaction" is **struck** (MINSKY objection 1: it books a borrow that has not legally occurred). Consumption point: drawdown at short-sale admission; terminalisation at conversion or expiry.
5. **`reserved` is a read-time fold, never stored (FINOPS, sustained against MINSKY's primary counter and NAZAROV's materialised aggregate):** `reserved(e,u,t) = Σ remaining(loc)` over active **hold-kind** locates. It sums *remaining* quantities (WILSON v. FORMALIS). Acceleration, if wanted, is a rebuildable non-authoritative index. The Projections definition must be split into coordinate-projections and store-projections, since `reserved` is not a function of the six coordinates (FINOPS round 2).
6. **`locate_kind` parameter (REGULATORY, NAZAROV):** four confirmation types per Implementing Regulation (EU) 827/2012 Art 6 — standard, same-day, easy-to-borrow, put-on-hold. Only **put-on-hold** enters the `reserved` fold (there the bound is law — the provider's undertaking); for standard/ETB/same-day the gate is firm policy and the kind may lawfully over-locate. SBL's desk-realism is carried by the kind parameter; SBL's haircut/anticipated-inventory fold into `avail` is **rejected** (MINSKY, FINOPS, WILSON: phantom asset, unpinned model parameter, breaks the depot reconciliation).
7. **Boundary split (NAZAROV's finding, adopted with MINSKY's totality repair):** the capacity gate is total only over in-ledger lenders. An inbound locate from an external/agent lender is an **attested boundary observation**, admitted as a unit on its attestation, ungated — capacity is the issuer's obligation. Under SSR 12(1)(c) the confirming party is a third party, so this is P14's main case, not an edge.
8. **CDM (ISDA, amended by MINSKY):** locate lifecycle stays off-model (gap registered, §16.17); the sole CDM event is the genuine `ContractFormation` at conversion, carrying the locate id as lineage. The "non-economic CDM BusinessEvent (no moves)" sentence at sec17.tex:452 is deleted; the record hook is restated as a positive obligation (WILSON's stylistic point v. ISDA).

## 2. The race question — definitive answer

**As written, the gate is not race-free, and no serialisation argument rescues it; the answer is REPAIR, after which race-freedom is a theorem.** Origin: FORMALIS's counterexample (two fresh locate units share no serialisation key under sec16.tex:48's per-unit model; both read capacity C, q1+q2>C, both commit), completed by WILSON's round-2 confirm-vs-sell interleaving, which kills every per-pair-owner remedy. Repair: the predicate becomes an `applyTx` admission condition evaluated at the transaction's commit position in the total order. Then: two confirms are totally ordered and the later sees the earlier's locate in the fold; confirm-vs-convert is safe because conversion's drawdown and loan moves are one transaction (capacity-neutral, atomically visible); confirm-vs-expire is a non-race because activity is a timestamp predicate — there is no expiry event to tear against; confirm-vs-sell is closed because the one door owns the out-of-family writers too. Race-freedom is inherited from the framework's existing total order, not argued case-by-case (NAZAROV's formulation).

## 3. The legal question — definitive answer

**Reservation semantics is FALSE under current law, and the adopted design states no false legal fact.** EU SSR Art 12(1)(c) requires a third-party confirmation of location plus a reasonable expectation of settlement; US Reg SHO Rule 203(b)(1) requires borrow, arrangement, or reasonable grounds to believe — ETB reliance rests on **203(b)(1)(ii)**, not 203(b)(2) (REGULATORY's citation correction, binding on the text). No property interest transfers; the same inventory lawfully backs sales, other loans, and multiple locates. Booking `free→reserved` records an encumbrance that did not occur. The one reservation-like species — the **put-on-hold** confirmation of **Implementing** Regulation (EU) 827/2012 Art 6 (instrument class corrected per MINSKY v. REGULATORY) — is a contractual undertaking to hold: an obligation, still not a move; FORMALIS's carve-out ("the one flavour where the law does block inventory" as a C-legitimate case) is **struck** (REGULATORY). **ESMA 70-448-10 contingency:** the proposed reinforcement strengthens the provider's commitment and record-keeping; it does not convert the confirmation into a transfer. If enacted, the change is a `locate_kind`/gate-parameter change and, where binding, an obligation record under §sec:obligation-liveness — zero change to move discipline. This robustness is itself part of the answer to THE QUESTION.

## 4. The double-count defect — ruling

**BUG. CRITICAL as a specification defect; the adopted design carries the fix.** Unanimous (all seven filings). Proof: `available_to_lend` (sec17.tex:377–385) already returns `max(0, own − onloan + borr − reserved − hold)` with `reserved = Σ active locates`; `confirm_locate` (447–450) subtracts the identical sum again, so the composed gate demands `avail − 2·reserved − hold ≥ Q`, while the prose one line later (451–452) states one deduction chain with `reserved` subtracted once — the code refutes the prose in the same subsection. Counterexample (FORMALIS): own=100, one active locate of 40 → true headroom 60; the gate declines every Q in (20, 60]. Extent stated exactly (REGULATORY's round-2 correction): wrongful declines occupy (atl − O, atl]; "declined once any locate is live" is false. Direction conservative — completeness failure, not soundness — but "intended buffer" is untenable: state-dependent, unmotivated, unstatable as an invariant, and the caller re-implements the projection it calls. **Fix:** `confirm_locate(lender, security, quantity, t) := CONFIRMED iff available_to_lend(lender, security, t) ≥ quantity`; the new locate is not yet active, so ATL already nets exactly the existing outstanding (REGULATORY's self-exclusion note). The subtraction lives in one place.

## 5. Over-location + P14 enforcement story

**Over-location is NOT made unrepresentable, and the memo rules that this is correct, not a concession.** The central finding (FORMALIS, seconded by MINSKY and WILSON): over-location freedom has *precondition* shape, not *invariant* shape — lawful operations (a sale of located stock) falsify the standing inequality afterwards. A property of precondition shape gets a gate at the admission door; a property of invariant shape gets structure. Enforcement is therefore:

- **Named admission-time check — the *locate-capacity weld*** (a Principle in the sec09 idiom): `applyTx` admits a locate-confirmation transaction for an in-ledger lender only if `AvailableToLend(lender, security, t) ≥ quantity`, decidable from the committed log and the transaction alone, `t` the transaction's log timestamp, `reserved` folding remaining quantities of active hold-kind locates.
- **Witnessing property — P24 (locate-capacity admission), step-indexed, log-checkable:** at every locate-confirmation commit position p, for the confirming in-ledger lender, Σ remaining over active hold-kind locates ≤ max(0, avail − regulatory_hold) evaluated at p. Falsifiable by replaying the log with no knowledge of the implementation (WILSON's step-indexed form). **Prohibition (MINSKY, binding on all layers including tests and reconciliation):** no artifact may restate this as a standing reachable-state invariant or solvency identity (WILSON's original INV-OL and FINOPS's "O ≤ B" are both barred) — that re-imports C's false theorem.
- **P14 becomes structural:** a short sale is admissible only by referencing a live locate unit and drawing down its `remaining_quantity` in the same admission; drawdown is monotone; double-spend of a locate is a non-state under the unit's serialised one-shot lifecycle. P14's restatement: *"Every short-sale admission draws down a named, live locate; drawdown is monotone and terminal states are absorbing."* The existential form ("a locate must exist") is retired (FORMALIS's HIGH finding).

## 6. What STYLUS must write

**(a) Into §16.14, a short native remark (place directly after the "Modelling" paragraph), substance fixed, wording STYLUS's:**

> **Why a unit and not a coordinate.** A coordinate holds a conserved, anonymous quantity of something that legally moved. A locate is the opposite on every axis: plural — several stand per security, each with its own lender, quantity, and expiry; identified — each carries its own record-keeping obligation and one-shot lifecycle; and non-conserved — nothing moves at confirmation, so there is no quantity to conserve. A per-lender relation does not embed in a scalar slot on the security's shared status, and booking a reservation move would record an encumbrance the law does not create: under SSR Article 12(1)(c) and Reg SHO Rule 203(b)(1) a locate confirms deliverability and blocks nothing — the lender may lawfully sell located stock the next instant. Freedom from over-location is therefore a condition at the moment of confirmation, not an invariant of every later state; a property of that shape is enforced at the admission door (the locate-capacity weld below), while identity, expiry, and audit belong to a unit. Only the put-on-hold confirmation of Implementing Regulation (EU) 827/2012 binds the provider to hold quantity, and there the bound is law, not policy; it enters the reserved deduction and, where binding, an obligation record — still no move. Should ESMA 70-448-10's reinforcement be enacted, the locate's commitment strengthens as a gate parameter and obligation; the primitive's type does not change.

RACE (i): NOT established race-free as written; it needs repair by relocation, not redesign. The framework's serialisation guarantee is PER UNIT: 'One lifecycle workflow is assigned per unit... concurrent lifecycle events on the same unit serialise' (sec16.tex:48; restated sec22.tex:16 — 'the executor never receives concurrent mutations for one unit'). But two concurrent confirm_locate calls create two DISTINCT fresh locate units; per-unit serialisation imposes no order between them, and neither owns the gate's read footprint, which spans OTHER units: PositionState(lender,security) plus the set of ALL active locate units for that pair. Formal counterexample: committed state sigma with capacity(sigma)=C; requests q1, q2 with q1<=C, q2<=C, q1+q2>C; both workflows evaluate the gate against sigma (each on its own fresh unit, no common serialisation key), both return CONFIRMED, both registration transactions commit — over-location, with no invariant violated that the machine can see, because the gate's predicate was never an admission condition of applyTx. The framework already possesses the correct idiom and uses it twice: the tip weld and the invariance weld are conditions 'checkable at admission from the committed log and the transaction alone' (sec09.tex:202-208), re-evaluated at the commit point. The locate gate is instead presented as a workflow-level function over derived reads at time t — outside the weld. Repair: (1) declare the serialisation domain (lender,security) — e.g. route locate confirm/convert/expire admissions for a pair through one owner (a per-pair locate book, or the security unit's workflow scoped per lender); (2) restate the gate as an applyTx admission condition evaluated against the committed prefix under that domain. The other two interleavings: confirm-vs-expire is SAFE in the dangerous direction — an uncommitted expiry makes the confirm see a larger outstanding and decline conservatively; determinism requires only that 'active' be defined by the expiry timestamp on committed data (a pure function of log prefix and t), not by due-event arrival. Confirm-vs-convert is capacity-NEUTRAL (conversion decreases reserved by q and increases onloan by q in ONE atomic transaction, so available_to_lend is unchanged) — but only if the gate reads one committed prefix; a torn read across the two state homes (PositionState updated, locate set not yet, or vice versa) shows capacity q higher than it ever was. Both hazards vanish under repair (1)+(2): a single serial commit order per (lender,security) with the predicate at the door gives, by construction, that any two conflicting admissions are ordered and the later one sees the earlier one committed. Verdict: the two-confirm race is real and fatal to the gate as specified; the fix is one declared domain plus one weld, after which race-freedom is a theorem of the framework's existing serialisation model rather than an assumption.
LEGAL (ii): FALSE under current law as a universal model; at most conditionally and jurisdictionally true under the proposed reform — which is fatal to C either way, because a primitive's semantics must be true in every regime the ledger books under. Current law: US Reg SHO Rule 203(b)(1) requires only that the broker-dealer borrow, arrange to borrow, or have 'reasonable grounds to believe' the security can be borrowed for delivery — easy-to-borrow lists satisfy it; no property interest is created, no inventory is blocked, and the same inventory lawfully backs concurrent sales, other loans, and multiple locates against anticipated supply. EU SSR Article 12(1)(c) requires a third-party confirmation that the share is located plus measures giving a reasonable expectation of settlement — a confirmation of deliverability, not an encumbrance; ESMA's SSR guidelines distinguish the standard locate from the 'put on hold' confirmation for less-liquid shares, and only the latter actually blocks quantity at the covering party. A locate is the opposite on every axis: (1) PLURAL — many concurrent locates per (lender, security), each with its own quantity, counterparty, and expiry; a scalar cannot hold a relation; (2) NON-CONSERVED — nothing legally moved (hard constraint: a move may be booked only for something that legally moved; "conservation is not engaged" is a claim about the WORLD, and the world here contains a confirmation, not a transfer); (3) LIFECYCLED — request→confirmed→converted|expired needs a state machine, a scheduler hook, and a per-instance audit record (EU SSR / ESMA 70-448-10 record-keeping wants exactly the per-instance record a coordinate structurally cannot produce). Units are the framework's home for identified, lifecycled, non-conserved facts; coordinates for conserved, anonymous quantity. State that dichotomy once and the question cannot arise.

Cons (both fatal as written): (a) the gate double-counts (above); (b) the gate is NOT race-free — see race answer; per-unit serialisation does not cover a cross-unit predicate. Also two determinism defects: active_locates(…, t) is ambiguous between clock-filtered (loc.expiry > t) and event-filtered (status == ACTIVE); if the gate compares clocks while the state machine transitions on scheduler events, live and replay DIVERGE — two definitions of "active." And regulatory_restrictions(entity, security, t) is an un-enumerated non-deterministic boundary: external reference data read at gate time must be an injected, recorded, as-of-queryable snapshot or replay is fiction. Over-location PROPERTY for A — falsifiable-by-test, ramp level 3 (safety): INV-OL: ∀ reachable states, ∀ (lender, sec): Σ_{loc ∈ active} loc.qty ≤ max(0, own − onloan + borr − reg_hold). C's property, stated for completeness: vacuous-at-ledger (free ≥ 0 enforced at admission) plus a mapping property — ∀ event streams: reserve moves ↔ confirmed put-on-hold locates, bijectively — which is HARDER to test than INV-OL.

D (adopted synthesis, concretely): keep the TTL locate unit exactly as A has it (audit, lifecycle, scheduler, SSR record-keeping, CDM-gap handling). Three additions. Confirm, convert, and expire for one (lender, security) then serialise at the one write door; the two-confirm race is closed by construction using a primitive the spec already protects. Minimalism is served: zero new machinery. (2) P14 STRUCTURAL: the confirmed locate is a linear capability — consumed exactly once, by conversion or by short-sale admission; double-spend is unrepresentable (the unit's terminal transition IS the consumption); P14 stops being a check on a query and becomes token-spend at the admission door. That is the correct trade: the check is a fact about the world (policy over inventory), so it belongs at admission; the race and the double-spend are facts about the machine, so they are eliminated structurally. Determinism requirements pinned as spec text: (i) active = status ACTIVE in the committed log, never a clock comparison; the scheduler's expiry event is the sole transition authority; (ii) regulatory_restrictions is an injected recorded snapshot keyed by (entity, security, as-of); (iii) consequently every gate decision replays bit-for-bit from (log + scheduled events) — the replay-equivalence test: record a run's CONFIRMED/DECLINED stream, replay, byte-diff. Cons of D: one more admission-predicate kind at applyTx (small, and the pattern exists); the linear-consumption rule adds a clause to the SBL state machine table (one row).

(2) INV-OL is STRICTER than law: Reg SHO permits the same inventory to back easy-to-borrow assurances and multiple locates against anticipated supply; agent lenders locate against pooled anticipated inventory. If the business intends law-maximal locate granting, INV-OL is internal POLICY, not a correctness law — the spec must say so, or my test oracle enshrines a business restriction as an invariant and we Goodhart ourselves into declining legal business. (3) The gate's `entity` is ambiguous for agent lenders: available_to_lend at which aggregation — beneficial owner or pool? The pooled-own row in sec17's reconciliation table (492) suggests pool, but the gate must name it. (4) Cross-venue: a short facing both SSR and Reg SHO (sec17.tex:476) may need TWO locate records with different evidentiary content; the unit model absorbs this with a regime field; I have not verified P14's quantifier handles two-regime coverage.
RACE (i): NOT race-free as specified — and the spec's own text proves it. The framework's serialisation guarantee is PER UNIT: sec16.tex:48 "One lifecycle workflow is assigned per unit … concurrent lifecycle events on the same unit serialise"; sec22.tex:16 "Each lifecycle workflow instance serialises events for its unit." But the locate gate is a CROSS-UNIT predicate: it reads the lender's position wallet, the set of N distinct locate units (each locate is its OWN TTL unit per sec17.tex:443), and external regulatory_restrictions — and the confirm it guards creates a NEW unit that belongs to no existing unit's workflow at decision time. No single serialisation domain contains everything the gate reads plus the commit it guards. Case analysis: (1) TWO CONFIRMS, same (lender, security): two workflow instances, two different (about-to-exist) units; both read committed atl = X ≥ Q, both pass, both commit; reserved becomes 2Q > X possible → INV-OL violated. Per-unit serialisation is silent because no shared unit exists. This is the classic TOCTOU that per-entity actors never fix for cross-entity predicates. (2) CONFIRM vs CONVERT: conversion is atl-NEUTRAL iff atomic — it releases the locate from the active set (reserved −Q) and books the loan (onloan +Q, so avail −Q); net zero. If the locate terminalisation and the loan moves commit in one transaction (sec16.tex:186 says the executor commits obligations atomically with the triggering event, but the spec does NOT currently state this for locate-terminal + loan-moves), a concurrent confirm reads only committed states on either side of the neutral step — safe. If they commit separately, there is a window where NEITHER the reservation nor the loan is visible: a concurrent confirm sees phantom capacity Q → over-confirm. The atomicity claim must be stated and tested (my stateful machine's atl-neutrality postcondition). (3) CONFIRM vs EXPIRE: one-sided safe for INV-OL in isolation — a confirm that still counts a not-yet-expired locate is merely conservative, and capacity freed by a COMMITTED expiry event is genuinely free. But two confirms racing FOR the freed capacity reduces to case (1). Additional determinism hazard: if the gate filters active locates by clock (expiry > t) while the state machine transitions on the scheduler event, the gate's answer depends on wall-clock at read time → live/replay divergence even absent concurrency. THE FIX (and why I rank D): route confirmation through applyTx as a move-less transaction whose admission predicate is the corrected gate, "decidable from the committed log" (the SetBasis precedent, sec09.tex:202); convert and expire already pass through the same door. One door ⇒ total order over {confirm, convert, expire} per (lender, security) ⇒ every gate reads the state left by its predecessor ⇒ two confirms cannot both see the same headroom. Race-freedom then follows from a primitive the spec already protects, and the interleaving test becomes a replayable enumeration: generate op sequences, admit under the deterministic scheduler in both orders, assert INV-OL and confirm-set determinism from the seed.
LEGAL (ii): Reservation semantics as C states it — every confirmed locate encumbers lender inventory — is LEGALLY FALSE under current law, with one carve-out that matters, and the carve-out is itself the strongest argument for the unit model. CURRENT LAW: US Reg SHO Rule 203(b)(1) requires the broker-dealer to have borrowed, arranged to borrow, or have "reasonable grounds to believe" the security can be borrowed — the easy-to-borrow list satisfies it with NO inventory identification at all, let alone a hold; the same lendable supply lawfully backs many simultaneous locates. EU SSR Art 12(1)(c) requires a third-party confirmation that the share is "located" plus measures giving reasonable expectation of settlement — a confirmation of deliverability, not an encumbrance. Booking free→reserved for such a locate records a transfer of control that did not legally occur — it violates the hard constraint that a move is booked only for something that legally moved. BUT the ground truth is not binary, and this is the sharp point: ESMA's own SSR guidelines already distinguish locate FLAVOURS — the standard (easy-to-borrow / liquid-share, same-day) confirmation versus the "locate WITH put-on-hold" confirmation used for illiquid shares and longer horizons, where the confirming party does put the identified quantity on hold. For an internal lending desk issuing a put-on-hold locate against our OWN inventory, a hold is operationally real TODAY — yet even then it is a self-imposed constraint on lending capacity, not a title movement: the correct model is a subtraction in a projection (exactly how the spec already treats regulatory_hold at sec17.tex:384), never a conserved-plane move. PROPOSED LAW: ESMA 70-448-10 (cited at sec17.tex:439) proposes reinforcing the locate provider's commitment — the direction of travel enlarges the put-on-hold class and hardens the lender's obligation, possibly toward earmarking. Under the proposal, C becomes legally TRUE-ER but still not true-as-title-transfer: an earmark is a commitment burdening the provider, not a movement of securities; the honest representation remains a locate record with a type field ∈ {confirmation, put-on-hold} plus the derived reserved-projection feeding atl. DESIGN CONSEQUENCE (why this decides between C and A/D rather than merely scoring them): the legal ground truth is HETEROGENEOUS PER LOCATE and DRIFTING. The unit model absorbs both facts with one field and one projection term; the coordinate model must split the coordinate and re-derive the move schedule when the law moves. C hard-codes tomorrow's possible law into today's conserved plane; A/D record today's law and are one field away from tomorrow's. Verdict: (ii) FALSE under current law as a universal semantics; PARTIALLY TRUE today for the put-on-hold subclass (correctly modelled as projection, not moves); under the ESMA proposal the subclass grows but the projection-not-moves conclusion stands.

### SBL (Margaret Chen — securities lending market practice)
RANKS FIRST: A, sharpened toward D1 — keep the locate as a TTL unit, but express over-location prevention as an admission-serialised derived projection (D1's enforcement without C's false moves), and fix the double-count bug. A is the only candidate that matches what a locate actually is on the desk: a plural, per-counterparty, time-limited relationship with its own identity and audit lifecycle, moving nothing.
POSITION: WHAT A LOCATE IS ON THE DESK (the ground truth §16.14 must encode). A locate is a confirmation of a reasonable expectation of borrowing — not an earmark of specific shares. Four market facts fix this and each is fatal to reservation semantics: (1) Agent-lender aggregation — a custodian running a lending program for hundreds of beneficial owners confirms locates against an aggregate anticipated-supply pool, never against a named owner's box; the same underlying backs many locates. (2) Anticipated inventory — desks locate against recalls settling, returns inbound, and street GC they can source; a locate can precede the shares being in the box. (3) Easy-to-borrow (ETB) lists — on deep GC names brokers publish blanket ETB lists and auto-locate any client, any reasonable size, with NO inventory check; the industry deliberately over-locates by orders of magnitude and this is legal and correct. (4) Locate-to-borrow conversion rates are LOW — commonly 10-40%, often less; a hedge fund locates the same short across 3+ prime brokers to keep execution optionality and borrows from one, so located volume vastly exceeds borrowed volume. The market functions BECAUSE locates are not reservations; a 1:1 reservation regime would need 3-10x the float blocked to support actual borrowing and would seize. Over-location against a probabilistic conversion model is the economically correct behaviour — airline overbooking.

DOES RESERVATION MATCH ANY REAL PRACTICE? Essentially none. The one corner that looks like it — a hard/exclusive locate on a special where the PB pre-borrows to guarantee supply — is not a locate at all: the shares moved, so conservation already engages; that is §16.8 loan-initiation ("convert early"), not the locate model. So even the hard-to-borrow case argues against C, not for it.

RANK AND FULL PROS/CONS:
- A (TTL unit + gate) — RANKED FIRST. Pro: faithful to practice; locates are plural and relational (each with its own lender, borrower, quantity, expiry); a unit gives identity, an auditable per-locate lifecycle, and a scheduler hook — exactly the 5-year retention record ESMA 70-448-10 will demand; the shared security status is untouched; conservation stays honest because nothing moved. This is literally how a locate desk runs: a locate log with same-/end-of-day TTLs plus an availability engine. Con: over-location is a check, not a structural impossibility (addressed by D1/D2 hardening below); the gate as written is buggy (double-count, ruled below) AND, even corrected, its strict 1:1 form is STRICTER than the market — it treats anticipated inventory as zero and every locate as consuming a unit of avail, so on a GC/ETB name it will wrongly decline valid locates. §16.14 must either scope the 1:1 gate to hard/HTB locates and exempt ETB, or fold anticipated inventory into avail and a conversion-rate haircut into reserved. Otherwise the section reads as operationally naive to any practitioner — failing the "careful reader never asks" bar.
- B (coordinate on the security's shared UnitStatus) — WORST-but-one. A global scalar answers no question the desk asks: locates are per-lender and per-relationship, so the scalar is either wrong or becomes a per-lender map smuggled into a scalar's single-writer home, breaking the closed status-writer discipline and destroying the per-locate audit record that is the entire regulatory point. Operationally you cannot run an agent-lender book off one number. Reject.
- C (reservation coordinate, free->reserved->on-loan) — WORST operationally and legally. It forces 1:1 encumbrance, making the desk decline sound business, and it structurally forbids ETB lists, agent-pool aggregation, and cross-PB duplication — the mechanisms the market depends on. It books an encumbrance that did not occur, misleading every downstream projection (avail, exposure) into treating freely-sellable inventory as blocked, and it pollutes the conserved plane with non-economic moves — the exact pollution the framework forbids. MINSKY's "unrepresentable" appeal is real but it makes the CORRECT commercial behaviour unrepresentable. Reject.
- D (synthesis) — the right refinement of A. D1 (units + derived, admission-checked reservation PROJECTION) is what a real availability engine IS: keep A's units, express the gate as a projection over the Unit Store evaluated at the serialisation point, and locate the conversion-rate haircut / ETB carve-out naturally inside the projection. This gives C's enforcement discipline with none of C's false moves. Endorse. D2 (typed locate capability consumed linearly at short-sale admission) usefully makes P14 structural — a locate spent exactly once, preventing one locate backing two shorts — BUT it must be DIVISIBLE and tolerate non-consumption: on the desk one locate covers "up to Q," may be partially drawn (partial fill leaves a residual), and frequently expires unused. A single-use indivisible token mismodels partial fills; model it as a divisible capability drawn down at conversion, expiring on TTL. Recommended layering: A's unit (identity + audit) + D1's projection-gate (enforcement, race-free at admission) + optionally D2's divisible capability at short-sale admission (structural P14). That is my ranked synthesis.

RULING ON THE FLAGGED DEFECT — it is a BUG the adopted design must fix. available_to_lend already returns max(0, avail - reserved - reg_hold) with reserved = sum(active_locates). confirm_locate then subtracts outstanding = sum(active_locates) = reserved AGAIN, testing avail - 2*reserved - reg_hold >= quantity. Existing locates are double-counted. Note the short-sale gate (lines 378-385) calls available_to_lend directly and is NOT affected — only confirm_locate is wrong. The prose is right and the code contradicts it: the stated deduction chain "owned -> minus on-loan -> plus borrowed -> minus reserved = available to lend" subtracts locates ONCE. Fix: confirm_locate returns CONFIRMED if available_to_lend(lender, security, t) >= quantity else DECLINED. available_to_lend already nets existing locates via reserved; the new locate's quantity is tested against the remaining headroom — one subtraction, once. (Separately, the market-practice caveat above stands: even the corrected 1:1 gate is stricter than desk reality and needs the ETB/anticipated-inventory treatment.)
RACE (i): Race-free — but only conditionally, and the spec does not yet discharge the condition. The gate reads two derived quantities (avail and sum of active_locates) and admits/declines; correctness depends entirely on WHEN it is evaluated. It is race-free if and only if three things hold. (1) Locate confirmation is itself an admitted event through the single admission-serialisation point, and the gate's read of state plus the append of the new locate unit are ONE atomic read-append under that serialisation (evaluated against committed state AS OF serialisation, not against a snapshot taken at request receipt). If evaluated at request-time, it is classic TOCTOU — the very race the 'native, not companion' argument (line 209) invokes to justify SBL being native; that same argument therefore demands the locate gate be admission-time, and the spec must state this explicitly. It currently does not. (2) confirm-vs-convert: conversion is a real move (loan-initiation) that raises onloan/borr and terminates the locate unit; under total serialisation the two orderings are both safe — convert-then-confirm sees reduced avail and the terminated locate gone from active_locates; confirm-then-convert sees the new locate and consumes it. No cross-boundary double-spend. (3) confirm-vs-expire: expiry MUST be a materialised, serialised terminal-state transition via the due-event scheduler (which the design specifies), NOT a lazy read-time TTL-vs-wall-clock comparison. If active_locates recomputes 'active' lazily, a locate can be simultaneously expired-for-the-reader and not-yet-terminated-in-the-log — a split view that races on the boundary instant. Because the design materialises expiry through the scheduler and the gate must read that materialised terminal state, this leg is clean. Conclusion: confirm-vs-convert and confirm-vs-expire are race-free by construction; confirm-vs-confirm is race-free ONLY if the spec commits the gate to admission-time read-append serialisation. That commitment is the missing sentence §16.14 must add. D1 makes this natural: the projection is defined to be evaluated by the admission engine at the serialisation point, request-time evaluation being advisory only.
LEGAL (ii): FALSE under current law, and still FALSE under the ESMA proposal. Current: EU SSR Art 12(1)(c) requires a third-party confirmation that the share has been located plus measures giving a 'reasonable expectation of settlement' — an evidentiary/expectation standard, not a property right or lien over the lender's inventory. US Reg SHO Rule 203(b)(1) requires 'reasonable grounds to believe' the security can be borrowed — again expectation, not encumbrance. Neither blocks the located inventory: the same shares remain freely sellable by the owner, lendable to others, and locatable against further requests. ETB lists, agent-lender pools, anticipated inventory, and sub-100% cross-PB conversion all directly evidence that located shares are not encumbered. Booking free->reserved therefore records a legal encumbrance that did not occur; under the Ledger's own rule ('a move may be booked only for something that legally moved') it is impermissible, and it puts non-economic moves in the conserved plane. Proposed (ESMA 70-448-10): still FALSE, gap narrows. The Final Report proposes reinforcing the locate provider's commitment (strengthening the confirming party's obligation) plus a Level-1 record-keeping obligation with 5-year retention. Reinforced commitment strengthens the undertaking-to-make-deliverable; it moves the locate nearer a firm commitment but stops short of a proprietary encumbrance on identified inventory — the located shares remain fungibly available. So even post-reform, reservation-as-encumbrance overstates the legal fact. What the proposal DOES mandate is exactly the auditable, retained, per-locate record — delivered natively by A/D and conflated with a phantom move by C. Hence proposed law strengthens the case for A's record-keeping unit and does NOT vindicate C. Were the law ever to require hard pre-commitment of identified shares, the faithful model would be an early BORROW (a real move under §16.8), never a reservation coordinate. 'Conservation is not engaged' thus remains a true claim about the world — a locate moves nothing — which is precisely why a locate is a unit (a plural, per-counterparty, time-limited relationship with identity and lifecycle) and not a coordinate (a conserved quantity that moves).

### REGULATORY
RANKS FIRST: D1 (synthesis): locate-as-TTL-unit + a derived, admission-checked reservation *projection* (with D2 linear locate-capabilities for P14) — the only candidate that is native for the per-locate record obligation AND legally accurate (no false encumbrance in the conserved plane). If forced to a listed letter: D.
POSITION: MY LENS: the binding texts and the per-locate record obligation. Two legal facts govern everything.

FACT 1 — the locate is the weakest of the covered-short pre-conditions. EU SSR (Reg 236/2012) Art 12(1) lists three alternatives: (a) borrowed / equivalent legal effect; (b) an *absolutely enforceable claim* (agreement to borrow, property/contract right to be transferred ownership); (c) a *locate arrangement* — a third party confirms the share is located and has taken measures giving a *reasonable expectation of settlement*. (c) is deliberately weaker than (b): no transfer, no enforceable claim, no encumbrance. Reg SHO 203(b)(1) mirrors this: 203(b)(1)(ii) accepts *reasonable grounds to believe* the security can be borrowed (easy-to-borrow) — no specific shares held at all.

FACT 2 — the law itself separates a locate from a hold. Del. Reg. (EU) 827/2012 distinguishes a "locate confirmation" (mere location) from a "put on hold confirmation" (shares actually reserved), and separately admits easy-to-borrow confirmations. So "reserving inventory" is a DISTINCT, STRONGER arrangement the regulation names apart from a bare locate. Any model that hard-codes reservation for all locates asserts a legal fact the regulation expressly treats as optional and separate.

PER-LOCATE RECORD OBLIGATION. Reg SHO 203(b)(1)(iii) requires the broker-dealer to *document* compliance per short sale — the FINRA-examined "locate log": security, source/provider identity, quantity, timestamp, type (hard vs ETB), disposition. SSR RTS 827/2012 likewise requires the confirmations be recorded and the locating third party identified. The obligation is intrinsically PLURAL and PER-ARRANGEMENT: many locates per security, each with its own lender/agent, quantity, expiry, and type.

CANDIDATE-BY-CANDIDATE (record obligation + legal accuracy):

A (TTL unit, no vector entries). RECORD: NATIVE — a unit gives each locate its own identity, lifecycle (request→confirm→convert/expire), provider, quantity, timestamp, and type-as-state; expiry-via-scheduler is the auditable terminal transition. This IS the Reg SHO locate log and the SSR confirmation record, by construction. LEGAL STATEMENT: CORRECT — "not a move, conservation not engaged" is exactly the legal character of a 12(1)(c)/203(b)(1) locate. CON: over-location is a runtime CHECK, not a structural impossibility — discipline substitutes for construction; and as written it carries the double-count bug (below). Legally impeccable; constructionally weaker.

B (scalar coordinate on the security's shared UnitStatus). RECORD: FAILS — a scalar cannot hold per-provider/per-quantity/per-timestamp/per-type provenance; you must keep a full supplementary locate log ANYWAY, making the coordinate redundant to the very records regulation demands. It also misrepresents plural per-lender arrangements as one fungible aggregate against the security — a global scalar answers no question the per-lender gate asks. Does not book a move, so no false encumbrance, but it misstates the *plurality/provenance* of the arrangement. WORST on my lens.

C (position-vector coordinate, reservation semantics: free→reserved on confirm). LEGAL STATEMENT: MATERIALLY FALSE for the base case — booking a free→reserved MOVE records an encumbrance of the lender's inventory that did not legally occur for a standard or ETB locate. The same inventory legitimately backs multiple locates (against anticipated supply), ETB lists, sales, and other loans; RTS 827/2012 reserves "holding" for the separate put-on-hold confirmation. It is legally TRUE only for that narrow put-on-hold subset — and even there the hold is a contractual undertaking, better recorded as obligation/unit state than as a conserved-quantity move. RECORD: also NON-NATIVE — a reserved-quantity carries no source identity or locate type, so a supplementary log is still required. C thus both misstates the law AND fails the record natively, while polluting the conserved plane with non-economic moves — a direct breach of the hard constraint that a move be booked only for something that legally moved. GRAVEST misstatement of the four.

D (synthesis). D1 = A's units + a derived, admission-checked reservation PROJECTION. RECORD: NATIVE (units, as A). LEGAL STATEMENT: CORRECT (nothing moves; "reserved" is a read over active-locate units, not a coordinate). ENFORCEMENT: over-location becomes an admission-serialised projection check, not a bookkept encumbrance — C's discipline without C's false moves. D2 = linear locate CAPABILITIES consumed once at short-sale admission: makes P14 (short-before-locate) STRUCTURAL — the token is spent exactly once by conversion — again without touching the position vector. D1 and D2 are COMPLEMENTARY, not rivals: D1 gates the issuance side (over-location), D2 gates the consumption side (P14). Together they are native on the record, legally accurate, and structural on both gates.

MISSTATEMENT AUDIT (explicit): A — no. B — misstates plurality/provenance (not the move-nature). C — YES, books a legal encumbrance that did not occur. D1/D2 — no; they preserve A's correct statement and add enforcement outside the conserved plane.

MINSKY — MATERIAL (four counts; my primary target).
MINSKY's D (d2/d4) promotes `reserved(lender, security)` to a **stored ℕ counter**, made PRIMARY (the gate reads it), with `Σ active-locate quantities` demoted to an audit CHECK. Every count is a books objection:

1. **Breaks the protected projection discipline.** sec17.tex:187 states projections are never stored, and sec17.tex:195 defines `AvailableToLend = avail − reserved`, making `reserved` a member of that projection family. A stored `reserved` counter directly contradicts a definitional, protected statement (State-Basis / projection-not-stored). This is not a licensed exception: MINSKY invokes sec16's "per wallet for per-wallet state such as futures accumulated cost," but that precedent stores a quantity that is **not derivable** from the six coordinates (average cost is path-dependent). `reserved` **is** exactly derivable — `Σ active-locate quantities`. Storing a derivable quantity manufactures a second representation; the futures precedent stores a non-derivable one and has none. The analogy fails on the only axis that matters.

2. **Reintroduces the exact condition that birthed the double-count.** MINSKY's own diagnosis (its ruling (c)) is that the bug arose because `reserved` "is computed in two call frames because it has NO SINGLE OWNER — state lacking a home." Correct — and the cure is one *definition* evaluated once (the read-time fold), not a stored second copy. A check that runs after the admission it was meant to guard cannot prevent the break; it can only discover it post-hoc. The safe direction is the inverse and is what A/D-projection give for free: the immutable per-unit locate lifecycle is truth, `reserved` is its read-time projection, and there is nothing to reconcile because there is one representation.

The identical two-homes hazard is baked into d2: the `reserved` counter lives in **per-wallet** workflow state, while the locate unit's `ACTIVE→terminal` transition lives on the **per-unit** workflow (sec16.tex:48 — one workflow per unit). **Against FORMALIS — MATERIAL: states a false legal fact about the put-on-hold locate.**
FORMALIS grants that C is "correct for the one flavour where the law does block inventory (ESMA put-on-hold locates for illiquid shares)," and elsewhere that put-on-hold is "a genuine hold feeding the existing reserved/regulatory_hold term." A put-on-hold confirmation (Del. Reg. (EU) 827/2012 Art 6) is the confirming party's *contractual undertaking to hold* the requested quantity — a commitment with a deadline, an obligation, not a transfer of title between the lender's own conserved coordinates. Nothing legally moves even for put-on-hold. So C's `free→reserved` conserved move is legally FALSE even for that subclass; FORMALIS's carve-out concedes precisely the false booking the hard constraint forbids. The put-on-hold belongs in obligation/projection state (as MINSKY and I hold), never in the conserved plane. This is not fatal to FORMALIS's D-first ranking, but the sentence must be struck or the document will license a false move for the one case it names as legitimate.

**Against MINSKY — MATERIAL: the categorical "over-location is NOT illegal in the world" is false for the put-on-hold subclass.**
MINSKY frames over-location as demanding "only that the FIRM'S POLICY gate be total, race-free, and singly-owned." True for standard/ETB/same-day locates, where the same inventory lawfully backs many locates. But for a put-on-hold locate the confirming party has undertaken (827/2012 Art 6) to hold the requested quantity; issuing put-on-hold locates in excess of held/sourceable inventory *breaches that undertaking*. For that subclass the over-location bound is LAW, not house policy. MINSKY's own D needs the gate parameterised by locate-kind for this reason, yet the categorical "not illegal" framing obscures it. Fix: state that the gate's status (policy vs legal) is kind-dependent. MINSKY does correctly classify the put-on-hold itself as an obligation, so this is a framing/precision defect in an otherwise legally sound filing.

**Against FINOPS — MATERIAL: `reserved = Σ active-locate quantities` asserts, for every locate, a capacity reduction the law attaches only to put-on-hold.**
FINOPS adopts a single uniform projection with no locate-type field and calls it "phantom-free by construction." It is phantom-free as to *storage*, but its *semantics* are legally inaccurate: it deducts every standard/ETB locate from lendable capacity, whereas under SSR Art 12(1)(c) and Reg SHO 203(b)(1)(ii) a standard/ETB locate reduces nothing — the same inventory remains freely sellable and re-locatable. FINOPS's gate therefore encodes put-on-hold semantics for all locates and would lawfully-but-wrongly decline ETB business (SBL's market point, put in legal terms). This is conservative, not unsafe, so it is not a false *move*; but it is a false *characterisation* of the ETB/standard locate's legal effect. Remedy: carry a `locate_kind` field and parameterise the projection by kind — the same fix I flagged in my Round-1 note.

**Against WILSON — STYLISTIC: locate-taxonomy conflation.**
WILSON's legal section is otherwise sound (defers to counsel, correctly models put-on-hold as a projection subtraction, flags INV-OL as stricter than law). But it lumps "standard (easy-to-borrow / liquid-share, same-day) confirmation" into one bucket. Under 827/2012 Art 6 these are four distinct confirmation types with distinct conditions: standard locate, same-day locate, easy-to-borrow, and put-on-hold. The conflation does not break WILSON's argument, but the document WILSON is helping to write must not inherit it — the whole point of §16.14 is that a careful reader never has to ask, and a practitioner reading "standard = ETB = same-day" will ask. **Against SBL — MATERIAL: omits the put-on-hold confirmation as a genuine no-move reservation-like category.**
SBL's case against C rests on "does reservation match any real practice? Essentially none," and disposes of the one lookalike — the hard/exclusive locate — by asserting "the shares moved, so … that is §16.8 loan-initiation." That collapses two legally distinct things. A pre-borrow (shares move) is indeed §16.8. But the **put-on-hold confirmation** (827/2012 Art 6) is a third category in which *nothing moves* and no pre-borrow occurs — the confirming party contractually earmarks inventory. It is real, named market practice, not a null set. This does not rescue C (put-on-hold is still an obligation, not a conserved move), but SBL's "no practice matches reservation" overstates: a practice matches the *enforcement*, just not the *move*. ETB reliance is 203(b)(1)(ii) — "reasonable grounds to believe the security can be borrowed." Rule 203(b)(2) is the *exceptions* to the locate requirement (e.g. 203(b)(2)(iii), bona-fide market making) — a different provision entirely, and one that if cited into §16.14 would misstate the law. ISDA's substantive legal conclusion (a locate is a confirmation, not an encumbrance; false under current and proposed law) is correct; the citation is not. Correct to 203(b)(1)(ii).

Two errors: (1) ETB is 203(b)(1)(ii), not 203(b)(2) (which is the exceptions clause); (2) there is no *express* non-exclusivity provision — non-exclusivity is inherent in the "reasonable grounds to believe" standard, not a stated rule. NAZAROV's design conclusions (locate off the conserved plane; put-on-hold as unit-state + stricter gate, not a move; external-lender attestation as a reason units beat coordinates) are legally sound and, on the external-lender point, add something no other filer surfaced. CROSS-CUTTING MATERIAL OBJECTION — THE FOURTH INTERLEAVING NOBODY ANALYSED: CONFIRM-vs-SELL.
Every Round-1 race analysis (including my own) enumerated confirm/confirm, confirm/convert, confirm/expire. But capacity is also reduced by ops OUTSIDE the locate family: an outright sale lawfully drops `own` (FORMALIS's own box-I refutation depends on exactly this op). Consequence: any repair that serialises only {confirm, convert, expire} on a per-(lender,security) owner — MINSKY's locate-admission workflow, ISDA's "(entity,security) writer", FORMALIS's repair option (1) "per-pair locate book", REGULATORY's "one-writer discipline that protects loan-initiation" — leaves gate-read-vs-sale-commit OPEN: the sale commits through applyTx from a different workflow between the pair-owner's read of committed avail and its commit of the locate. At the confirm's commit position, Σ active locates > capacity: the admission-time invariant is violated; the gate certified a stale state. The ONLY mechanism on the table that closes it is evaluating the predicate at the transaction's position in the log's total order (sec03.tex:111) inside applyTx admission — the sec09.tex:202 idiom — because the one door owns the out-of-family writers too. This objection is MATERIAL against MINSKY (his d2 as designed), and demotes the per-key-owner remedy to insufficient-alone in FORMALIS, ISDA, and REGULATORY (each of whom also names the applyTx idiom; it must be stated as the load-bearing mechanism, not an alternative). Killing test:

```python
@given(inv=inventories(), q1=quantities(), q_sale=quantities())
def test_confirm_vs_sell(inv, q1, q_sale):
    # interleave: gate-read(confirm q1) ; commit(sell q_sale) ; commit(locate q1)
    s = interleave(confirm_read(q1), sell(q_sale), confirm_commit(q1), state=inv)
    assert sum_active_locates(s) <= capacity(s)   # fails under any pair-owner gate
```
