Dear correspondent,

The five questions are answered in one place: §8.4, "The market-data contract," within §8, "The State-Basis Discipline." Each answer below names its primary location first; supporting references follow. The FAQ entry "What does the Ledger require of market-data providers, and who tracks corporate actions?" condenses the same material into one page, and the reader-roadmap row "Market-data integrator" routes a first reading to §8.4 directly.

**Q1 — Minimum requirements on providers; how far the market-data layer must track lifecycle state.**
Primary: §8.4, the obligation set, items O1 and O3. Under O1, a raw observation carries exactly a value, an observation time, a signed source id, and a unit reference resolvable to a registered unit — nothing more is demanded of a provider. Under O3, a provider is not required to track corporate actions: a pre-adjusting source and a lagging source are both representable, because the basis stamp states which basis the value is in. The stamp itself is attached by the ledger, not the provider, at the ingest door (O2); feed signing and value corrections are O7 and O8 of the same list.

**Q2 — Tagging and reconciling incoming data with internal state; whether a datum carries CA-awareness.**
Primary: §8.4, obligations O2 and O4, with the `Convention` and `ingestAt` listing of the same subsection. The datum does not carry whether it reflects a corporate action; the ledger attaches the stamp at ingestion, computed from the pinned basis projection and the per-(unit, source) *source basis convention* on file — tracks the chain, lags by a declared offset, pre-adjusts, declares a fixed basis, or makes no claim (O4, `Convention`). The attestation envelope records the projection version and convention version consulted, so every stamp replays as a pure function of recorded data (O2). A datum whose basis cannot be determined is refused with payload retained — quarantined, never guessed, never defaulted from prevailing ledger state (O5; `IngestError` in the same listing). Consumption then requires pointwise basis agreement or a ledger-authored arrow, per C13 and the basis invariant of §8. The one-bit ancestor of the stamp is `mQuoteEx` in the appendix "Coordination Between Pricing, Contract State, and Market Data," which the appendix itself identifies as the degenerate per-(unit, source) case of the §8.4 coordinate.

**Q3 — Scope beyond dividends: implied vols, index compositions and weights, fixings, benchmarks.**
Primary: §8.4, the datum-class table, together with the "Primitive and derived" paragraph that precedes it. The table covers spot and quotes; implied-vol observations, whose coordinates' basis-sensitivity is declared data transcribed from the source — an absolute-strike coordinate carries `Scale` where the exchange publishes a contract adjustment — with no vol model and no mandated parametrisation; index levels as primitive observations of the index unit; compositions, weights, and divisor files as `Recompose` boundary notices; fixings and benchmarks, with cessation or succession expressed as `Subst` composed with `Shift`, an instance of the existing operators; and curves as derived data carrying the joint stamp of their inputs compositionally, per the rederivation principle and the composition law. Each row states the raw datum, what its stamp covers, the declared operators acting, and what fail-closed means for it.

**Q4 — Direction of information flow.**
Primary: §8.4, the "Direction of flow" paragraph. Exactly two arrows cross the boundary: the basis projection flows out — read-only, versioned, a view of the log — and raw observations flow in through the single ingest door, where the ledger attaches the stamp; the market-data layer never writes ledger state, and the ledger never accepts a basis it did not stamp or verify against its own committed chain. The projection interface (`BasisView`) is arithmetic-free — no operator kinds, parameters, or arrows are exposed — so no consumer possesses the information corporate-action logic would need, per the Stamping-authority principle that opens §8.4. The traffic is pull-based: no per-datum query of the ledger, no callback into the ingestion layer (same paragraph).

**Q5 — Real-time, batch, and multi-vendor alignment without duplicating CA logic.**
Primary: §8.4, "One contract, three modes," with the worked example "Two consumers across one boundary." All three modes face the same door; one ingestion run pins one projection version, and the stamp is a pure function of projection version, convention version, source, observation time, and raw datum — mode is not among the arguments, so batch and real-time agree by construction (property form P-MODE, same subsection). Vendor disagreement on value within one basis partition is data quality, outside the contract; disagreement on basis is two different stamps, resolved by the W3 partitions of §8's failure-semantics subsection, across which no aggregate is representable (fault F10 of the §8.4 boundary-fault catalogue). The worked example puts two consumers on opposite sides of a 2-for-1 boundary consuming one stored series: both mark €100,000, the phantom pairing is formable in neither scope, and neither consumer knows the split's terms. No corporate-action logic exists outside the ledger, and none can: adjustment arrows are declared once, in the log, and applied by the one evaluator — a stated consequence in §8.4. Door soundness is invariant P25, stated in §8.4 with its property catalogue; the executable forms are authored in catalogue shape with generators, as recorded there.
