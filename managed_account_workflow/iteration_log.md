## Round 0 — Step-2 review: logged tensions

### `finops-architect`
- **vs `formalis`** — formalis B2 derives a single baseline law B_k = V^ref_{t_k} - Perf_k = V^ref_{t_{k-1}} ('book returns to the capital base'). That is correct ONLY for the desk/Treasury 100%-PnL sweep. For a fee-bearing client managed account the gain stays in the book and only the fee fraction leaves, so the baseline does NOT return to the prior capital base - it ratchets to the new post-fee HWM. The baseline law BIFURCATES by whether you sweep full PnL or extract a fee, and §6 conflates the two cases; formalis treats §6's single mechanism as the whole story and derives one law. Must resolve which baseline the Reset step writes for each regime.
- **vs `correctness-architect`** — correctness-architect's headline invariant test_perf_telescopes_net_of_flows (Sigma crystallised = V_n - V_0 - Sigma flows) treats the flow-adjusted full Perf as the amount that crystallises. That HOLDS for the Treasury/desk sweep but is FALSE for a client managed account, where only the fee fraction crystallises and most of the gain remains in-book ratcheting the HWM. The disagreement is on WHAT crystallises: full flow-adjusted Perf (their model, correct for the desk case) vs a fee-only leg with the gain retained (the client case). Their flow-adjustment fix is necessary but their telescoping invariant must be scoped to the sweep regime only.
- **vs `jane-street-cto`** — jane-street's remedy for the reset baseline is to snapshot P_{t-1} (or V_{t-1}) into the reset event payload so Perf stays a pure fold. That is necessary for replay but not sufficient: the baseline NAV (entry_nav / last_reset_NAV / HWM) is not merely a cached price - it is a first-class economic state field that must ABSORB subscriptions, redemptions, and the fee draw, so deriving it 'by fold' requires the flow-netting and fee history to be inside the fold. Disagreement on framing: 'snapshot the price' (their view) vs 'a tagged economic state field with flow-adjustment and ratchet semantics' (mine).
- **vs `banking-auditor`** — banking-auditor characterises stored accrued mgmt/perf fee as 'a residual internal-reconciliation surface the no-internal-reconciliation claim does not cover' because it can diverge from a recomputation from ProductTerms + V_t. I hold accrued_fee is a monotone-carrier field mutated by one C11 handler and re-derivable by replay (a fold), so it is NOT an independent record that can break - it is a projection checkpoint, and there is no second record to reconcile against. The genuine point we converge on is the cut-off case (reporting date != reset date needs intra-period recomputation) and that the accrual formula must live in ProductTerms - which §6 fails to supply. We disagree only on whether stored accrued_fee reintroduces a reconciliation surface (it does not, given a deterministic ProductTerms formula).

### `minsky`
- **vs `correctness-architect`** — correctness-architect treats hwm/accrued_fee and a reset baseline as legitimate stored PositionState fields (3D: 'add the baseline field with a C11 writer'; flags stored accrued fee only as a reconciliation risk, not as a category error). I argue hwm, entry_nav, and accrued_fee are fold-derivable and, by A1's own first_touch_date ruling (l.189), are NOT state — caching them reintroduces the exact back-dated-correction desync that demoted first_touch_date. Tension: stored-field-with-single-writer vs derived monotone projection. This is the state/projection boundary and must be resolved (escalated).
- **vs `formalis`** — formalis's PO-Baseline law persists B_k := V^ref_{t_k} - Perf_k at PositionState[w_ref,u_MA] under one C11 handler — i.e. endorses a stored, mutable baseline field. I hold the baseline (like hwm) must be derived or snapshotted into the reset event payload, never a mutable PositionState scalar, for the same back-dated-correction reason. We agree the literal Reset step is incorrect (his B2); we differ on whether the corrected baseline is stored state or a projection.
- **vs `jane-street-cto`** — Strong agreement that the reset baseline must be derived-or-snapshotted, never a desyncable cached scalar (his finding 1). Remedy divergence: he frames the fix as snapshotting P_{t_{k-1}}/V_{t_{k-1}} into the reset event payload so Perf stays a pure fold while a handler still updates a baseline/hwm field; I go further and make hwm a max-projection with no authoritative writable field, so a silent reset is unrepresentable rather than merely reproducible. Snapshot-into-mutable-field vs full projection.
- **vs `banking-auditor`** — Agreement that u_MA must not enter V_t and that accrued fee stored-not-projected is a residual reconciliation surface. Minor tension on the u_MA fix: banking-auditor accepts 'P_t(u_MA)=0 or undefined'; I argue 0 is wrong because a per-wallet NAV gains +1*P(u_MA) with no cancellation (only the global sum cancels), so u_MA must be structurally outside V's domain (no price slot), not priced at 0. And I extend the stored-fee observation to hwm/entry_nav: banking-auditor stops at flagging accrued fee, I argue the whole valuation payload is a cached projection.

### `formalis`
- **vs `banking-auditor`** — Accrued fee as a stored PositionState scalar. Banking-auditor treats it as a manageable audit surface that 'should be re-derivable and tied out to V_t'. I hold it is a correctness defect: the performance fee is NOT re-derivable from the move stream (path-dependent stored monotone HWM plus external historical price path), so the prescribed tie-out is impossible by construction. Resolution: model the accrual as a conserved obligation unit u_fee so zero-sum becomes a theorem, OR explicitly retract Section 6.9's projection claim for fee state.
- **vs `isda-board-advisor`** — Isda-board accepts the quantitative-constraint guards as 'deterministic and auditable' CDM validation rules as stated. I contest this: determinism fails without P_t in the signature (F6), the guard is partial on missing price and V_t<=0 (F7), and it enforces an admission-time precondition rather than the portfolio invariant (F8, passive breaches escape). The CDM mapping is fine, but the determinism/totality claim must be re-quantified before it is auditable.
- **vs `minsky`** — Multi-mandate performance attribution. MINSKY (B5) leaves 'two rows, one wallet' vs 'attribution needs disjoint sub-wallets' as an open tension. As tie-breaker I resolve it: one wallet value V^ref yields one scalar Perf, and splitting it into per-mandate performance for two independent HWM/fee streams is not a projection of the move stream - it needs an attribution rule the primitives do not supply. Each co-resident mandate must own a disjoint reference sub-partition, or ProductTerms[u_MA] must declare a total deterministic allocation function reconstructible from the stream; until then per-mandate Perf is non-deterministic w.r.t. the stream.

### `banking-auditor`
- **vs `finops-architect`** — finops asserts 'double-entry by construction (P1)' and 'there is no internal sub-ledger to reconcile — the move stream is the evidence' as universal. I contest the scope: it holds for cash and positions but is FALSE for the accrued management/performance fee and HWM, which A1 stores as non-conserved scalars (FIELD_SPEC conserved:False) with no contra-account. Those are single-entry, are not projections of the stream, and ARE a genuine internal-reconciliation surface. Resolution: adopt the framework's own SBL priced-fee-unit pattern (l.3630/4201) so the accrual becomes conserved and double-entry — then finops's claim becomes true across the fee population too.
- **vs `jane-street-cto`** — jane-street frames the managed account as 'a derivation over primitives ... no bespoke record' and 'double-entry by construction, including u_MA, no carve-outs.' But accrued_mgmt_fee/accrued_perf_fee/hwm are precisely a bespoke STORED, non-conserved record (a desyncable cached scalar — the same failure mode jane-street flags for the reset baseline), not a derivation. The carve-out from conservation is real. Same resolution as with finops: priced fee-accrual unit restores 'no bespoke record'.
- **vs `correctness-architect`** — correctness-architect (with formalis) targets a legal, sign-correct, flow-adjusted SINGLE NET move as the fix (their A and B). I agree those fixes are necessary but contend they are not sufficient for my charge: even a perfect single net move is NOT journal-entry-equivalent — it nets PnL settlement, management fee and performance fee into one cash line, destroying the gross detail required for IFRS 15 revenue recognition, EMIR VALU, and audit substantiation. The reset must be a multi-move transaction (gross legs), netting only at the settlement boundary. This aligns me with regulatory-reporter's 'net-settlement hides gross detail' and is an additional constraint on, not a contradiction of, the correctness lenses' move-legality fix.

### `isda-board-advisor`
- **vs `jane-street-cto`** — jane-street-cto calls the collapse of TRS, periodic reset and managed-account into one contract 'the right refactor'; correctness-architect/finops/formalis accept them as one mechanism. I dissent at the CDM product layer (Finding T1): a CDM TotalReturnSwap is two-legged (return + financing) with a notional, whereas the managed-account Perf=V_tk−V_tk−1 is single-leg, no notional, no financing. Same cash primitive, different CDM products. One code path either drops the TRS financing leg from EMIR reports or fabricates a phantom notional on desk resets. Resolution: share the settlement primitive, separate the product representation.
- **vs `regulatory-reporter`** — regulatory-reporter's MH-1 assumes 'report source = the stored CDM BusinessEvent, which F keeps whole in the log.' That holds for ingested trades but NOT for the reset/TRS/margin settlement, which is synthesised by the contract and emitted as a bare Move with free-string metadata (l.910–920, l.946–956) — F has no upstream CDM event to preserve (Finding T3). The design currently cannot satisfy MH-1 for exactly the synthetic/TRS/margin population. Resolution: require the handlers to construct the CDM Reset+Transfer event.
- **vs `banking-auditor`** — banking-auditor asserts u_MA is a zero-value memo unit to be excluded from V_t AND from RWA/exposure. I agree the mandate handle is non-valued (M1 makes that a property of its CDM type), but the exclusion must NOT extend to the synthetic exposure the mandate wraps: under §6.4 the CSA margin and under Basel the counterparty exposure of the real u_TRS are valued, margined and reportable. If 'exclude u_MA' is read as 'exclude the relationship's economic exposure', margin (C1) and RWA are understated. The boundary between the non-valued handle and the valued synthetic exposure must be drawn explicitly.

### `correctness-architect`
- **vs `formalis`** — Multi-mandate performance attribution. formalis (B3) holds that splitting one wallet's scalar Perf into base+overlay performance is 'not a projection of the move stream' and requires disjoint sub-partitions. I hold that a total deterministic allocation function declared in ProductTerms[u_MA] IS a projection — provided each mandate acts on tagged disjoint positions. Where overlay mutates the same positions, attribution becomes a counterfactual that is not state-sufficient, and there formalis is right. Tension: is disjoint sub-partitioning the only fix, or does a declared allocation function suffice for the non-overlapping case? Needs the formalis/jane-street tie-break.
- **vs `nazarov-data-architect`** — Scope of the determinism boundary. nazarov bundles attestation (provenance, signature, quorum, staleness) into the price/benchmark boundary. I separate two distinct invariants with different owners and failure signals: determinism (the same datum reproduces on replay — closed by recording + content-addressing) vs veracity (the datum is the right one — needs attestation). My charge (replay/P3/P10) is closed by recording alone; nazarov's correctness-of-settlement needs the attestation layer on top. A factoring tension: log so the two boundaries are specified separately, not merged.
- **vs `jane-street-cto`** — Minimal recorded object for the reset baseline. jane-street (#1) proposes the reset event snapshot P_t(k-1)/V_t(k-1) into its payload so Perf stays a pure fold. I prefer storing the baseline as a single C11-tagged NAV scalar B_(k-1) in PositionState[w_ref,u_MA] (= prior post-settlement value): replay is still a fold and the recorded object is one decimal, not a whole price vector. The two diverge under a back-dated price correction (jane-street re-derives from corrected prices; my scalar goes stale and needs a compensating reset). Tension: snapshot-the-prices vs store-the-scalar as the minimal AND correction-safe recorded baseline.

### `nazarov-data-architect`
- **vs `formalis`** — Determinism ≠ correctness. formalis's PO-Determinism treats P_t as a trusted given argument ('pure function of the external price vector'); recording P_t as an event makes the wrong number reproducible, not right. Attestation (signature + multi-source + freshness) is upstream of determinism, not a substitute. Their fix (inject the price as a recorded input) is necessary but not sufficient — a recorded unattested price is a reproducible lie. Encompasses correctness-architect (H), finops 'flag any live read', jane-street-cto #3.
- **vs `jane-street-cto`** — Snapshot-as-consistency vs snapshot-as-attestation. The shared-handle fix (jane-street-cto #3 / minsky B7: thread one immutable P_t into both ℒ_v and ℒ_r, 'parse the snapshot once') kills divergence but makes both ledgers agree on a value neither has attested — consistency without provenance. The shared snapshot must additionally be content-addressed AND signature-verified, and the settlement move must hash-reference it. Necessary but stops short.
- **vs `banking-auditor`** — Placement of independent price verification. banking-auditor frames cross-source/IPV as a downstream accounting overlay (IFRS 13 hierarchy). I require cross-source validation as an upstream gate that defers crystallisation (the spec's own L1642) BEFORE the irreversible move. If IPV lives only in the accounting overlay, the client cash has already moved on a single unverified price. The placement difference is load-bearing.
- **vs `regulatory-reporter`** — Report substantiation source. regulatory-reporter (MH-1) says reports derive from the stored CDM BusinessEvent payload, but the valuation/MTM field (EMIR VALU, daily per MH-4) is computed from the price-snapshot oracle, which the forgetful map F keeps OUT of the CDM payload (L1999) and which is not hash-bound to the event. A VALU number cannot be substantiated from the CDM event alone — it needs the attested content-addressed price snapshot that §6 does not currently produce. The reporting boundary and the valuation boundary need the same snapshot object; neither lens has placed it.

### `regulatory-reporter`
- **vs `nazarov-data-architect`** — He scopes price-consistency as internal trust assumptions (TA-PRICE-CONSISTENCY) with an internal detection signal (snapshot-hash mismatch). I assert the authoritative detection for reporting is an EMIR reconciliation break at the trade repository, observable only after the counterparty reports - outside both ledgers. A hash-consistent but contractually-wrong price replays cleanly yet produces a reportable-but-wrong VALU; internal attestation cannot certify the regulatorily-correct valuation source, which must be the contractually-specified one.
- **vs `isda-board-advisor`** — He proposes replacing the hand-set reportable flag with DRR-derived executable eligibility logic. DRR runs over a CDM product representation, but by his own B1 there is no native CDM product type for an IMA - so DRR has nothing to consume for the u_MA wrapper. The reportability of u_MA issuance is necessarily a legal characterisation (IMA service vs synthetic derivative), upstream of DRR. DRR-derivation is right for the underlying/TRS populations and a category error for u_MA itself.
- **vs `banking-auditor`** — His escalation to confirm u_MA is a zero-value memo unit excluded from V_t/RWA is correct, but the 'excluded from everything' framing must be scoped: it must not bleed into 'the managed-account relationship is non-reportable.' Reporting obligations attach to the underlying/observed units, which are not memo units - zero-value is for valuation/RWA only; the indexed relationship retains full reporting obligations on its constituents.
- **vs `minsky`** — Their B1 (crystallise/Payment_k can emit an illegal q<0 / mis-signed move) is also a reportable-data-quality defect: the move direction populates the EMIR/CFTC payer-receiver and cashflow sign; a silent mis-sign triggers a reportable EROR and a potential CFTC Part 43 misreport. Tension: fixing the sign of the NET move is necessary for settlement but insufficient for reporting - the report must not be derived from the net move at all (MH-1) but from the gross CDM event, so the two artefacts diverge.
- **vs `finops-architect`** — We agree the §6 structural identity hides differing regimes, but he frames the root as differing control regimes the unification papers over (operational). I locate the root in a missing per-relationship reportability + counterparty-classification attribute (FC/NFC(+/-), SD/MSP, RC) that the move algebra does not encode and cannot infer from the mechanism. The control-regime difference is downstream of a classification that must live in WalletRegistry/ProductTerms, not be read off the mechanism.
